Summary:

A recent post by Alicexbt has sparked discussion around the process for adding new maintainers to a particular project. The post advocates for a more democratic approach where community members can share their opinions and self-merging pull requests are avoided. In response to this, Michael Folkson acknowledges some confusion around the process and pledges to look into the matter offline. Folkson advises against "ranting and raving or throwing toys out the pram" on the mailing list and wishes everyone happy holidays. The post includes links to various pull requests, IRC meetings, and issues related to the process for adding maintainers. These links provide additional context and support for Alicexbt's proposals. Bitcoin Core needs to improve the process of adding new maintainers to ensure unbiased merging of pull requests and encourage contributions from long-term contributors globally while avoiding politics in nominating new maintainers.
