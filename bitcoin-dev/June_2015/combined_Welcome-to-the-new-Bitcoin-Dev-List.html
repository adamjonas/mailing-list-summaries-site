Summary:

The Bitcoin development mailing list has recently undergone some changes and updates. The author submitted a request for gmane to monitor and archive a new list, bitcoin-dev, and suggests that others may not need to re-request the same thing. Gmane has an HTTP/HTML archive and a newsgroup proxy that is useful for reading the archive and can subscribe the list to mail-archive.com.However, the old bitcoin-development list has a few missing months from its archive on gmane, but the new list archive contains the same content as the old list. The author is seeing if they can import old archives from https://lists.linuxfoundation.org/pipermail/bitcoin-dev/ since it is complete. They may request that gmane delete or rename their old, incomplete archive in order to avoid confusion. There is also a bitcoin-list, which may be discontinued, and the author wonders whether they should try to preserve this historical correspondence before sourceforce disappears. In addition, a recent announcement on the Bitcoin development mailing list stated that the migration of the previous archive has been successfully completed and new posts will now be saved. Warren Togami Jr. sent an email on 6/22/2015 notifying readers of the completed move and that their first post to this list may be delayed by 5+ minutes due to greylisting. He also shared some TODOs including upgrading Mailman soon to better handle posts from DKIM enforced domains and making a few more config tweaks like auto-rejecting non-subscribed posts. However, he clarifies that it is too dangerous to auto-reject spam as those messages need to go into a blackhole to prevent bounce abuse.Overall, the context revolves around the completion of the move of the bitcoin-dev mailing list archives and the implementation of some changes to the Mailman system to better handle posts. There are also discussions about archiving and updating related platforms. However, concerns have been raised about updating bitcoin.org and bitcointalk.org which are viewed as important sources for development updates. Additionally, the Bip0001 page needs an update.
