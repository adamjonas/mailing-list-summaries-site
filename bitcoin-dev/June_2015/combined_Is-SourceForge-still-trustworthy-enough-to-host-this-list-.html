Summary:

The Bitcoin-development mailing list has been discussing the issue of changing email list providers. Some members have suggested finding a free solution, while others propose a pay-per-message or byte system to limit spam and encourage concise messages. However, there is no agreement on where to move the list, and some members do not see it as an urgent issue.The current provider, Sourceforge, has been found to be sufficient, although there have been concerns about delayed delivery, ads in message footers, and broken digital signatures due to list server modifications. There have also been warnings against downloading software from SourceForge, which prompted the deletion of all downloads (including older ones) from the site. The mailing list is public, so there are no sensitive data on it, but some members suggest implementing live mirrors and read-only archives to mitigate business risks. Overall, the consensus seems to be between "pay with commits or pay with bitcoin" for a sustainable solution.In June 2015, members of the Bitcoin-Development mailing list discussed switching to a new email provider due to issues with the current one. The current provider, Sourceforge, delays delivery sometimes for up to several days, adds ads to the footer of messages, does not allow HTML messages, and often breaks digital signatures due to slight modifications made by the list server. However, some members disagreed with switching, stating that the mailing list is public and therefore not sensitive data. They also argued that Sourceforge is sufficient and has a nice web UI. There was no agreement on where to move the list, and some members did not see it as an urgent issue. One member suggested finding a free solution or creating a pay-per-message model to cover the costs of a new provider. This would limit spam and encourage contributors to be more concise with their messages. Another member proposed implementing a HashCash system as a filter plugin that would ensure the required bitcoin fee has been paid, or better yet included in the message in some standard form. The idea of paying per message was dismissed as far-fetched and likely to lead to a mailing list only used for advertisement and promotion, rather than technical discussion and release announcements.The context also mentions that all Bitcoin Core downloads have recently been deleted from Sourceforge due to concerns over downloading software from the site. For those who did not like Sourceforge's web archive UI, other options were available via gmane.
