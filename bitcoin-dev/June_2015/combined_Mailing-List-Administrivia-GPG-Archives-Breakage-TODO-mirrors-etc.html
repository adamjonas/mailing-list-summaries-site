Summary:

The bitcoin-dev mailing list has been experiencing issues with its archives, specifically with the "mbox" format. The archives are missing necessary headers and attachments, resulting in valuable content being lost. There are also instances of unescaped '^From' in the "mbox". Members of the list suggest solutions, including disabling the "obscure_addresses" option and demanding pristine historical sources for mirrors. Turning off message footers and subject prefixes is also suggested.To address these issues, the full raw archives should be provided in regularly updated [gzip/7z] mbox format. Some users feel that spam cannot be prevented by this method, and that any attempts to do so are futile. Overall, there is agreement that current mbox archives are broken and useless, and a need for full raw archives in regularly updated gzip or 7z mbox format.Warren Togami Jr. disabled the obscure_addresses option for Mailman as it was not effective in mitigating spam. Despite this, mailman continues to break clearsign GPG signatures for messages with e-mail addresses, which has been reported on web and mbox archives.A user requested that full raw archives be provided in regularly updated [gzip/7z] mbox format to fix these issues. Regarding message footers and subject prefixes, they are considered corruptive and space-wasting clutter and should be turned off.Additionally, gmane was able to change the subscription of the previous list to the current list, but there is a gap in their archive at the beginning and last week of the archive. They have requested a link to a single mbox file archive that can be used to restore the list archive in its entirety. Finally, an attachment named lost.c will not appear in the mbox archives, resulting in its loss to both mirrors and users who use the mbox archives as their canonical source for local seeding or MUA access. These issues have caused wasted time attempting to post-process the archives into usable form.
