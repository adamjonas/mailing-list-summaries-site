Summary:

Bitcoin developers are considering moving their mailing list away from SourceForge due to concerns about its hosting and stability. The Linux Foundation has been proposed as a suitable alternative host, as it is seen as neutral and competent in maintaining open-source infrastructure. The proposal would involve migrating the current list archive to the new host's archives for user convenience.Jeff Garzik suggests moving away from SourceForge mailing lists, using LF as a mailing infrastructure provider, and researching secure mailing list models for bitcoin-security. There is also discussion about DKIM signatures not being handled properly by Mailman 1.0, and uncertainty regarding whether switching to a different mailman provider will fix this issue.The proposed action plan includes discussing the matter openly within the community, importing the entire list archive into the new host's archives for user convenience, and deleting the entire Bitcoin SourceForge project. Warren Togami Jr. expresses support for the proposal of having bitcoin-dev hosted by the Linux Foundation (LF) due to their neutrality and competence in open source development.In June 2015, Warren Togami Jr suggested creating a new mailing list for Bitcoin development and moving away from SourceForge mailing lists. The group discussed choosing a name for the new list, agreeing on a cut-off date for the old list, and identifying moderators. The post also referenced Gittorrent, a decentralized alternative to GitHub.There have also been discussions regarding secure mailing list models for bitcoin-security. The author of the context emphasizes the importance of using an open-source platform like Mailman, instead of GoogleGroups, that can be easily moved around. The author advises the need for exports of their message database. The author discourages the use of forums due to their lack of bidirectional real-time message copying between list and forum.Overall, there is a consensus among the Bitcoin community to move forward with creating a new list and subscribing it to the old list. Once greater than 50% of the members of the old list are members of the new list, an announcement would be sent out that the old list will be turned into read-only mode in 3 weeks and to use the new list only after that time.
