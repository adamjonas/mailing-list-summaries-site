Summary:

The author of the article discusses the potential impact of a strong fee market on Bitcoin adoption and decentralization. They raise concerns about how Bitcoin can compete with services like PayPal if fees are not competitive, which may discourage users from adopting it. While layer 2 solutions like Lightning Network could help with scalability, they still require fees when settling on the actual blockchain, which could contradict Bitcoin's main selling point of instant payment. The author also questions the incentives to keep running a node if people start relying on layer 2 systems, and suggests that a strong fee market might harm the ecosystem more than it helps. The author proposes letting miners decide through proposals like BIP100.
