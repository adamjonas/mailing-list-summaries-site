The Bitcoin-dev mailing list has been discussing the development of Merkelized Abstract Syntax Trees (MAST) and Taproot, which aim to improve efficiency and privacy in Bitcoin transactions. Taproot proposes paying directly to a public key instead of a public key hash, raising concerns about quantum resistance against ECC attacks. However, hashing may not provide any meaningful quantum resistance, and direct SPK use allows for efficient zero-knowledge proofs over the UTXO set. The use of pay-to-contract-hash construction can make fancier contract use cases indistinguishable from common payments while still satisfying the contract with nothing other than a signature. Providing a privacy wrapper by default in the implementation of MAST is also emphasized.The email discusses the implementation of cross-input signature aggregation in P2P transactions, with an additional field to hold the aggregated signature. The author proposes a Taproot construction method for top-level "threshold-signature OR arbitrary-conditions" that can be made indistinguishable from a normal one-party signature, with no overhead at all and without requiring extra rounds of interaction between contract participants or durable storage of other data. Recursive taproot constructions that can nest and support interior nested keys with CSV/CLTV predicate or other simple predicates are also suggested.Mark Friedenbach and others have suggested that interesting scripts have a logical top level branch which allows satisfaction of the contract with nothing other than a signature by all parties. The special case of a top level "threshold-signature OR arbitrary-conditions" can be made indistinguishable from a normal one-party signature, with no overhead at all, with a special delegating CHECKSIG which is called Taproot.Gregory Maxwell has proposed a new construction called Taproot that makes smart contracts look like the simplest possible payments, allowing for the largest possible anonymity set for fixed party smart contracts. It works the same for any number of participants, and with setup interaction any threshold of participants, so long as an inability to tell which members signed off is not a problem.The concept of Merkelized scriptPubKeys for efficiency and privacy in smart contracts is driven by two main areas. A suggestion has been made to make fancier contract use cases as indistinguishable as possible from the most common and boring payments. The important bits of this "generalized funding transaction" pattern are that the contract that enforces correct behavior spends an unsigned and unbroadcasted funding transaction output (which requires N-of-N) and the enforcement contract is signed first by all parties before the funding transaction is signed by anybody.
