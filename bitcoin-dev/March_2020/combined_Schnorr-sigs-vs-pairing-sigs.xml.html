Summary:

Schnorr signatures, which rely on the masking provided by a random nonce, are vulnerable to bias introduced through simple methods like hash + modulo. Even 2 bits of bias can lead to serious attacks as per a presentation given at ECC2017. Therefore, it may be advantageous to consider pairing based signatures which could potentially offer more flexibility and better security implementations despite being slower than Schnorr signatures.There is an ongoing debate on the Bitcoin-dev mailing list regarding the use of pairing based signatures versus non-deterministic signatures like Schnorr over BLS. The randomness in signature schemes enables a type of signature encryption called "adaptor signatures" that is crucial for layer 2 protocols, according to one argument favoring the use of randomness. The original poster also shared their paper titled "One-Time Verifiably Encrypted Signatures A.K.A Adaptor Signatures" on the topic. However, Erik Aronesty argues that even though Schnorr signatures are deterministic, they heavily rely on the masking provided by a random nonce, and there are many ways to introduce bias. He cites a study that shows how 2 bits of bias can result in serious attacks. Aronesty suggests pairing-based signatures, while slower, may be more flexible and better suited for secure implementations.
