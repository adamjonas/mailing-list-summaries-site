Summary:

Bitcoin transactions are limited by the block weight limit to ensure miners get paid, but this creates a collective action problem where individuals pay the minimum required while others pay for security. Off-chain mechanisms can increase capacity while maintaining fees, but reducing demand for block-space could cause miner revenue to drop. The author expresses concern about miners controlling inflation rates and decision-making in new rulesets. Bitcoin's mechanism creates competition for limited block space so that each user pays the market rate, but some may pay less than needed to keep the network secure. Off-chain mechanisms do not remove on-chain fees but only amortize them to multiple transactions. Lightning nodes are not a permanent solution, and a block weight limit is necessary for miners to get paid. The fixed inflation rate schedule avoids beneficiaries of higher inflation rates deciding the rate.
