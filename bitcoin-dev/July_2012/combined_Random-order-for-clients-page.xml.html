The email conversations and discussions are focused on the best way to present information about Bitcoin clients on bitcoin.org. There is a debate about whether to use a feature matrix or prose to describe the clients, with some arguing that feature matrices can be confusing for new users. The potential biases of feature matrices were also discussed, with a suggestion made to have separate matrices for features and security/privacy. The importance of track record for end-users was emphasized, and it was suggested that a well-designed page listing major and vetted clients should be created.The ordering of clients is also debated, with some suggesting randomization and others proposing more sensible choices based on "goodness" determined by someone trusted to understand what is good for users and who has familiarity with the programs. Randomized order was not recommended, but if chosen, it should be done client-side using JavaScript. There were concerns about disputes over the order in which clients were shown, leading to Amir Taaki making a change to randomly order them. However, Gregory Maxwell disagreed and said that the other clients are becoming stable and mature, leading to diversification in the ecosystem.
