Summary:

MimbleWimble is a blockchain protocol that enables privacy and scalability. However, incorporating offchain protocols like Lightning network may not be possible while retaining its "magical shrinking blockchain" property. The lack of relative locktime is the main hindrance to practical channel constructions with indefinite lifetime, making validating transactions difficult and forcing new nodes to validate all transactions from creation rather than starting from the current UTXO set.Grin and Beam have implemented absolute lock heights since launch, with Grin proposing relative lock heights and designing payment channels based on them. Beam recently included relative lock heights in its HardFork and has a payment channel design as well.In a recent Bitcoin-dev mailing list post, ZmnSCPxj discusses the possibility of off-chain protocols on MimbleWimble and how it affects the Lightning Network. While the "magical shrinking blockchain" property of MimbleWimble eliminates the need to validate already-spent outputs, it creates a problem for validating relative locktimes necessary for off-chain updates and indefinite-lifetime channel constructions. ZmnSCPxj argues that the lack of relative lock-time, not the lack of SCRIPT, is what prevents Lightning-over-MimbleWimble.All practical off-chain cryptocurrency systems require some kind of timeout which could be implemented as either absolute or relative lock time, but an absolute locktime would limit the lifetime of the channel. MimbleWimble supports 2-of-2 with Schnorr and other homomorphic signatures, which can be used to implement HTLC-like constructs and impose relative locktimes using simple nSequence. MimbleWimble also supports the two-transactions construct for HTLCs hosted inside indefinite-lifetime Spilman channels and Decker-Russell-Osuntokun. However, while sequence numbers enforce relative timelocks and are described as an independent constraint in BIP68, OP_CHECKSEQUENCEVERIFY is required to enforce them. An alternative pre-signed nlocktime transaction double spend technique was proposed, but it requires the ability to aggregate signatures across transactions.Despite the loss of SCRIPT, ZmnSCPxj believes that a MimbleWimble blockchain can still use an off-chain updateable cryptocurrency system. In a conversation discussing the possibility of off-chain protocols on MimbleWimble, ZmnSCPxj expresses that he believes it is not possible while still retaining its "magical shrinking blockchain" property. However, he acknowledges that relative lock heights can be incorporated into MimbleWimble and provides links to Grin and Beam, which have included them since launch.ZmnSCPxj notes that kernels with relative locktimes are not pruneable and will contribute to blockchain size, but still believes it is worth accepting this cost in exchange for being able to layer on top of a MimbleWimble blockchain. In a discussion about the "magic shrinking blockchain" property in implementing relative locktimes, it was noted that this property did not apply to kernels. To validate the current UTXO set, all kernels must be validated, which is the same check applied to individual transactions. The design of Beam was identified as a straightforward port of Poon-Dryja, and the Grin "Elder channel" design similar in spirit to eltoo. Lack of scripts is not a significant handicap for MW as multi-sig, atomic swaps, and payment channels are all possible.
