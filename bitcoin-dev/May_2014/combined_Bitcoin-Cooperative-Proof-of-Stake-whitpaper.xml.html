Summary:

Stephen Reed from Austin, Texas has completed a whitepaper proposing a proof-of-stake version of Bitcoin. The proposal involves using a single nomadic verifiable mint agent and distributed replication of a blockchain by compensated full nodes to achieve 6-hop, sub-second transaction acknowledgement times. This version would pay dividends to holders instead of miners, making subsidized transaction fees lower. However, the code has not yet been written, and comments are appreciated on the project thread, which will serve as a development diary. A hard fork of the Bitcoin blockchain is planned in early 2016 after a year of public system testing and wide approval. The author expresses concern about the complexity of the proposed system and suggests incentivizing full nodes instead. Referring to the subsidy for miners as "wasting it on miners" is unlikely to gain much support. While the paper is not bad, Bitcoin's indestructible kernel means that it is unlikely to change drastically, and it may experience a slow death over the next 5-10 years.
