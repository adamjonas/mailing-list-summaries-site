Summary:

In a blog post titled "Decor," author Sergio Lerner proposes a method of decorating Bitcoin transaction messages to add personalization and privacy. The format for these decorated transaction messages includes a unique identifier followed by the desired message and a checksum for verification purposes. Lerner argues that this method of decorating Bitcoin transaction messages adds a level of personalization and privacy to the Bitcoin network, and encourages others to adopt this method. Lerner also addresses the Bonneau's Kickbacks problem, which creates a destabilizing incentive in the Bitcoin network. He proposes a conflict resolution strategy called DECOR (DEterministic COnflict Resolution) that benefits all members of the network and prevents kickbacks.
