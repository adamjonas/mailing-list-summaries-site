Summary:

The Bitcoin community has been discussing the idea of creating a standard for paper bitcoin currency. The proposed system would be based on reloadable, NFC-based notes that anyone can verify instantly using their phone. Merchants could convert these notes into fully online bitcoins, which would eliminate the costs of maintaining paper money turnover. However, protecting the notes from unauthorized redemption is a concern, and solutions such as Trusted Platform Modules (TPMs) and multisignature P2SH addresses have been suggested.Discussions have also covered technical security issues related to protecting notes from fraud and improving the atmosphere of the Bitcoin mailing list. There is a call for neutral individuals to become list moderators to help keep the debate civilized. Additionally, there have been discussions on improving the design of the proposed paper currency system, including making it more user-friendly and establishing a proper tradeoff between convenience and security.Other topics discussed include the creation of a Bitcoin social contract outlining features that will never be added or taken away from Bitcoin, concerns about fungibility and privacy, and the potential for fraud in physical bitcoin notes. One proposed solution is to embed electronics that can run an SPV client into printed paper, which could eliminate the need to trust anyone for testing.The author of the proposal believes that a single internationally accepted, borderless paper currency could disrupt the multi-billion dollar market of exchange fees for travelers. However, technical security issues would need to be addressed, and marketing details such as note size, shape, color, and naming conventions would need to be resolved. Ultimately, all efforts must be made to ensure the safety and protection of the notes, with encryption methods and other protective factors being considered.
