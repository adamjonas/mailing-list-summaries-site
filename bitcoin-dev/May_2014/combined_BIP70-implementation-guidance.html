Summary:

The article and email thread discuss important considerations related to the implementation of BIP70, a payment protocol used in Bitcoin transactions. One concern is error handling during signature checking, which should be treated as unsigned if PKI checking fails. Showing the business name instead of the domain name is better for identification and security purposes, as domain names are more phishable than EV names. However, there is no code to check whether a certificate was subject to extended validation before displaying its contents. The article suggests showing the organization data instead of the domain name if available for EV certs.In the UK, unincorporated businesses or sole traders are unable to obtain EV certificates due to the absence of a trade register. Nonetheless, they can easily trade under their name, email address, domain name, or telephone number if an SMS verifying CA is set up. On the other hand, companies that are unable to obtain EV certificates may indirectly cause confusion amongst users and make them uncertain about payment expectations, potentially making it easier for confusion attacks to occur.The importance of handling different types of certificates correctly, such as S/MIME certs, is also discussed. Testing is highlighted as essential, with a test site provided for payment requests on the main network. Finally, memo contents should contain useful information about what is being purchased or the merchant's name, and expiry times should not be too aggressive since some users may need to coordinate payments from multi-party accounts.
