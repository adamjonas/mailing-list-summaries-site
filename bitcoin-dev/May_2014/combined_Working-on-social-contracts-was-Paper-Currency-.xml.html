Summary:

The context includes various discussions about social contracts and promises regarding the development of Bitcoin. Justus Ranvier suggested creating a Bitcoin social contract that would promise what features would never be added or taken away from Bitcoin, but Gavin Andresen expressed confusion over why he and Mike Hearn would have the authority to write such a contract for future-Bitcoin. Mike Hearn disagreed with the concept of dangerous ideas that cannot be discussed and instead suggested building robust social and technical infrastructure to prevent failures and attacks. Gregory Maxwell also talked about the limitations of social contracts in cases where individuals are coerced or threatened with violence. In addition, Gavin Andresen made a promise to never endorse or work on changes to the Bitcoin system that would allow for confiscation, blacklisting, or devaluation of anyone's Bitcoin. He encouraged others to write Request for Comments (RFC) themselves as he had higher priorities on his to-do list. Andresen believed that the only social contract for Bitcoin was the decentralized one already in place. The context also includes a message from Justus Ranvier urging for respectful communication without personal attacks to uncover actual attacks and another message from Mike Hearn addressing an individual causing problems on a development list.
