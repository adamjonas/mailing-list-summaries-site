Summary:

The discussions revolve around the security concerns related to non-custodiality of funds, the design of a new off-chain coin-swap protocol, and 'statecoin' - a UTXO that can be transferred off-chain and is strictly non-custodial if it can be trusted. A proposed protocol called 'statecoin' is discussed in a conversation between Tom and ZmnSCPxj regarding non-custodiality. The aim of the concept is trust minimization, and if a service is non-custodial, it is much easier for it to operate without requiring its users to be KYCed. However, there are objections against custodiality.A team is designing an off-chain coin-swap protocol that will work with the statechain implementation they are developing, enabling users to transact peer-to-peer off-chain while remaining in full custody of their assets. To ensure privacy, the conductor would use a blind signature scheme similar to the zerolink protocol. One challenge with this protocol lies in assigning blame in case of a failed multi-party coinswap, as it's not possible to determine whether the sender or receiver of a coin is malicious without revealing the encrypted value sent between them.The security of statechains is discussed compared to other trust-minimised protocols such as TumbleBit and Wasabi. While statechains can be vulnerable to hardware corruption, it can be mitigated with secured infrastructure and the use of hardware security modules/trusted execution environments that enable secure deletion. The aim of statechains is to replicate the speed of a completely centralised mixer while making theft more difficult, providing users with proof of ownership/theft and privacy guarantees.An email discusses a high-level description of how blinding can operate for swapping ownership of coins privately. Four individuals want to swap ownership, and they signal their participation by signing the swap token with the proof key of their input coin. They then generate new statecoin addresses, which they blind and sign with the proof key of their input coin, sending them to the conductor who authenticates each signature and signs each payload with a blinded signature scheme. The conductor then randomly assigns each address to one of utxo1, utxo2, utxo3, and utxo4 and requests each participant initiate the transfer to the given address. The participants finalize each transfer, and atomicity is guaranteed by the SCE.ZmnSCPxj argues that trust minimization is the entire point of non-custodiality. Finally, there is an objection against custodiality where someone else can prevent a user from spending the coin, highlighting the importance of trust minimization. Overall, the conversations discuss various aspects of non-custodiality, trust minimization, and privacy in off-chain coin-swap protocols, highlighting potential challenges and solutions.
