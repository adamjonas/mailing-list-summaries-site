Summary:

Gavin Andresen, a developer of Bitcoin, expressed his concerns about offering bounties for fixing bugs in the system. He believes that it might not motivate long-term contributors and instead encourage short-term fixes that may not be sustainable. However, some developers disagree and think that offering bounties could incentivize people to fix bugs. Rick Wesson suggested putting a bounty on the person who leads the effort to improve the software, rather than on individual bug fixes. Gavin is currently working full-time on Bitcoin after receiving payment for his work. Additionally, it was suggested that adding more tests to the existing boost testing framework would be beneficial for improving the quality of the code.
