Summary:

The context discusses various proposals for improving the efficiency and decentralization of Bitcoin mining. One such proposal is pooled-solo mining mode, where a miner makes two connections to their pool and local bitcoind. They work on the subset of transactions common to both and share with the pool only those that do not meet difficulty. To reduce bandwidth, they suggest passing only the block header for normal shares and having the pool randomly audit a subset of transactions. The deliberate orphaning of slow-to-propagate blocks is also discussed by passing headers separately via UDP.In addition to these proposals, there are discussions on improving transaction prioritization. Jeff Garzik suggests telling peers what the fee/kb is when INV advertisements are sent, and one proposal involves accepting tx's into a node's mempool indiscriminately but delaying broadcasting INV messages until high-profit child tx's are found. Garzik also recommends prioritizing fast peers when sending INV messages and attending to incoming messages.There are also conversations between Peter Todd and Luke-Jr about the use of shares in pools and the importance of auditing and troubleshooting in mining operations. Todd proposes P2Pool-like solutions and optimizing the process of pooling and solo mining by handing over only transaction hashes where possible.Finally, Garzik recommends that pools target at least 5-10 seconds per share, no matter the strength of the miner. All of these proposals aim to increase efficiency and decentralization while reducing the impact of hacking or network disruptions on the entire Bitcoin network.
