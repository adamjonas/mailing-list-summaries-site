Summary:

In the world of blockchain, miners are crucial in ensuring the security and validity of transactions. Recently, a proposal has been made to require miners to specify minimum fees for the following block to be considered valid. This proposal aims to bring the equilibrium closer to a socially optimal state and benefit network security in the long term. However, there are concerns about potential drawbacks such as increased reorgs/orphan rates and chain instability until enough fees accumulate in the mempool to satisfy min_fees. It remains unclear whether this proposal would change much if humanity is willing to spend X BTC/day on mining fees, regardless of whether it's spread out through fewer or more blocks. The proposal warrants deeper analysis and discussion.Additionally, the bitcoin community has not seen a situation where the coinbase drops below fees on any chain. As bitcoin's capabilities grow, demand for it will as well. There are efforts to increase the amount of transactions that can fit into a block. The combination of limited block space and a reduced amount of bitcoins entering the market is the right combination for the system to self-sustain.Some worry that this proposal would give excessive power to the miners as they would be setting both the price and quality of their product. Despite these concerns, miners have no incentive to set super high numbers for the minimum fees, as this would result in delayed or nonexistent payment. Overall, the proposal aims to address the issue of low transaction fees potentially resulting in reduced network security and bring the equilibrium closer to a socially optimal state. However, it requires further analysis and discussion before implementation.
