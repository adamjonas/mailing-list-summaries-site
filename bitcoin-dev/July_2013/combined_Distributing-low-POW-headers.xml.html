Summary:

The email exchange revolves around a proposal for Bitcoin network consensus and the use of 'magic constants'. One of the proposals is to distribute headers with 1/64th of the standard Proof of Work (POW) to incentivize miners to take headers into account and prevent forks from becoming the main chain. The proposal also suggests changing the behavior of not relaying blocks to peers if they conflict with blocks in the best-known chain. Another suggestion is to distribute low proof-of-work (POW) block headers to break ties in the Bitcoin network, which would not require miners to have better network connections but could impact economics.The proposed changes include broadcasting low POW headers by the network, miners using header information to decide on the longest chain, and determining the longest chain. If 75% of the miners follow this rule, then the top fork will eventually catch up and win, so it is in their interests to follow the rule too. The proposal means that if the network is split 80/20, then miners should stick with the 80% fork, even if the 20% fork wins the race for the next block. Winning a few rounds is easier than winning many rounds worth of mini-confirms. In the email exchange, Peter Todd requested equations and data justifying the 'magic constants' in the proposal. The first "magic number" discussed was 1/64 of the standard Proof of Work (POW) for distributing headers. The proposal also discusses changes to the current behavior of not relaying blocks to peers if they conflict with blocks in the best-known chain. Headers would still be broadcast, but blocks would not be forwarded. A warning could be displayed on the client if a fork is getting more than 15% of the hashing power. Another suggestion for measuring orphan blocks revolves around setting some threshold relative to the height of the best block. Beyond this limit, orphan blocks or headers would not be propagated further. This proposal is a system to agree on the main chain in the face of consensus loss forks where a significant number of miners are following an alternative chain. The email also mentions that Pieter Wuille is working on letting Bitcoin propagate and make use of pure block headers, which would be a step towards Simplified Payment Verification (SPV) and partial UTXO mode.
