Summary:

The conversation surrounding Bitcoin mining involves several topics, including merge-mining, the security of merge-mined coins, and implementing Zerocoin on the Bitcoin blockchain. One concern raised is the potential for attacks from miners who maliciously or negligently mine invalid blocks. While validating clients reject invalid blocks, if an attacker has hash power for the parent chain, they can mine invalid blocks for the merge-mined chain at no cost, posing a security threat.Proof-of-sacrifice is seen as a stronger defense against 51% attacks than proof-of-work mining, but this defense still depends on the existence of a proof-of-work chain. Sacrificing Zerocoins, rather than using proof-of-stake, is suggested as a consensus algorithm. Additionally, there is discussion about the creation of an alt-coin with no-reward/PoSacrifice merged mining as well as (rewarded) Prime POW and no subsidy halving.Jorge Timón suggests that pegging zerocoins to bitcoins is not necessary, but it provides stability to a new alt-coin. He believes that merger mining dilutes the compute available for a given coin, and the sacrifice method is a simple and stable cost starting point for an altcoin. The exchange rate of zerocoin to bitcoin may depend on the extra functionality or value from the characteristics of the other coin.There are also concerns over scalability, validation, and cross-chain trading protocols. Alt-currencies face low market liquidity, and there is a suggestion to use p2p transfer for trading. The group discusses merged mining and its costs, and the importance of having a cost to encourage miners to pay attention to what they mine. In another email exchange, Jorge Timón discusses various topics related to Bitcoin mining, including the issue of sacrificing something outside of Bitcoin's chain and new BTC appearing. He suggests merged mining as a solution, but notes that value may vary for different jurisdictions and miners. Proof-of-sacrifice can be done in a way that is opaque to the master blockchain, and SCIP-based merged mining is seen as beneficial but currently uncertain.Peter Todd suggests a system that requires no changes to the Bitcoin codebase, thus avoiding any extra costs for pegging Bitcoin and Zerocoin. He also discusses the risks associated with starting an alt-chain based on proof of work that pool operators may attack. The conversation highlights the ongoing efforts to improve the security and scalability of cryptocurrencies through innovative new ideas and solutions.
