Summary:

The article discusses various ideas to prevent a theoretical attack on Bitcoin. One of these ideas is the use of Tor SOCKS proxy and hard-coded long term stable hidden peers run by known community members. Discussions on the Bitcoin-development mailing list revolve around the need for bigger configurability in Tor itself, with some participants feeling that what they need is more significant configurability in Tor itself.A new Tor.framework has been released for Cocoa developers, which is similar to BitcoinKit. Jeff Garzik suggested that it would be interesting to explore linking directly with a Tor library, which is far superior to requiring a properly configured external process. However, some participants in the discussion felt that what they need is more significant configurability in Tor itself. In terms of SPV security, Peter Todd discusses in an email thread from 2013 a potential improvement to Simplified Payment Verification (SPV) security, which is unrelated to Tor. Hive had plans to make their wallet proxy through Tor by default, but this was not possible due to bitcoinj lacking SOCKS support. This raised a discussion on whether it is advisable practice for SPV wallets to use Tor in the first place.Mike Hearn suggested in a conversation dated July 30, 2013, that it would be interesting to talk directly to the Tor protocol and have an application explicitly choose its own diverse set of exit nodes. However, this is likely to be complicated as Tor does not provide any library or API. The idea was discussed on IRC, and some believed that linking directly with a Tor library would be superior for several applications compared to requiring a properly configured external process. Without such a Tor library, one must be written.To support Tor well in bitcoinj, hidden peers would need to be added along with an anti-sybil heuristic. In regions where Bitcoin is illegal, packet filtering may not be far off, making it necessary to be proactive and prepared. Gregory Maxwell's comments on hidden service support for bitcoind were suggested as being interesting to get.Peter Todd advises against trusting zero-conf transactions as they are not secure.
