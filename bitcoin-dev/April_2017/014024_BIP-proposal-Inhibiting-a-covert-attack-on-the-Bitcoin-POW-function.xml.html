Summary:

A proposal has been made to inhibit covert exploitation of a known vulnerability in Bitcoin's Proof of Work function. This attack is called ASICBOOST and allows an attacking miner to save up to 30% of their energy costs. There are two ways of exploiting the underlying vulnerability in the Bitcoin mining process, and one of them is highly detectable. However, the other method can be easily used covertly and could result in payoff of as much as $100 million USD per year. In response, a BIP proposal has been offered to prevent any covert forms of this attack.The proposed rule requires the coinbase transaction of each block to either contain a BIP-141 segwit commitment or a correct WTXID commitment with ID 0xaa21a9ef, beginning block X and until block Y. The proposal does not include blocking the non-covert form because it may become generally available without restriction in the future, does not generally interfere with improvements in the protocol, and can be detected and blocked if it becomes an issue in the future.
