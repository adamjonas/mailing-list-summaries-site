Jimmy Song responded, stating that if some newer ASIC optimization comes up, having an ASICBoosted hash rate would be better in terms of securing the Bitcoin network against newer optimizations. Song explained that if someone figures out an ASIC optimization that's completely unrelated and gives a speed boost over non-ASICBoosted implementation, banning ASICBoost would allow someone with this optimization to get 51% of the network by adding N machines with their new optimization.
