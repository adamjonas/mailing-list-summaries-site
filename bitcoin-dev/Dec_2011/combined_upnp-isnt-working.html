Summary:

In 2011, Amir Taaki reported an issue with the functionality of upnp on Bitcoin's daemon. Sipa and gmaxwell had speculated that the protocol may no longer be working, but there was no way to test it. To troubleshoot this, Taaki created an alternate chain and ran the daemon on his VPS. However, he noticed that the connections were sometimes accepted while other times they were not due to patchy behavior. During a conversation on IRC, Matt suggested that the issue may not be with the function itself but with the failure to re-announce to the router that the port needs to remain open. Despite the uncertainty surrounding the situation, Taaki wanted to share his experience with others.It is important to note that UPnP (Universal Plug and Play) is a set of networking protocols that enable devices to discover each other's presence on a network and establish functional network services. In the context of Bitcoin's daemon, UPnP is used to automatically configure the router to allow incoming connections to the Bitcoin network.
