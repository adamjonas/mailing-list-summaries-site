Summary:

The conversation revolves around the privacy concerns related to web wallets, specifically BWS or Bitcore Wallet Service. The sender expresses concern that BWS might be considered a web wallet but clarifies that private keys are not stored on the server. The recipient explains that they are referring to setups like blockchain.info or CoPay where clients have private keys but rely on servers for wallet information.BWS coordinates transaction proposals in multisignature wallets, stores BIP32 derivation indexes, accesses the blockchain, and provides functions like `getBalance` and `getTxHistory`. The service is designed to be easy to set up and run while ensuring privacy and security. However, the possibility of BWS not having extended public keys may lead to broken functions like `getBalance` or `txHistory`. Gregory Maxwell's proposed schema needs further evaluation.Clients being SPV can check their own balances and download transaction history, which means coordination tasks could be done by storing encrypted blobs on servers instead of the servers themselves having insight into transactions. Copay uses the encrypted blob idea to check balances and transaction history through Insight. However, preventing servers from knowing xpubs keys would only reduce the amount of information known by the server from everything to 95%. A reference to Subspace was provided for further exploration.The discussion also touches upon multisig facilitators having some visibility of the wallet for better user experience and the challenging problem of push notifications in a decentralized manner.Suggestions for improving the privacy of BWS usage include using pseudo-random BIP32 paths with a seed only known by the peers to address the privacy concern related to holding the set of extended public keys of the wallet's peers to derive addresses. A suggestion was made by Peter Todd to give the server the master pubkey P only without the chaincode, which would be more private than giving people the chain code. The approach suggested is also not gratuitously incompatible with hardened derivation.Overall, the conversation focused on the challenges and potential solutions related to web wallet servers and multisig wallets, including privacy concerns and coordination tasks. The developers of BWS are seeking feedback on their usage of BIP32 paths and exploring potential improvements to address privacy concerns.
