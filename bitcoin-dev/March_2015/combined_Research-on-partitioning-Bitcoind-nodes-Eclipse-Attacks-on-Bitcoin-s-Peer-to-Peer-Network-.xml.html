Summary:

The dev list has been discussing the issues of misbehaving peers and attacks on the P2P network. To address this concern, a research paper titled "Eclipse Attacks on Bitcoin's Peer-to-Peer Network" has been shared. The paper describes how an adversary can control enough IP addresses to monopolize all connections to and from a victim bitcoin node, allowing them to exploit it for attacks on the mining and consensus system of Bitcoin.The research paper uses probabilistic analysis, Monte Carlo simulations, measurements, and experiments with live bitcoin nodes to quantify the resources involved in such an attack. It proposes countermeasures inspired by botnet architectures that are designed to raise the bar for eclipse attacks while preserving the openness and decentralization of Bitcoin's current network architecture.The full paper is available at http://cs-people.bu.edu/heilman/eclipse/.
