In 2015, Kalle Rosenbaum proposed a standard format for Proof of Payment (PoP) that would enable wallet-controlled funds to be proven as spent in real transactions. PoP could be used in various scenarios such as pre-paid hotel rooms, online video rental services, ad-signs, and lotteries. The desirable properties of PoP were outlined, including generation on demand, single-use to avoid theft issues, and creation for any payment regardless of script type. Current methods of proving payment, such as BIP0070 and signing messages with private keys, were also discussed.The email thread discussing the concept of "proof of payment" in Bitcoin explored various use cases, including sending a payment ahead of time and collecting goods after confirmations, using hot wallet software for authorized pickup, exporting spent keys to enable PoP to work as a gift claim, and third-party contingency services. However, there were concerns about PoP being permanently invalid transactions to remove any possibility of their being mined and spending everything to fees when used in this way, and also in cases involving reorganizations. It was suggested that changing the version number on the PoP to something other than 1 or using a really high lock_time would help make it less vulnerable.The security issues surrounding PoP were discussed, such as interception of the PoP-request and changing the destination. To address this issue, the server should have mechanisms for detecting a brute force attack of this kind, or at least delay the PoP request by some 100ms. There were also concerns around security issues with 0-confirmation transactions and potential interception of PoPs. Changing the version number on the PoP or using a high lock_time were suggested as possible solutions.Kalle Rosenbaum has also proposed Anonymous Credentials, which allows an issuer to declare that a person has certain rights until a certain date while keeping a degree of accountability for the payment. Zerocoin developers have come up with a paper on a blockchain version of Distributed Anonymous Credentials.In addition to developing the PoP method, Kalle Rosenbaum shared a proposal for improving the efficiency of Bitcoin's network communication via an email to the Bitcoin-development mailing list. The proposal suggests using a "compact block relay" method that would reduce the amount of data that needs to be transmitted between nodes when new blocks are added to the blockchain.Furthermore, Martin and Kalle discussed standardizing PoP as an extension of BIP-70, including adding identifying information, having a POP_TYPE field, and determining the length of the POP_NONCE field. There was also a discussion among developers about using PoP as a way to verify that a payment has been made. The group agreed that PoP could be complementary to BIP70.Overall, the email conversation highlights both the potential benefits and challenges of implementing PoP in Bitcoin transactions.
