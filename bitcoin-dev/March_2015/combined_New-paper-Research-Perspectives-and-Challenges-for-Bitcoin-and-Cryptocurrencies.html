Summary:

A team of researchers from various universities including Stanford, Maryland, Concordia, and Princeton have written a paper about Bitcoin-related research that aims to build a bridge between the computer science research community and the cryptocurrency community. The paper will appear in the Oakland security conference later this year and explains all the dimensions along which altcoins and other next-generation cryptocurrency designs differ from Bitcoin. This effort has been ongoing for around two years and the team is still open to comments and suggestions before the final version is due. The researchers hope their paper helps some of the best ideas and research questions from the Bitcoin community bubble up and inspires researchers to build on them. Joichi Ito from the MIT media Lab has referenced the work during yesterday's MIT Bitcoin Expo.In addition to the paper, an intelligent assistant responds to a minor point made by one of the authors regarding SPV clients not needing to maintain an ever-growing list of PoW solutions. This suggests that the team is dedicated to addressing even small points in order to improve the overall understanding and development of cryptocurrency technology.On a different note, AI Coin is an altcoin set to launch on April 27. Unlike other coins, it does not use staking for network-wide algorithmic trustworthiness and has a single version of blockchain appended by a single peer. Peers wrap Bitcoind instances, controlling the transaction and new block routing for scalability, and have self-signed X.509 certificates to encrypt messages and prevent impersonation. The tamper-evident technology used to secure Bitcoin's blockchain is extended to secure the entire network. Peers maintain tamper-evident logs inspired by an idea published by Nick Szabo, which are replayed, verified and signed by other peers. More information can be found on their forum.Finally, papers being created by researchers in distributed systems groups help convince supervisors to pay more attention to blockchain technologies. The "systemization" paper written by the team of researchers has received positive feedback within the mailing list and forums/wikis/irc channels. The team plans to continue updating their online version for the foreseeable future and the paper can be found at http://www.jbonneau.com/doc/BMCNKF15-IEEESP-bitcoin.pdf.
