Summary:

The Bitcoin community has been discussing the implementation of address expiration to reduce the number of addresses that need to be monitored for future payments. One suggestion is to encode an expiration date into the address itself and specify that clients enforce it, which would force users to periodically create new addresses instead of keeping old ones active indefinitely.However, some have raised concerns about this approach potentially undermining users' rights to reuse their addresses when necessary, such as in the case of a government body issuing addresses for use by a business. The issue of privacy and fungibility through avoiding reuse has also been discussed. Another proposal involves blocking transactions if a prior confirmed transaction has already paid the address, but this method would require an unprunable verification state. The discussion has also touched on stealth address proposals and the potential cost-benefit of implementing address expiration.The core developers have discussed solutions such as limiting duplicate checks to a specific X-block segment. However, concerns have been raised that enforcing reuse-proof addresses could undermine users' rights to reuse their addresses when necessary. Additionally, there is no foolproof method to prevent intentional hiding of payments or changing the payment string. The defense against this is to not accept payments that do not follow the payee's specifications.Harding suggested implementing address expiration by encoding an expiration date into the Bitcoin address itself and having clients enforce it, which could help reduce the burden of monitoring old addresses and prevent the buildup of unused addresses over time. However, Gregory Maxwell expressed concerns about the potential cost and impact on the payment experience, noting that a simpler and safer client-enforced behavior may be preferable. The Australian Digital Currency Commerce Association has proposed a voluntary register for Bitcoin users to avoid unwanted government regulation. While the concept of limited-lifetime addresses presents an intriguing idea for improving Bitcoin's security and efficiency, it remains a hypothetical proposal at this stage. Overall, the topic remains open for further debate and exploration within the Bitcoin community.
