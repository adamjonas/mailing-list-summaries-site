Summary:

The bitcoin-dev community has been discussing various aspects related to NOINPUT and output tagging. One concern raised was regarding the impact on fungibility if output tagging and NOINPUT are used in Eltoo and Statechains. Johnson Lau proposed a solution to reduce fungibility in multiparty eltoo channels by tagging an output, which must be made explicitly by the payer for it to be spendable with NOINPUT. Two ways to tag an output were suggested: setting a certain bit in the tx version or setting a certain bit in the scriptPubKey. While tagging with scriptPubKey would allow for per-output basis tagging, it is only possible with native-segwit, not P2SH. On the other hand, tagging with tx version will also protect P2SH-segwit, but it could only tag all or none outputs in the same transaction.The discussion also addressed the concept of a walletless protocol when relying on a third party to open an off-chain smart contract. ZmnSCPxj suggested that users should have control over their hardware wallet or "somewhat-cold" storage. In settling multi-party channels, the need for a walletless protocol was discussed, with the suggestion that users create channels for their hot off-chain Lightning wallets with some kind of hardware wallet or similar storage they control themselves.Another proposed solution to preventing issues related to communication between hardware wallets and off-chain wallets is to establish a channel factory of three or more members protected by an n-of-n multisig output. However, NOINPUT impacts channel factories because cheating could come from any member of the group. Therefore, a commitment to the state that's spendable only by any later state and is then redeemed after a timelock is workable.Preventing address reuse was another issue discussed, with the consensus being that it can only be prevented at a social level because it is impossible to ban it at the protocol level. Johnson Lau suggested that publishing the key after a coin is confirmed is a stronger way to prevent address reuse.Finally, the discussion centered around settling a three-party channel where A, B, and C have respective balances of 2, 3, and 6 BTC. One proposal suggested establishing a channel factory of three or more members protected by an n-of-n multisig output. Updates to transactions in a lightning channel or sub-factory do not generally involve updating the containing factory.
