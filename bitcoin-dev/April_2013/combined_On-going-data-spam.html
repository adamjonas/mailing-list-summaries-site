However, there was no clear definition of what constitutes an uneconomical output, and concerns were raised about backwards compatibility issues with implementing the proposed patch.Anti-virus software and its impact on Bitcoin's testnet chain were also discussed, with suggestions for storage scrambling if AV tools scan big binary files of unknown types. The conversations involved prominent figures in the Bitcoin community, including Jeff Garzik, Peter Todd, Mike Hearn, Gregory Maxwell, and Caleb James DeLisle.Overall, the discussions highlighted the complex and evolving nature of blockchain technology, and the need for ongoing collaboration and innovation among developers to address emerging challenges and opportunities. The conversation also touched upon broader philosophical issues related to the assumption that blockchains are a data structure that will exist forever and be served by everyone for free, which may not hold true in the long run due to legal issues and the need to host blocks forever.
