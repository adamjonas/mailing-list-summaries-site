Summary:

The Bitcoin network is exploring ways to handle the storage and retrieval of the blockchain. One concern is the potential for side effects and vulnerabilities. Several proposals have been suggested, including a hybrid option where nodes store a section of the blockchain fully and selectively prune all other sections not included in it. Another proposal is for partial archival nodes that can store a subset of blocks so that collectively, the entire blockchain would be available even if no single node had the complete chain. Communication about what blocks a node has should be compact and computationally efficient, and the coverage created by the network should be uniform and remain as such as the blockchain grows. Refetching old blocks should not be necessary.One proposed method of storing certain blocks in the blockchain involves keeping the initial 50MB of data starting at a specific block height, generating a sequence of start points. However, tiny blocks have the same priority as larger blocks, so nodes must keep the first 50MB of blocks with some probability to ensure low-height blocks are covered. Another proposal involves dividing all blocks into intervals with exponentially growing sizes and broadcasting three pieces of information, including Seed, M_bits_lsb, and N, to calculate the sizes of each interval. They also discuss issues with block sync horizon and cliff and suggest using torrents to download ancient data. Overall, the goal is to provide a solution that retains full validation capabilities while distributing the full copy among many nodes, significantly reducing storage needs on a node.Further discussions centered around the issue of security and efficiency in syncing the blockchain, with suggestions for localizing block coverage, compact communication about what blocks a node has, and fractional full nodes instead of just archive and pruned nodes. The decision to contact a node should require O(1) communications to avoid delays and sybil vulnerability. Overall, the discussion highlights the importance of considering different ways to maintain security and participation in the network while ensuring efficient block coverage.
