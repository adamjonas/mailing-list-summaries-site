Summary:

The Open Bitcoin Privacy Project has released its first report on Bitcoin wallet privacy features, which assesses the privacy of several popular wallets. The organization plans to produce more reports over time and is open to community feedback. Justus Ranvier, founder of the project, explained that they rated as many wallets as possible based on the available manpower and will be recruiting more volunteers for future ratings. Eric Lombrozo questioned why mSIGNA was not included in the report, and Ranvier replied that they would add it if it meets their standards. An email from Jonas Schnelli, a representative of include7 AG in Switzerland, includes contact information for the company and a warning about sending sensitive data in unencrypted emails, providing a link to a secure form instead. The email also includes a message from Ranvier regarding the rating of Bitcoin wallets and ends with a question from an unknown sender asking about the absence of Bitcoin-Core and Breadwallet (iOS) from the ratings.
