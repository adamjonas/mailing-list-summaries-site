Summary:

The context explores the potential risks and challenges associated with implementing a hard fork in the Bitcoin system. Gavin Andresen has proposed different levels of supermajority of miners required to trigger a hard fork, ranging from 99% to 80%. The author believes that a consensus on the level of supermajority is necessary before any specific hard fork proposal can be considered. Establishing a sufficiently large supermajority of miners is crucial to avoid forking Bitcoin into two coins. A realistic scenario where 99% of miners vote for a hard fork, and the economic majority votes to stay on the old blockchain seems unlikely due to the significant drop in hashrate and block time. The conversation also touches upon the potential power of users to change the hash function or freeze transactions in extreme circumstances.The email exchange between Pieter Wuille and Roy Badami discusses the safety of a hard fork and the need for miner support. Pieter argues that a hard fork is only safe if 100% of economically relevant users upgrade, while Roy believes that requiring a supermajority of miners is a wise precaution as beliefs can be wrong. The email also mentions a proposal to set a 'block size cap' to prevent rogue miners from creating large blocks if the block-size limit is removed. However, before anyone attempts such a change, they would need to convince people and ensure code modification.In conclusion, the context highlights the importance of rough consensus on the level of supermajority required to establish consensus before debating specific hard fork proposals. It also presents various viewpoints on the safety of hard forks, the need for miner support, and the potential risks associated with the implementation of hard forks.
