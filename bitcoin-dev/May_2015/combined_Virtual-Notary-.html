Summary:

The Virtual Notary project, introduced by Emin G端n Sirer in May 2015, is an online witness to factoids which provides proof of various facts like Bitcoin funds, address ownership, tweet, real estate value, DNS ownership, existence, web page contents, and weather conditions without revealing public addresses or fund location on the blockchain. The website maintains a free attestation chain and also allows recording factoids on the blockchain by paying for the transaction with Bitcoin or PayPal. They provide a permanent URL to the generated factoids and an X.509 certificate that can be downloaded and kept safe in perpetuity. The website link is http://virtual-notary.org, and the link to the writeup describing the various factoids and their use cases is http://hackingdistributed.com/2013/06/20/virtual-notary-intro/.The site needs some user-experience improvements and fixing payment verification issues. Two ideas have also been proposed: decentralizing the notary service log via an open-source P2P daemon and adding an open-source UI app to examine certificates that could demonstrate independence from the website/service. Jonas Schnelli expressed his appreciation for http://www.deedbot.org, a similar website that offers proof of existence and ownership for various types of information. However, Schnelli suggested some improvements for the site's user experience, including fixing issues with Bitcoin payment verification and adding a "US only" badge for weather and real estate notarization.In a conversation, someone praised the tool as a building block for oracle-based services but noted that working with X.509 can be annoying and suggested documenting how to extract data from a certificate. They proposed embedding either a protocol buffer or DER encoded structure inside the extension to allow developers to extract notarized data directly without additional parsing.
