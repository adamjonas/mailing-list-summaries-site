Summary:

Alicexbt, a member of the Bitcoin Development mailing list, has raised concerns over BGP hijacking attacks on Bitcoin nodes. The vulnerability of bitcoin nodes to such attacks was noted in an answer from 2014 on Stack Exchange and was demonstrated during the March 2022 Twitter prefix hijacking incident. The Tor network is also vulnerable to BGP hijacking. To prevent these attacks, Alicexbt suggested using RPKI ROA and BGP prefix length. However, tests run on the first 10 IP addresses returned in `getnodeaddresses` for Bitcoin Core showed vulnerable results. In response, Elias recommended research articles by Maria Apostolaki et al., Muoi Tran et al., and related works that delve into routing attacks. Alicexbt conducted some research and discovered that RPKI ROA and BGP prefix length could help prevent BGP hijacking attacks. They are working on a simple Chrome extension that connects to local bitcoin core and checks the IP address of all peers for prefix length and other attributes. The extension will highlight peers with different colors based on certain criteria.Finally, Alicexbt expressed interest in writing a detailed blog post or research article on the subject and requested technical feedback and links to past discussions with potential solutions. If no such resources exist, they plan to write one themselves in the coming weeks.
