Summary:

The Bitcoin community is currently discussing ways to maintain the security and value of the network without relying solely on transaction fees, which are subject to a well-established day/night cycle. One major concern is that this could lead to a mining cabal that could eventually 51% attack the whole thing. To counteract this, measures that smooth out fees over time were suggested, such as having wallets opportunistically collect dust during times of low transaction fees and creating a UX that clarifies when transactions may take longer but are reliable.Incentivizing miners to build on a particular block instead of reorganizing it was also discussed. While some suggested using anyone-can-spend outputs as bribes, others argued that this would lead to centralization pressure. A new type of transaction would need to be added to make the bribes only valid in a specific block, requiring a signature committing to the non-segwit part of the coinbase outputs.Other topics of discussion included different approaches to smoothing out block rewards and preventing tail supply attacks. Some suggested sending 0.01 BTC per block to timelocked outputs or burning all fees while keeping the ~21M coins limit. However, there were concerns that these approaches went against Bitcoin values and may lead to monopolization by rich holders who can create cartels.The community also discussed potential issues that could arise if transaction fees remained unpredictable due to the established day/night cycle and longer gaps on weekends and holidays. To counteract these issues, miners may create anyone-can-spend outputs to bribe other miners to build on their block rather than reorg it, requiring some amount of floating capital.Overall, the discussions emphasized the importance of market design in ensuring that fees are chosen based on the weight of transactions, rather than relying completely on transaction fees for security. Proper distribution is crucial for the emission curve, and the community is discussing possible solutions to address the issue of inconsistent transaction fees, which could pose a serious threat to Bitcoin's security if not addressed.The conversation concludes that designing protocols in anticipation of different scenarios is essential as the future remains unpredictable. Bitcoin's network security is currently maintained by users who act out of self-interest, rather than relying on fees. However, if it fails to grow in utility compared to fiat currencies, there is no point in maintaining the network. Possible solutions to address the issue of inconsistent transaction fees include having wallets opportunistically collect their dust during times of low transaction fees and making UX which clarifies when things are likely to take a day or week but that it's reliable. The even distribution of these fees over time could act similarly to fixed block rewards, but in reality, this is not the case. Market design is key to ensuring that fees are chosen based on the weight of transactions, with weight being the sole limiting factor for miners.In conclusion, the Bitcoin community is exploring various proposals to maintain the network's security without relying solely on transaction fees. Proper distribution is crucial for the emission curve, and possible solutions include dragging most of East Asia eastward to a later time zone to smooth out the day/night cycle or hard forking in fixed rewards in perpetuity. Evidence shows that the market can support higher fees, though it's unclear whether working on this today would be smart.
