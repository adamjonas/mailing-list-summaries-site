Summary:

Bitcoin developers are debating the use of full RBF (Replace-By-Fee) as a policy in vulnerable projects, such as coinjoins and dual-funded LN channels. Antoine Riard submitted a patch enabling Bitcoin Core to turn on full-RBF, but there are concerns about its security implications if a project relies on it. Some suggest providing basic replace-by-fee policy options rather than defining a good policy and removing the ability to disable it when necessary.There have been reports of DoS attacks affecting almost all types of coinjoin transactions, including coordinator-based implementations, and discussions about the potential for DoS attacks on popular Bitcoin wallet Wasabi. The lack of a basic option to disable all RBF policies in a node's mempool was removed, but developers should provide basic RBF policy options rather than attempting to define what constitutes a good policy and removing the ability to disable something when necessary.Bitcoin Knots still uses the service bit, despite a bug in some older versions that did not signal it by default. At least 100 nodes with full RBF already exist, and node operators interested in experimenting with it can connect to a public node or spawn one themselves. In an email exchange between Antoine Riard and Peter Todd, they discuss fee-bumping transactions without the opt-in RBF flag set, and Todd suggests reaching out to mining node operators to advocate for the new policy setting once the "fullrbf" patch lands in Bitcoin Core.The Lightning dual-funded channel proposal aims to increase the efficiency and usability of the Lightning network by enabling users to easily create channels with other parties in a secure and decentralized manner. However, there are concerns about the security and reliability of new use-cases that did not exist a few years ago. Overall, developers should provide basic RBF policy options rather than attempting to define a universal policy and removing the ability to disable it when necessary. Users can manage their LN usage by using better ISPs or adopting different mempool policies.
