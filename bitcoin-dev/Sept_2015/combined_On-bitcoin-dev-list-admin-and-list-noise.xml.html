Summary:

The bitcoin-dev mailing list is intended for discussing ongoing development issues related to the protocol and its instantiation in bitcoin core. However, some developers have switched to digest-only or unsubscribed due to concerns that the list could become a channel for PR and populist venting, which could decrease its utility by expanding its scope. A member expressed concern that the mailing list is one of the last uncensored persisted forums related to technical bitcoin discussion with wide viewership, but there is uncertainty regarding who will draw the line on what is considered "off-topic" by list admins.A suggestion has been made to reduce noise on the bitcoin-dev mailing list by narrowing its focus to near-term technical changes, while creating a second list, bitcoin-tech-discuss, for broader discussions. This split has served IRC well and generally manages to keep the noise down to a productive level. The goal is to increase productivity by focusing on technical discussions related to the development of bitcoin while providing a separate platform for broader discussions.Furthermore, it was suggested that maintainers should take charge and make decisions on how Core does things to reduce circular conversations and prevent random people from dismissing valid objections as philosophical discussions. Despite these concerns, it only takes 15 minutes or less daily to read the forum while drinking coffee.
