The bitcoin-dev mailing list was created in 2011 to focus on the technical aspects of core software and avoid philosophical discussions. However, over time, it became cluttered with off-topic noise, impacting productivity. To address this issue, a discussion list is being created as an outlet for these types of conversations.In an email conversation on the bitcoin-dev mailing list, Milly Bitcoin stated that past events are not relevant to the current state of Bitcoin as the stakeholders have changed and expanded. The discussion revolved around the importance of clearly describing any topic being discussed when people sign up.A member of the bitcoin-dev mailing list, Neil Haran, posted about his idea for a gamified bitcoin mining app and is looking for a partner who is knowledgeable in cryptography and the Bitcoin code base. He has received some interest from individuals but is still searching for the ideal candidate to work with. Neil provided his email address (nharan81@gmail.com) for interested parties to contact him. The post reminds readers not to solicit interest in advance of content but instead to share the idea first so that people can decide if they find it cogent and interesting before volunteering any assistance. The author of a post is responding to individuals who are claiming that certain topics being discussed on a development discussion list for Bitcoin protocol and its implementation are "off-topic". The author suggests that a more precise description may be necessary if the discussion is to be limited in some way beyond the current "official" description. Overall, the author believes that claims about what should be posted on the list are without merit and that individuals should not be making up their own rules without proper authority or guidelines.Neil Haran proposes to build the largest rock-paper-scissors (RPS) tournament ever seen, using bitcoin-qt. The tournament is envisioned to be like the World Series of Poker but for RPS. Each player will play a best-of-five tournament each round with players randomly matched against each other. The number of rounds would effectively be the depth of a binary tree. For instance, if a million people enter the tournament, they would need to win 20 rounds to win the cash prize that Neil is offering. Neil also mentions he has media connections to get coverage for this and fully believes he could get millions playing in this tournament.Trevin Hofmann previously started a cryptographically secure RPS game as a proof of concept. However, due to time constraints, he was unable to further develop it. He provided a link to the GitHub repository for the RPS protocol for interested parties.The Bitcoin-dev mailing list received an email on September 29, 2015, that was deemed off-topic by Jeff Garzik, a prominent member of the bitcoin development community. Milly Bitcoin responded to Garzik's comment and accused him of pretending to be in charge and telling people what to do. The context noted that making statements about a developer's personal character is also off-topic for the list.Developers who created the bitcoin-dev list are in agreement that something needed to be done about the off-topic noise. Since 2011, the bitcoin-development list was created to focus on highly technical aspects of the core software and avoid philosophical discussions. However, since then, Bitcoin has changed and stakeholders have expanded. Therefore, it is important for the actual description of the list to clearly describe what people can expect to discuss when they sign up. This will ensure that discussions remain relevant and focused. The list's original intention to avoid philosophical discussions is not as important as providing a clear understanding of what topics are acceptable.
