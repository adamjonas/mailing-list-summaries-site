Summary:

A member of the Bitcoin-dev mailing list questioned the naming of "soft forks" and their potential danger. Soft forks are protocol rule changes that are compatible with the existing network, unlike hardforks which are incompatible. They allow for critical fixes and improvements to be made by narrowing the permissible actions of participants, reducing the risk and cost associated with flag days where users coordinate software upgrades.The behavior being soft-forked is first made non-standard, meaning that participants will not relay, mine, or display unconfirmed transactions that violate the new rule. Violations in a block are still accepted but a vast super-majority of hashpower (recently 95%) agrees to enforce the new rule via the blockchain itself, resulting in low disruption on account of the enforcement. Old software continues to enforce all the old rules it always enforced, with the only difference in behavior relating to non-standard transactions and contests between otherwise valid blocks.
