Summary:

Lykke Corp, a Zurich-based company, funded a Bitcoin-related competition in 2015 to build a trading platform that allows participants to settle trades through the blockchain via colored coins. The competition initially focused on foreign exchange, with other assets to follow. The use of a sidechain was suggested but not required for trade reconciliation. Richard Olsen, who sent the message to the bitcoin-dev mailing list, invited questions about the competition. However, this competition is off-topic for the mailing list as it focuses on bitcoin protocol and reference implementation development.The competition aimed at developing a trading platform for foreign exchange and other assets that would allow participants to settle their trades through the blockchain using colored coins. Lykke Corp funded the competition and offered 2 million Lykke coins ($8,000 USD) for core development over the next three months. More details on the requirements can be found on the www.lykkex.com website. Contributions were made available under an MIT license on Github to make this a community-driven project.Despite the excitement around the competition, some members of the Bitcoin-dev mailing list expressed their concern about the lack of details regarding who was funding the competition and why core development was not being funded. In response to the announcement of the trading platform competition, one member questioned why the funds were not being used to fund Core development instead. Eric Lombrozo, another member of the community, suggested creating a list where people can discuss proposed and ongoing projects and look for developers to hire. This suggestion came after some members of the Bitcoin-dev mailing list expressed the need for a more open mailing list for Bitcoin and a dedicated venue for discussing proposed and ongoing projects.
