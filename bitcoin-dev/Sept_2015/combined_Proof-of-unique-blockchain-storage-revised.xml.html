Summary:

Incentivizing Bitcoin network nodes to exist can be accomplished by paying peers for providing data in the blockchain. However, it is challenging to determine if a peer is genuinely storing the blockchain or relying on other peers as a proxy. Asymmetric-time functions can be implemented to prove unique (IP-tied) blockchain storage and make improvements to make it practical. Sergio outlines this proposal in detail on his blog, including a review of the use of asymmetric-time functions.
