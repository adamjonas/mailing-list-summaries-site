Summary:

Ken Shirriff, a member of the Bitcoin community, has proposed adding the bitcoin symbol to Unicode standard, as it is currently not included. The use of the bitcoin symbol in text is inconvenient because it is not recognized by the Unicode standard. Shirriff believes that having this symbol added will make using the bitcoin symbol in text more convenient.The proposed symbol is the common B-with-vertical-bars bitcoin symbol, which is easily confused with the Thai Bhat currency symbol. Danny Thorpe suggested using the already available B-with-horizontal-bar (Ƀ) symbol, but it was argued that it may lead to confusion with Thai baht currency symbol.Shirriff has previously proposed a new character for Unicode and thinks this proposal has a good chance of succeeding. He has requested endorsement from Bitcoin developers to help the Unicode Committee understand the significance of adding this symbol. Contact information for Richard Moore, founder of Genetic Mistakes Software Inc., is also included in the email thread. Theo Chino has contacted Ken Shirriff to join and support his proposal to add the bitcoin symbol to Unicode.In an email sent to the bitcoin-dev group, Shirriff requested their support for the proposal. He believes that endorsement from Bitcoin developers will help the Unicode Committee realize the importance of adding this symbol. A link to the Bitcoin symbol page on en.bitcoin.it/wiki is provided in the email thread.Danny, however, is not fond of B-with-horizontal-bar (Ƀ) as a symbol. The full proposal can be found at http://righto.com/bitcoin-unicode.pdf.
