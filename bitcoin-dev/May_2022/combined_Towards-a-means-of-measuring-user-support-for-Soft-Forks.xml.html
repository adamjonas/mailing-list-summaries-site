Summary:

The Bitcoin community is currently engaged in a debate on how to measure user support for proposed soft-fork changes. One proposal involves allowing transactions themselves to signal for upgrade, while another suggests incentivizing CoinJoin use and enforcing it as a soft fork. However, concerns have been raised about potential manipulation and false consensus with these proposals.To address the lack of ability to measure consensus currently, alternative mechanisms have been proposed such as coin-weighted polling, transaction signaling, miner signaling, and developer consensus reviews. The community hopes to find a logical solution that is defendable and encodable into the social fabric of Bitcoin, rather than arguing until only one opinion remains standing.The conversation also centers around the concept of rationality and consensus in decision-making for Bitcoin, with some suggesting a futures market for predictions as a way to understand people's goals without revealing them directly. The issue of soft fork activation in Bitcoin raises questions about the threshold for saying "this consensus change is ready for activation" and whether it changes based on the nature of the consensus change and different constituencies.There are concerns about who would have power over deciding consensus and whether implementing certain proposals would encourage spam. The group continues to discuss how to come to a consensus in a structured way, moving away from appeals to authority and relying on logic and tooling to measure consensus. Ultimately, the goal is to ensure that technical consensus aligns with user consensus to avoid major schisms in the future.
