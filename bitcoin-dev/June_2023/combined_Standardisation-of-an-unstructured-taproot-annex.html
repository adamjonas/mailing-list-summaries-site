Standardization processes for the annex were discussed, with ongoing debates about its structure and adoption.The discussions highlighted the potential benefits of the taproot annex, including improved space efficiency and the ability to include additional data in transaction witnesses. Concerns were raised regarding the incentive compatibility of altering witness transaction IDs and the potential for malleability attacks. Proposed solutions included implementing changes to allow transaction replacement by smaller witnesses and addressing payload extension attacks.The use of the annex in LN-Symmetry for time-locked vaults was discussed, along with the need for alternative methods of relaying transactions to miners outside of the peer-to-peer network. Presigned transactions were deemed more efficient and private, while the annex offered improved space efficiency. Concerns were raised about witness replacement and the need for alternative transaction relay systems, with considerations for transaction fees, bandwidth usage, and future consensus changes.Concerns about annex malleability and its impact on transaction integrity were addressed, with suggestions to inform developers of the risks and focus on fixing annex malleability in a future soft fork. The benefits of using the taproot annex with LN-Symmetry and the elimination of commit transactions were discussed, highlighting the advantages for time-locked vaults.The design of the taproot annex was also discussed, emphasizing the need for immediate utilization and future flexibility. It was suggested to define any annex starting with '0' as free-form without constraints. Safety concerns for time-locked vaults were addressed through alternatives like scriptpath solutions and offchain data storage. The discussions explored various aspects of the taproot annex, including its potential for data storage and time-locked vaults, as well as proposals for addressing payload extension attacks.In summary, the discussions aimed to address concerns and propose solutions related to annex malleability in Bitcoin transactions. Proposed solutions included allowing the use of the annex without restrictions, improving time-locked vaults, and defining a non-structured form for the taproot annex. The conversations emphasized the importance of balancing risks and benefits while maximizing the potential of the annex in different applications.
