The conversation begins with a definition of front-running in a peer-to-peer marketplace, where a trade is made with advance knowledge of a block transaction that will affect the price of the underlying security. Mary the Maker publishes a limit order to buy a derivative, which Bob the bulletin board operator can execute or relay to Terry the Taker and other subscribers. The concern is raised that this model resembles a brokerage rather than an exchange market structure, and it is suggested that Terry should open his own brokerage to compete with Bob. The focus is on finding what products people want to trade and providing a good user experience.The discussion then shifts to the concept of front-running resistance in different types of trades. It is acknowledged that certain types of trades, such as commodities, may have lower front-running risks due to factors like production cycles and weather. However, for trades involving fiat currencies or bitcoin financial contracts, front-running risk by the bulletin board is a qualified concern. Two security paradigms are proposed to mitigate front-running risks. The first involves duplicating offers to multiple independent bulletin boards and monitoring latency anomalies. The second suggests running the bulletin boards as a federation using techniques like Honey Badger BFT.The conversation concludes with the idea that the CivKit framework provides a flexible approach to peer-to-peer marketplaces, allowing for adaptation of front-running resistance parameters based on the types of trades and market participants' tolerance. Antoine Riard, Nicholas Gregory, and Ray Youssef are working on a peer-to-peer market system that enables censorship-resistant and permissionless global trading. The design combines the Nostr architecture with Lightning onion routing infrastructure to provide confidentiality to market participants. The market boards are Nostr relays with a Lightning gateway, operating autonomously and in competition. Trades are escrowed under Bitcoin Script contracts, relying on moderations and know your peer oracles for adjudication.Feedback on the paper publication highlighted the importance of front-running resistance in a peer-to-peer marketplace. Two security paradigms are proposed to mitigate front-running risks, involving duplicating offers to multiple boards and running the boards as a federation. The whitepaper also proposes a Web-of-Stakes framework for scoring trades, counterparties, and service operators. The researchers plan to release code gradually, leveraging Lightning Dev Kit and Nostr libraries, and they aim to collaborate with the community to standardize libraries and ensure interoperability between clients. The researchers aim to build a secure and efficient peer-to-peer market system while adhering to open-source and decentralized standards established by Bitcoin Core.
