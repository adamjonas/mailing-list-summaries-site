Summary:

In a recent discussion, the concept of front-running in a peer-to-peer marketplace was explored. Front-running refers to entering a trade with advance knowledge of a block transaction that will impact the price of the underlying security. This can be a concern in the context of a bulletin board operator who has the option to execute or relay orders from market participants.The scenario discussed involved Mary the Maker, Terry the Taker, and Bob the bulletin board operator. Mary publishes a limit order to buy a derivative, which Bob can choose to execute against. If Bob decides not to execute, he relays the order to Terry and other subscribers. Terry then has the option to execute the trade. If Terry declines, Mary's order sits on the bulletin board.One perspective shared was that this model resembles more of a brokerage rather than an exchange market structure. It was suggested that Terry should consider opening their own brokerage and compete with Bob by providing better prices. By comparing metrics like execution quality, Bob and Terry can draw more market activity. Front-running was mentioned as a potential concern, but it was also seen as a sign of active participants and liquidity in the market. The focus was placed on finding what products people are interested in trading and providing them with a good user experience. The idea is that everything else will fall into place after that.Different security paradigms were discussed to mitigate front-running risks. One approach involved duplicating the announcement of offers to multiple independent bulletin boards and monitoring latency anomalies. Another suggestion was to run the bulletin boards as a federation, using consensus mechanisms to ensure fair publication of offers.Overall, the CivKit framework proposes a flexible system for peer-to-peer marketplaces that can adapt to different types of trades and market conditions. The design aims to enable censorship-resistant and permissionless global trading while maintaining confidentiality for participants. Reputation systems and incentive frameworks are also considered to ensure efficient and trustworthy operations.The authors of the paper express their gratitude to the peer reviewers for their input on the architecture of the system. They announce their plans to release code and modules gradually, leveraging the Lightning Dev Kit and Nostr libraries. The development will adhere to the established Bitcoin open-source and decentralized standards set by Bitcoin Core. The authors are enthusiastic about collaborating with the community to standardize libraries and ensure interoperability between clients in the long run. They welcome feedback from the community.The paper's link is provided [3], along with additional links to the Lightning Dev Kit [4] and Nostr libraries.
