Summary:

In a recent discussion, Antoine Riard addressed the concern of front-running resistance in a peer-to-peer marketplace. Front-running refers to entering a trade with advance knowledge of a block transaction that will influence the price of the underlying security. Antoine outlined a scenario involving Mary the Maker, Terry the Taker, and Bob the bulletin board operator. Mary publishes a limit order to buy a derivative, and Bob has the option to execute against her order. If Bob chooses not to execute, he relays the order to Terry and other subscribers to his market. Terry then has the option to execute a trade against Mary's order, and if he decides not to, the order sits on the bulletin board.Antoine believes that this model is more akin to a brokerage rather than an exchange market structure. He suggests that Terry should open his own brokerage and compete with Bob on quoting prices. By comparing metrics like execution quality, Bob and Terry can attract more market activity by providing better prices. Antoine also mentions the possibility of tiered services, where a market bulletin board focuses on processing large-scale volumes of data, while brokers offer content aggregation. Monitoring algorithms, similar to those used for watchtowers in Lightning clients, can be implemented to assess execution quality.Regarding front-running concerns, Antoine proposes two security paradigms. The first involves duplicating the announcement of offers to multiple concurrent boards run by independent identities and monitoring latency anomalies. If repeated anomalies occur, makers can disqualify misbehaving boards. However, this approach may not be perfect as boards can collude. The second paradigm suggests running bulletin boards as a federation using Honey Badger BFT or similar technologies. Offers become consensus items announced to all federation members, and e-cash tokens can serve as payment credentials for publication. Members earn income as a group, following consensus rules.Antoine concludes that front-running, while perceived as a concern, indicates active participants and liquidity in the market. He emphasizes the importance of finding products people are interested in trading and providing a good user experience. Additionally, he discusses the possibility of different types of trades, such as fiat currencies or bitcoin financial contracts, having varying levels of front-running risk. Finally, Antoine mentions the CivKit framework's flexibility, allowing parameters to be adjusted based on real-world deployments.In a recent announcement on the Lightning Dev mailing list, Nick, Ray, and Antoine shared their excitement about the release of code and modules for a new system architecture. The team plans to leverage the Lightning Dev Kit and Nostr libraries as they adhere to the open-source and decentralized standards established by Bitcoin Core. They emphasize the importance of collaboration and standardization within the Bitcoin community to ensure long-term interoperability between clients.The authors welcome feedback from the community regarding their work.
