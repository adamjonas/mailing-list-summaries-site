Summary:

Bitcoin Core developers have been discussing the activation of Taproot and debating on the best course of action. One proposal is to activate it through a traditional, no-frills flag day activation with a suggested date of August 2022. However, some developers disagree on the implementation of lot=false, which could cause drama and lead to a minority group threatening to fork off onto a lot=true chain. There is also concern about miners generating invalid blocks through SPV mining, and "bad block enhanced selfish mining" could be profitable.Another proposal is to use BIP-8 to enable early activation, but there are concerns about a cooptable consensus process and potential compatibility issues with clients. Some argue for mandatory signaling instead of a flag day, while others believe that removing miners' influence over consensus rules entirely is the best solution.There is much debate around user-activated forks and the best way to ensure activation without causing a chain split. David Harding's "speedy trial" approach is seen as a better solution than running a lot=true variant. The determining factor in ensuring that the fork has activated is whether a vast majority of economically-active nodes enforce the rules of a new fork. Despite these concerns, it was generally agreed that a flag day may be the best option at this time, and using LOT=true could allow for early activation if desired. The group also emphasized that LOT=true for Taproot is not a precedent-setting occurrence for any future forks. Ultimately, the discussions highlighted the complexity of implementing changes to the Bitcoin network and the need for careful consideration and planning.Bitcoin developer Matt Corallo has proposed activating Taproot via a traditional flag day activation with a suggested date of August 2022. This would require bip8/lockinontimeout=true for activation by block 747936. Corallo also proposes updating getblocktemplate to allow miners to signal uptake for taproot rules prior to activation.There were debates about the activation method for Taproot, with some individuals committing themselves to running incompatible consensus rules, which could potentially cause harm if a fork were to occur as a result. The issue of SPV mining generating invalid blocks has also been discussed, with concerns about the potential profitability of "bad block enhanced selfish mining." It was noted that releasing a flag day without LOT=true code would make clients incompatible with early activation proposals, and that there were pros and cons to activating rules at a fixed date versus via signaling. Ultimately, the discussions highlighted the complexity of implementing changes to the Bitcoin network and the need for careful consideration and planning.Bitcoin operates without identity or centralized control, and anyone can mine and/or accept bitcoin - nobody is in charge. In order to change consensus rules, there are three options: hard fork, soft fork, and 51% attack. However, even a 51% attack may fail as no one can assume 100% explicit adoption of any rule change. Miner signaling provides some level of assurance that it will be successful and this level of assurance is increased by adoption of a higher than majority threshold.
