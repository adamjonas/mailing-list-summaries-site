Summary:

The recent discussion on the Bitcoin-dev mailing list focused on the security of a proposed HD wallet scheme that uses a private key to calculate a public key and incrementing it. A concern was raised over the ease of linking all resulting public keys together. The design of an interesting and simple HD wallet that used pure ECDSA and SHA-256 was shared in the bitcoin forum. It allows for potentially unlimited custom derivation paths and all keys start with 02 prefix.Erik Aronesty suggested using sha3-256 instead of sha256 for generating hashes due to certain attacks such as length extension. A user named Arik Sosman asked if sha256-hmac(nonce, publicKeyPoint) would be a suitable and safe alternative. The discussions highlighted the importance of choosing secure algorithms when designing cryptographic schemes and thoroughly testing and verifying their security before implementation.However, Tim Ruffing cautioned against assuming safety of the hierarchical deterministic (HD) wallet scheme that can be specified in just three lines without specifying the security goals. One respondent agreed that the intuition behind the proposal was sound but raised concerns about the predictability of the offset and the possibility of deriving a parent private key from a child private key. They suggested that BIP 32 would be a more practical solution. Another respondent noted that the security goals of the proposed scheme had not been specified, and cautioned against assuming safety based on a brief description.In summary, it is important to prioritize safety when it comes to cryptocurrency wallets. While the use of sha3-256 over sha256 may provide better protection against certain attacks, the safety of any new implementations should be verified and tested. Additionally, HD wallet schemes like BIP 32 address concerns such as predictability and the possibility of deriving a parent private key from a child private key. Overall, the discussions highlight the importance of careful consideration and thorough testing when designing cryptographic schemes.
