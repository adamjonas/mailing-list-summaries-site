Summary:

On April 1st and 2nd, 2014, the Bitcoin-development mailing list received messages from Kevin, a developer who claimed to have forked Bitcoin Core and was working on a secure tax "mode" for Bitcoin written in Autoit. However, the link he provided to his project was suspicious and led to a phishing site with two Bs in the URL.Some members of the mailing list questioned the legitimacy of Kevin's project, while others suggested that he may have accidentally linked to the wrong website due to speech-to-text or other assistive technology. Jeff Garzik criticized Kevin's choice of URL, calling it a phishing attempt, but Kevin defended himself, stating that his project was serious and well-intentioned. Laszlo Hanyecz speculated that the website might be serving up exploits selectively, targeting specific individuals while showing others a 'domain for sale' message.Eventually, Jud pointed out the correct repository location for Kevin's project, and Kevin promised to register a domain for his project and create a proper webpage. Despite some doubts and confusion surrounding Kevin's project, he invited open dialogue and feedback about it.Additionally, a user named Laszlo Hanyecz reported that the website, www.githubb.com, associated with Kevin's link resolves to addresses announced by AS53665, which is a malware site. The article linked discusses how Tor developers' computers were mysteriously re-routed to Virginia, indicating potential NSA interception. Finally, there was a reminder to support online privacy by using email encryption whenever possible and a link to learn more about it.A cautionary email sent out to Bitcoin developers and enthusiasts warns against accessing websites associated with AS53665, which is believed to have a dedi or VPS at Cogent. The website www.githubb.com, associated with this AS number, is reported to have malware by Google's Safe Browsing service. The author of the email, Kevin, mentions that he has forked Bitcoin core to create a secure tax "mode" for Bitcoin using Autoit scripting language. He invites people to review his work and provide feedback, both positive and negative. Jeff Garzik, a Bitcoin core developer and open source evangelist, signs off on the email with his contact information at BitPay Inc.
