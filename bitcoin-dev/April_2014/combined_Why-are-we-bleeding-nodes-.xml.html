Summary:

The Bitcoin community is focused on improving the efficiency, speed, and user experience of using Bitcoin. Suggestions have been made to create pre-baked databases calculated up to the last checkpoint, extending the protocol to support UDP transport, and loading headers and blocks in random order. Additionally, there were discussions about pruning the blockchain, using a bitmap to advertise non-contiguous blocks, and improving block fetching capabilities.One issue that has been discussed is the declining trend in Bitcoin nodes, which has raised concerns about improving user experience and making it simpler for average users to run Bitcoin correctly. The resource requirements for running a full node have also been debated, with some arguing that it is not necessarily a problem that these requirements are becoming too much for casual users. However, others highlight implementation issues where running a full-node on home DSL connections can cause other internet activity to become unresponsive. There are also concerns over the safety and trustworthiness of Bitcoin following the collapse of Mt Gox. Multi-sig authentication is seen as a good start towards addressing security concerns, but trusting a third-party with full access to your Bitcoin is just replacing one centralized banking system with another. It is important that people use Bitcoin properly to ensure its survival.In May 2014, China updated its firewall and blocked Bitcoin sites and pools, targeting VPN handshakes specifically. However, the number of full nodes in the Bitcoin network does not matter as long as it is enough to satisfy demand by other nodes. Having a high number of nodes does have benefits, such as lower cost of running each node and psychological benefit of making Bitcoin appear bigger and more robust. Establishing better metrics to measure capacity vs demand is necessary as there is no good way to measure it currently. The characteristic of being able to make use of non-dedicated nodes should be regarded as an advantage of the Bitcoin protocol, and not something to get rid of as it may lead to even more robustness than decentralization alone.
