Summary:

The Bitcoin development team discussed several issues related to the compatibility of Bitcoin-Qt on different systems, including Tails users who cannot build Bitcoin-Qt from source as it is a mostly stateless Linux distribution for privacy applications. The team suggested possible solutions, including removing one symbol or putting it behind #ifdef COMPATIBILITY_BUILD. Building against the 4.6 Qt headers instead of statically building may be more compatible. For users who need Bitcoin-Qt on an incompatible system, they can at least build it from source.In an email exchange from April 24, 2014, Wladimir, Gregory Maxwell, and Warren Togami Jr. discussed options for building against the 4.6 Qt headers to avoid a library swap. Wladimir suggested using a cut-off point of 4.7 or 4.8 since Qt 4.6 was ancient as it was released in February 2010, besides Tails, nobody was using those old stable distributions on the desktop.Warren clarified that while bitcoin-qt may not work due to qt-4.6, bitcoind does work on most distributions. He emphasized that the priority is for bitcoind to work on as many distributions as possible since older stable distributions are generally headless. For users who need Bitcoin-Qt on an incompatible system, building it from source would be the only option.In another email thread, Warren suggested that for users who need Bitcoin-Qt on an incompatible system, they can at least build it from source. However, Tails users cannot usually build it from source. One member of the thread agreed that statically linking QT on Linux generally should be avoided due to theming issues.The Bitcoin Core developers announced a development roadmap for v0.9.2 in May 2014, which focused mainly on bug-fixes and translation updates. The feature and string freeze started three weeks from May 2014. The developers may delay particular goals if there are good reasons on a case-by-case basis. Non-developers and translators could get involved in testing unofficial deterministic nightly builds available at https://bitcointalk.org/index.php?topic=571414.0. Transifex allowed open source projects to coordinate the work of many translators.The release date for Tails 1.1, based on Wheezy, is set to be released on June 10th. In an email correspondence, Kristov Atlas raised concerns about the compatibility of the latest nightly build with Tails/Debian Squeeze. Warren clarified that while bitcoin-qt may not work due to qt-4.6, bitcoind does work on most distributions. The official Bitcoin 0.8.6 binaries also did not work on Squeeze, so this was not a regression.The Bitcoin-Translators mailing list is an announce-only mailing list for developers to communicate to translators at specific times when new translations are needed. Prior to an intended release, a String Freeze is declared on a specific date to ensure that translators have a reasonable amount of time to translate new or modified source strings so their work can be included in a release. v0.9.2 is planned to be released directly from the master branch so translations for this upcoming release can be developed directly.In conclusion, the Bitcoin development team discussed various issues related to Bitcoin-Qt compatibility on different systems, the release of version 0.9.2 with a focus on bug fixes and translation updates, and the need for a cut-off point regarding the use of old stable distributions on the desktop. The team also acknowledged the value of subscriptions to the Bitcoin-Translators mailing list and made nightly Gitian Builds available to facilitate testing by non-developers and translators.
