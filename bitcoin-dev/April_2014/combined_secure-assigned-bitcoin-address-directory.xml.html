Summary:

In the Bitcoin space, ongoing conversations and ideas among developers are taking place to address various proposals related to Bitcoin development. One of the proposals is to use a decentralized ledger of device keys for encrypting messages to an intended recipient without a third-party CA or prior dialogue. The ledger is called the "distributed ledger of device keys" and is a re-imagining of the web-of-trust concept. Chris D'Costa is working on this ledger, which requires devices to send a last heard from date to ensure records are not expired. If a record has been expired, the device key will require reinitialization.Another proposal being discussed among users on bitcointalk is regarding vanity addresses. Users want their vanity addresses to be easily accessible for others to find and verify ownership through a web of trust. However, the current method of signing their own addresses and posting them on forums is not secure. Various proposals were discussed, including integrating OpenPGP into payment protocol or defining new types of Bitcoin addresses such as stealth addresses. PGP keyservers were also considered but would only work for single vanity addresses. A potential solution could be utilizing webfinger where servers of a business can prove ownership of a specific address. Daryl Banttari suggests using a simple x509 digital signature to a Bitcoin address URL for authentication purposes. This would allow for an x.509 authenticated Bitcoin address without having to do a full BIP70 implementation. For added authentication, one could wrap the URL with a PGP signature. Despite SSL's faults, Daryl believes that PKI is a pragmatic solution to verifying signatures in Bitcoin transactions.Jeff Garzik brought up the topic of using Namecoin in combination with SIN or PGP identity. As of September 2021, Namecoin appears to be a viable option for those interested in decentralized domain registration and online identity verification.A hardware wallet is also being developed to address the security of transmission of person-to-person pay-to addresses. The Bitcoin software using a second blockchain, like Namecoin, was not seen as necessary for providing safe communication of a non-face-to-face, person-to-person, pay-to address. Instead, a standard equivalent to BIP70 for this use case is expected to emerge. Overall, the email thread showcases ongoing conversations and ideas among developers in the Bitcoin space regarding various proposals related to Bitcoin development.
