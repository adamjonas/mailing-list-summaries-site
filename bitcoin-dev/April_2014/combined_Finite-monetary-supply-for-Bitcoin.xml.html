Summary:

In April 2014, Pieter Wuille proposed the idea of turning Bitcoin into a limited-supply currency and wrote a draft BIP for discussion. He believed that the current subsidy schedule was unacceptable and suggested reducing the block reward by half every four years until it reaches zero, which would result in a maximum supply of 21 million Bitcoins. Matt Corallo suggested redesigning the whole system with a hard fork instead of a soft fork, but questioned whether the community could reach an agreement in such a short timeframe. The proposal was met with controversy, but Wuille considered it a worthwhile idea to explore. In an email exchange on April 5, 2014, Matt Whitlock and Jorge Timón discussed their preferences for date formats. Timón expressed his liking for both DD-MM-YYYY and YYYY-MM-DD formats but disliking MM-DD-YYYY and YYYY-DD-MM. Whitlock pointed out that Timón's preferences reflect a cultural bias and argued that the only entirely numeric date format that is unambiguous across all cultures is YYYY-MM-DD.
