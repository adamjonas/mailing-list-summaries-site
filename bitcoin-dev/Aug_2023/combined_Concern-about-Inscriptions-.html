Summary:

The discussion on the bitcoin-dev mailing list revolves around the issues related to the growth and storage limitations of the blockchain. It is argued that while the blockchain's growth rate is significant, the storage capacity is still sufficient. Even in a worst-case scenario, where the blockchain grows by 210.24GB per year, the decreasing cost of hardware makes it feasible to store the data for a long time. For example, a 5TB hard drive costing less than $150 can last for 21 years before running out of space. The use of optimization techniques like utreexo is also mentioned to further enhance storage efficiency.The discussion also addresses the problem of unconfirmed transactions and their impact on the network. The idea of pricing space in the UTXO set is proposed as a potential solution to determine whether certain transactions are spam or not. By considering resource costs, it is believed that this mechanism could discourage the usage of inscriptions. However, it is acknowledged that implementing such a mechanism may face resistance from some Bitcoin users.One of the main challenges discussed is preventing spam transactions known as "inscriptions" from reaching miners and being included in blocks. It is noted that even if a majority of the network upgrades their relay policy to prevent inscription propagation, users can still send non-standard transactions directly to miners or modify the script used for inscriptions. Thus, economic means are suggested as the most effective solution, rather than relying solely on technical measures. Appeals to Satoshi Nakamoto are deemed unconvincing in this regard.The discussion also highlights the difficulties associated with using pre-segwit nodes and emphasizes the need for a comprehensive solution to address the growing UTXO set and initial blockchain download time. Simply blocking inscriptions in the mempool is seen as an inadequate solution that would lead to a never-ending chase of constantly blocking new spam methods.It is pointed out that inscriptions have been introduced in the Bitcoin community, even on chains without Taproot or Segwit. These inscriptions are primarily used to sell NFTs or Tokens, concepts that have been rejected by the community. However, miners and nodes do not enforce these additional rules, resulting in valid Bitcoin transactions becoming invalid Ordinals transactions. This raises concerns about more serious underlying issues such as initial blockchain download (IBD) time and UTXO size.The introduction of inscriptions is seen as an acceptance of sidechains in a more centralized form, despite the initial rejection by the community. Soft-forks are considered unstoppable concepts, and inscription creators have implemented a version that is not enforced.
