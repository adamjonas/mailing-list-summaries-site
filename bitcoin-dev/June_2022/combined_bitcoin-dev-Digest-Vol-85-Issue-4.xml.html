Summary:

Luke Kenneth Casson Leighton suggests that trust metrics can be a potential solution to address misinformation, which is being systematically deployed to undermine Bitcoin. Trust metrics are basically a graph of who trusts whom, allowing quantification of who's trusted among a particular set or subset of people. This mechanism helps to measure consensus and could significantly benefit the Bitcoin ecosystem. While trust metrics can be done separately from Bitcoin, it can be used to answer questions about Bitcoin. AliceXBT suggests that Trust Metrics could be a potential solution to the problem of misinformation. She explains that public declarations of trust, and their propagation through standard Maximum-Flow Graph analysis, help greatly to filter out the crap. AliceXBT points out that the similarity between trust metric evaluation and the Bitcoin protocol is remarkable and she is stunned that it was not added right from the start.The concept of consensus involves trust, and this trust is measured using a mechanism called Trust Metric. The Ford Fulkerson Max Flow Algorithm is used to convert an unordered graph of "Trust Declarations" into a Directed Acyclic Graph with weighting to limit long-distance Transitive Chains from being faulty. Sybil resistance is achieved by linking pseudo-identities to someone who is linked to the "seeds" and requiring a minimum number of incoming Trust Declarations before flow is permitted to cross outwards. However, attackers can still pose as trustworthy individuals and mark themselves as trusting various sock puppets.Moreover, there was a discussion about whether developers are being paid enough in BTC to afford making a Declaration. To address this, the idea was proposed to separate the Declaration from the payment threshold so that anyone can enter a zero-value transaction with a Declaration of Trust. However, independent post-processing would determine whether their Declaration would be included in the Graph Processing. A rule was suggested that if a developer has 500 incoming Declarations of Trust and is only one degree away from the "seeds," charging them for making Declarations would be unnecessary.Overall, adding trust metrics evaluation built-in to the Bitcoin protocol can help developers evaluate whom to trust when it comes to protocol development. It is important to note that while there are challenges and potential vulnerabilities with implementing trust metrics, alternative approaches can be explored to address these issues.
