Summary:

The article proposes the idea of introducing sidechains without any forks using transaction joining and homomorphic encryption. The process involves creating a new chain with zero coins and introducing coins from other chains by signing transactions. Coins can be withdrawn by publishing the transaction from the original chain, which can be copy-pasted to the new chain and used to destroy previously produced coins. Transaction joining enables sidechains by transforming a chain of transactions into one transaction. Homomorphic encryption such as Pedersen commitments / ElGamal commitments can be used to add new features without touching consensus.The hash of the chain can also be added to the signature inside a Bitcoin transaction, preventing overwriting history. Merged mining is suggested as a way to reward sidechain miners. MimbleWimble coins, like Grin, work by encrypting the amount using homomorphic encryption. The article suggests that this approach could be used to introduce sidechains in a no-fork way. Overall, the article explores various ways of introducing sidechains without any forks while ensuring the security of the network.
