The context is about the testing strategy for Bitcoin development, which is discussed in a series of email conversations, forum posts, and messages. The team emphasizes the importance of comprehensive and unified testing strategies and the need for proper tools and structure for quality assurance (QA). They discuss the use of automated tests, third-party software, bug reporting systems, regression testing and creating detailed test cases and plans. Steve shares his experience with test-driven development and requirements management and is looking for help or reviews from interested parties. There are also discussions around the creation of a Bitcoin testing project, proposals to create a page on bitcoin.org linking to developer resources, and the importance of having detailed test cases and plans. In addition, the team uses Jenkins to run test scripts after every new commit and emphasizes the need for Windows and Mac testing. They suggest unifying all build testing and test cases into one place and setting up a workflow where people can sponsor or donate to specific campaigns for testing various features. The lack of code coverage from current tests is concerning, and the team acknowledges that more needs to be done in this area. Some community members express interest in getting involved in QA work and propose mailing lists for Bitcoin testers. However, resistance to changing established processes is acknowledged, but the benefits of setting up requirements, functionality, and tests with traceability from the start are emphasized.Steve expresses his willingness to work for free and suggests that people should be compensated for writing test cases, executing them, passing or failing them in that order. He also talks about the confusing time and how he doesn't know if he is doing right from wrong, trying to lead without leading and feels like he is wasting people's time. In response to Steve's question about the meaning of the word "bless," Peter Vessenes sends a detailed and professional response that clarifies his confusion. Overall, the conversations highlight the importance of proper testing and QA for Bitcoin development and suggest various methods and tools for achieving it. The team acknowledges the need for more comprehensive documentation and code coverage and the importance of laying proper foundations for testing and selecting the right tools for the job. They also emphasize the need for a QA team to ensure that software runs on all platforms before release.
