Summary:

The conversation on bitcoin-dev mailing list involves a discussion about the consensus rule, CLTV deployment and hard forks. The main concern is that any effort to create gridlock for political reasons will not be successful. There have been disputes among regular contributors in the past but the standard for a hard fork is different. Soft forks can happen at any time at the whim of miners and are categorically different from hard forks. The space for soft-forks that Bitcoin Core contributors would consider is tiny and they cannot meaningfully undermine the properties provided by the rules enforced within the software. Finally, the behavior of the technology arising from the inherent compatibility radically lowers the cost of deployment, making them different. Even if someone is trying to disrupt the process and nothing else, it can help learn by acting as an adversary that causes extending the minds and understanding.
