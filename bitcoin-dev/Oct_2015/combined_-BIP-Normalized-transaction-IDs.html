Summary:

The issue of malleability in Bitcoin transactions has been a topic of discussion among members of the Bitcoin-dev mailing list. A variety of proposals have been suggested to address this issue, including using canonical encoding, normalized transaction IDs, and empty scriptsigs. However, there are concerns about their effectiveness and implementation costs.One proposed solution involves specifying flags upfront in UTXO-creating transactions to allow for fully malleability-proof wallets. But Luke Dashjr raises concerns about signer malleability and suggests that wallets should actively CoinJoin, bump fees on, and manage pending transactions to address this issue.The discussions also touch on topics such as double-spending, off-blockchain protocols, and segregated witnesses. Segregated Witnesses are suggested as a solution to signature malleability, but there is confusion around the term "malleability" and what it specifically refers to. Christian Decker proposed a new implementation for normalized transaction IDs, which involves stripping signatures before hashing the transaction to prevent modifications while still remaining valid. However, there were concerns raised about the effectiveness of these solutions and the associated costs involved.Overall, the conversations highlight the complexity of addressing malleability in Bitcoin transactions and the need for continued collaboration and innovation. While solutions have been proposed, there are still concerns about their effectiveness and implementation costs. The issue of malleability remains an important one that requires ongoing attention and discussion.
