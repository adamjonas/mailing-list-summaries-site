Summary:

In 2015, Bitcoin Core's lead maintainer, Wladimir J. van der Laan proposed a schedule for the development of the next major release, version 0.12.0. The proposed schedule included opening Transifex translations for 0.12 on November 1st, a feature and translation string freeze on December 1st, and a split off of the `0.12` branch from `master` in January, with the aim to release 0.12.0 final on February 1st, 2016.However, concerns were raised by members of the community regarding the consensus freeze and its coupling with major releases. Luke Dashjr questioned the absence of a "Consensus freeze" and suggested postponing it until after the HK workshop in case a hardfork is decided on. Van der Laan acknowledged the benefits of decoupling consensus changes from major releases but did not want to postpone 0.12 for the reasons mentioned by Dashjr. Van der Laan explained that the versioning of Bitcoin software does not follow the Semantic Versioning (SemVer) specification and that only critical bug fixes could be made to 0.12. The discussions shed light on the technical aspects of Bitcoin development and the importance of adhering to industry standards.The proposed schedule for the next major release, 0.12.0, was set for the end of 2015. The team planned to open Transifex translations for 0.12 on November 1st, followed by a soft translation string freeze with no large or unnecessary changes. On December 1st, there would be a feature and translation string freeze. In January, the team would split off the `0.12` branch from `master`, start the RC cycle, tag and release `0.12.0rc1`, and begin merging for 0.13 on the master branch. The final release of version 0.12.0 was expected to occur on February 1st.On September 30, 2015, Luke Dashjr started a discussion on the Bitcoin-dev mailing list regarding the consensus freeze for the upcoming Bitcoin Core release. He questioned the absence of a consensus freeze and suggested that it should be postponed until after the HK workshop in case a hardfork is decided on. He also asked whether the consensus freeze has been entirely de-coupled from the release process since old versions need an update too.In general, there is a consensus to decouple from Bitcoin Core releases. However, the discussion did not provide any specific information on the outcome or decision regarding the consensus freeze.
