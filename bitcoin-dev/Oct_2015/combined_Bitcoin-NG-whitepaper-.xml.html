Summary:

Bitcoin-NG is a new technique that aims to address scalability issues faced by Bitcoin without impacting its open architecture or trust model. The core technique involves electing a 'leader' who vets future transactions every 10 minutes, which can run as fast as the network allows. Microblocks contain ledger entries and a header but do not affect the weight of the chain as they do not contain proof of work.Bitcoin-NG is compatible with both Bitcoin and Blockstream-like sidechains and is seen as complementary to both technologies. It has benefits such as fast confirmations that are stronger than 0-conf in Core and come much more quickly than Core's 1-confirmations, resilience to fluctuations in mining power, and healthier incentives for participants to ferry transactions.However, there are concerns about how information will be managed and protected in the Bitcoin-NG scheme. There is also a potential issue with double spending in microblocks within the NG protocol. The confirmation time for microblocks may not need to be a full key-block but should be a reasonable period after which an attacker would lose more fees than the value of their double-spend. A proposal for fixing problems associated with double spending by taking reward compensation back by fraud proofs is not seen as sufficient, and fidelity-bonded ledgers are recommended instead.Moreover, there is a discussion on the use of microblocks in Bitcoin, with Matt Corallo raising the issue that there is no way to punish people in case of double spend in a microblock that happens in a key block which reorganizes away the first transaction. Greg Slepak also raised a point on Twitter that users could no longer pick transactions in a mining pool set up in such a way that they can commit fraud at a much lower cost than the block's total reward.Despite these concerns, the creators of Bitcoin-NG hope that their work will advance the state of blockchains and inspire even better solutions. Feedback from the community is welcome to improve the system.
