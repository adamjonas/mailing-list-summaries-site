Summary:

On January 30, 2018, an email was forwarded to the bitcoin-dev mailing list regarding concerns over section 8.1.2 of the NIST 8202 Blockchain Technology Overview. The section covers Bitcoin Cash (BCH), which was developed as a result of a hard fork of the Bitcoin blockchain in 2017. Instead of implementing Segregated Witness (SegWit) changes, developers opted to increase the blocksize, allowing people to have access to the same amount of coins on both Bitcoin and Bitcoin Cash.In an email conversation between Damian Williamson and CANNON, Williamson suggested changing the wording from "Bitcoin Cash decided to increase the blocksize over implementing SegWit changes alone" to "Rather than being satisfied with the implementation of SegWit changes alone, the developers of Bitcoin Cash decided to also increase the blocksize." However, CANNON clarified that SegWit offers many improvements unrelated to scaling, such as advanced transaction trees, enhanced smart contracts, trustless mixing protocols, micropayment networks, and increased signature security.Peter Todd pointed out that SegWit is primarily a blocksize increase rather than transaction size optimization. The message provides insights into the technical aspects of SegWit and its advantages, including enabling the Lightning Network upgrade to Bitcoin, which allows for instant and cheap transactions while freeing up space on the blockchain. It contrasts the vision of the "bitcoin" crowd versus the "bitcoin cash" crowd with respect to scaling approaches, highlighting micropayment channels and networks as the preferred approach by the former and blocksize increases by the latter.Furthermore, the author of a message commented on Draft NISTIR 8202 Blockchain Technology Overview, stating that the information presented in the paper is technologically invalid and contains false information. The section regarding "8.1.2 Bitcoin Cash (BCC)" was corrected with four points: 1) Bitcoin Cash uses the ticker BCH, not BCC; 2) Segwit was a softfork, not a hardfork; 3) the original bitcoin blockchain is still called Bitcoin, not Bitcoin Cash; and 4) technically, Bitcoin Cash was forked from the original Bitcoin blockchain to create the altcoin "Bitcoin Cash" (BCH), so it is not the original chain but a forked one.In addition, Dylan Yaga of the Federal government responded to feedback on section 8.1.2 of NIST 8202 Blockchain Technology Overview, which discusses Bitcoin Cash (BCH). While Bitcoin adopted an improvement proposal for Segregated Witness (SegWit) through a soft fork, developers of Bitcoin Cash decided to increase the blocksize instead. This led to a group of users having access to the same amount of coins on Bitcoin and Bitcoin Cash after the hard fork occurred. The revised section was sent to reviewers for further comment to promote transparency.Overall, these conversations and comments provide insights into the technical aspects of SegWit and its advantages, as well as highlighting the different approaches to scaling between the "bitcoin" and "bitcoin cash" crowds.
