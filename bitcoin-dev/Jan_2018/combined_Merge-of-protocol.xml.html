Summary:

In 2017, a discussion arose about the feasibility of merging two blockchain protocols to create a resulting coin that combined the strengths of both. While it is possible for communities behind two coins to merge, the process is difficult and risky, requiring agreement from each coin's community as well as between the two communities. The merge block would have two parents and would represent a hard fork on both chains. With the development of Lightning Network, many altcoins began considering merging with Bitcoin. However, no concrete resources or ideas were provided in the initial post.
