Summary:

The email conversations revolve around the improvement of the Bitcoin payment protocol to make it more appealing to customers and increase adoption while maintaining explicit standards. Participants discuss various aspects of the protocol, including extensions such as discounts for using BTC and the use of a numeric memo field for structured data. Despite differing opinions, there is a shared goal of improving the functionality and usefulness of the Bitcoin payment protocol.One proposed feature in Bitcoin is to show users how much they are saving by using Bitcoin instead of credit cards or bank payments. The feature would require a minor BIP 70 extension that includes a field for recording the size of any discount provided by the merchant only because the user chose to pay with Bitcoin or other digital currency. While this could encourage more people to use Bitcoin as a payment method, concerns are raised about the misuse of such features by merchants and the need to keep things simple and generalized to prevent buggy and poorly standardized implementations.Jeff Garzik suggests offering automatic cashback instead of discounts to prevent potential abuse of the system. In addition, there is a discussion on the importance of having explicit standards and avoiding overly complicated and edge-case-oriented protocols. There is also talk of creating a living document version of the payment protocol with all the different extensions folded into it to simplify programming tasks and avoid field number collisions.The conversation also explores the idea of using a numeric memo field as structured data for Bitcoin transactions. Some argue that if one cosmetic field is supported, then all fields should be supported with a generic structured data format. Others believe that the textual memo field is already sufficient and that any unnecessary features should be avoided. Jeff Garzik argues that there is nothing wrong with having a numeric memo field as it provides structured data rather than unstructured data.During the conversation, slush and another user discuss a new feature that claims to show how much money customers can save by using Bitcoin for payments. Both express concerns about the validity and potential abuse of such a feature. They propose that the memo field would be a better option to provide such data since it is a part of the payment protocol and could contain information about exchange rates without misleading customers.Finally, the conversation shifts to whether or not the payment protocol should contain information about exchange rates. The second user plans to draft a BIP for this next week, believing that the protocol should contain whatever is useful. The importance of keeping the payment protocol simple and explicit is emphasized throughout the discussions, with examples cited of over-engineered protocols that have not been widely adopted due to vague definitions or poor design.
