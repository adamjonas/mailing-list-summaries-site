Summary:

The email thread on bitcoin-dev discusses the issue of fee management in Bitcoin transactions. The loss in revenue from a higher sat/vb transaction replacing a lower one is minimal, and it would be economically rational for miners to trade off that immediate loss against the chance of a simpler policy encouraging higher adoption of bitcoin or more on-chain activity. The mempool accept policy for replacements could be improved to make reasoning about the mempool easier, but it is important to address issues such as pinning attacks and network cost before making changes to the current system. This proposed design principle seeks to minimize bandwidth and chain space consumed by a fee-bump. The proposed approach allows feerates to be bumped after a transaction's broadcast, regardless of the structure of the original transaction. However, concerns were raised regarding the possibility of pinning-like attacks and the need for a soft-fork. Despite these concerns, the author believes that the current complexity in fee management is untenable, and significant practical benefit justifies considering a soft-fork as a one-time cost.
