Summary:

The Bitcoin-dev mailing list has been discussing a new idea called 'path coin'. The proposal suggests achieving a fully trustless transfer of coins between parties using signature adaptors and CTV or similar covenants. However, the limitations are extreme, and the idea is not yet viable. The hope is that imaginative minds may be able to develop it further.The discussion revolves around potential solutions for creating a trustless contract enforcement mechanism. Some members suggest burn mechanisms, while others propose penalties as disincentives. For instance, Lightning is cited as having a stable model as long as the penalty payment is larger than the amount stolen. However, there are concerns about stability due to game theory, bribery, and collusion.PathCoin is also discussed in the email conversation. It uses penalties consisting of a payment directly from the attacker to the attacked. This method is considered stable and has the potential for a fully trustless transfer of coins between parties. The conversation also includes discussions of the Sabu protocol and potential methods for jumping over hops in fixed paths. While these ideas may require further development, they open up the possibility for a fully trustless transfer of coins and offer alternative tradeoffs against the 'digital cash' goal compared to statechains or payment pools.In summary, the Bitcoin-dev mailing list is exploring potential solutions to create a trustless contract enforcement mechanism.
