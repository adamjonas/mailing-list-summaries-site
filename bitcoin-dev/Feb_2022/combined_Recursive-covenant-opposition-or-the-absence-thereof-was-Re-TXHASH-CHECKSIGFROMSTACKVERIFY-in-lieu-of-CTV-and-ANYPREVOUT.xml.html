Discussions on the bitcoin-dev mailing list cover various topics related to Bitcoin, including sidechains, recursive covenants, and drivechains. In one thread, users discuss reducing the number of bytes needed for channel factories and sidechains. Another conversation explores whether sidechains can solve the problem of a less secure altcoin becoming dominant and the benefits and drawbacks of large block sidechains compared to other payment systems such as Visa and Lightning.Users also express differing opinions on the potential harm recursive covenants could cause to the fungibility of individual UTXOs. There is debate around whether drivechains are a scaling solution or not, with some arguing that they are inefficient and only interested in scaling solutions. Additionally, there are discussions around creating encumbrances that require spends from a covenant to only be sent to a specific script involving an authority figure.The rejection of Drivechains through Turing completeness was also discussed, with concerns about sidechain validators and mainchain miners merging their businesses. The rejection was based on the potential for funds deposited in them to be stolen or adding a "block size increase" where every full node and miner needs to do more work when validating a block.The concept of recursive covenants is also explored, with debates surrounding the risks and benefits of adding more kinds of computation into Bitcoin script. Some suggest limiting operations in Bitcoin script to verification and encouraging general computation off-chain. While some argue that recursive covenants are not an issue, others express concern about unbounded recursion in combination with too much generality/flexibility in what types of conditions future UTXOs can be encumbered with based on the restriction of such covenants. In terms of onboarding new users to Bitcoin, there is a discussion on using channel factories for onboarding new members to the Lightning Network while ensuring fixed public keys required for channel opening are not known until after the channel has been confirmed on the blockchain. Channel factories were proposed as a solution that meets this requirement and has 0 bytes per block being published. However, the downside of using sidechains for onboarding is that onboarding rate needs to be as fast as the rate at which people want to join Bitcoin and any security sacrificed to get a higher onboarding rate would be needless for extra capacity that cannot be utilized.Finally, the benefits and drawbacks of sidechains, channel factories, and altcoins are debated, with the consensus being that all chains should be in the same monetary unit to avoid interference from monetary network effects and ensure the decentralized chain always exists. The conversation also touches on the potential for a sidechain to dominate Bitcoin and the merits of having all chains, centralized and decentralized, in the same monetary unit.
