Summary:

This message is a tutorial by Jeremy Rubin on how to use the Sapio CLI to generate contracts and play with them on the network using a congestion control tree. The tutorial provides steps for installation of JQ (json manipulating tool) and setting up a node as described above. The attendees are requested to build a branch from https://github.com/JeremyRubin/bitcoin/tree/checktemplateverify-signet-23.0-alpha and connect to [signet] signetchallenge=512102946e8ba8eca597194e7ed90377d9bbebc5d17a9609ab3e35e706612ee882759351ae and addnode=50.18.75.225. Moreover, they should skim through https://learn.sapio-lang.org and download/build the sapio cli & plugin examples ahead of the meeting. The team will then try to get everyone building and sending a basic application such as congestion control tree or vault on the signet. Attendees can also load up the Sapio Studio and try it through the GUI, modify the congestion control tree code and recompile it, and try out other contracts.At the upcoming fourth CTV meeting, the first topic of discussion will be the objective of Signet and the decision function of observations from a test network. The second topic will be connecting to CTV Signet Tutorial, ensuring that everyone who wants to be part of it can join and debug any issues. The third topic will be receiving coins/sending coins, where there is a faucet for this signet at https://faucet.ctvsignet.com and an explorer at https://explorer.ctvsignet.com. Finally, the meeting will end with general discussion for 30 minutes.
