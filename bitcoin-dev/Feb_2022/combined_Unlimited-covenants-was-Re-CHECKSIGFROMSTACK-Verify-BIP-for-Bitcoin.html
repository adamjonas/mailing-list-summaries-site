Summary:

The bitcoin-dev mailing list is currently discussing the issue of enabling covenants in Bitcoin Script. Some argue for total Turing-completeness, while others suggest banning partial Turing-completeness. The ongoing discussion focuses on the potential risks of recursive covenants and the implications of allowing total Turing-completeness. Jeremy Rubin had previously warned about the potential dangers of evil multisig covenants back in 2015. Concerns have been raised about the potential implications of unbounded covenants. However, as research is conducted on what covenants can achieve, arguments against them lose weight.Multisig alone can enable recursive covenants, which are being used in permissioned systems like Liquid. The possibility of adding `OP_CHECKSIGFROMSTACK` opcode has been generally supported, but would require an update to BIP340 and addition to sigops budget. ZmnSCPxj, a Bitcoin developer, recently discussed the feasibility and risks of enabling covenants in Bitcoin Script. ZmnSCPxj previously proposed using CHECKSIG(FROMSTACK) to simulate recursive covenants, but acknowledged that state could be sneaked into UTXOs or sibling outputs to create them. Altcoins already exist with worse implementations than what is being proposed for Bitcoin, and they would run on Turing machines anyway, making full EVM support possible.ZmnSCPxj argues that "inescapable" covenants, which require the output to be paid to the same covenant, are equivalent to a `while (true);` loop and can only be created with Turing machines. However, covenants could encode an upper bound on the number of recursions, preventing full Turing-completeness while still allowing for a large number of use-cases. The discussion also focuses on creating a useful recursive covenant by modifying the scriptPubkey through sighash introspection, but this requires hashing and tweaking that cannot be performed with existing opcodes. Recursive covenants could create unexpected problems, and the post discusses the issue of permitting recursion at the transaction level and the arguments against it from a safety standpoint.Multisig-based covenants should be considered, and implementing an upper bound on the number of recursions could prevent full Turing-completeness while still enabling a wide range of use-cases. Overall, the discussion highlights the need for caution and careful consideration when implementing covenants in Bitcoin Script.
