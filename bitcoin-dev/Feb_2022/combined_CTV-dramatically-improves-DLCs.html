Summary:

A new method for improving the performance of Discreet Log Contracts (DLCs) has been proposed. This method involves using OP_CHECKTEMPLATEVERIFY (OP_CTV) instead of MuSig2 adaptor signatures, which reduces the time taken from hundreds of microseconds to around 5 per party. The use of script also makes it easier to do a threshold along with the script, further reducing computational and communication complexity. In addition to this, the proposal suggests getting rid of the attestation point multiplication. A new alternative method is suggested to replace hashing in the attestation point computation, which helps remove the EC multiplications.By using OP_CTV in DLCs, performance can be improved by around 30x compared to using MuSig2 adaptor signatures in the worst case scenario. Depending on the kind of event, removing the attestation point multiplication will also help. Communication complexity also becomes constant. However, it should be noted that in the un-cooperative case, the size of the witness is bigger and the transaction is distinguishable from other protocols. The use of CTV in DLCs enables non-interactive asynchronously third-party unilaterally creatable contracts, making multi-party DLCs easier to execute. Additionally, CTV allows for a "trustless timeout" branch and enables the creation of pay-to-DLC addresses. There are also opportunities for composing DLCs in interesting ways, such as options over DLCs, perpetual-like contracts, and iterative DLCs traded over a fixed set of attestation points with variable payout curves. Jeremy Rubin proposes a way to make the close portion of a DLC somewhat secure without exposing oracles on-chain at all. The discussion also highlighted the effectiveness of using CSFS for privacy benefits and the impact of a large taproot tree on transaction fees. Finally, the performance of a DLC with respect to its CIT points and CTV hashes was discussed, and it was suggested that although there may be some issues with overall performance, users always have ten minutes to confirm the DLC. Overall, the discussion highlights the potential benefits and uses of CTV DLCs and their significant improvement in performance over the current approach. Despite some potential limitations, the proposed improvements offer significant advantages in terms of speed and efficiency for those using DLCs.
