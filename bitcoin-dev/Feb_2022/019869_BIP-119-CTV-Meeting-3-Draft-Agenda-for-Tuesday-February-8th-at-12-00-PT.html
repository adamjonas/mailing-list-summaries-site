Summary:

On February 8th, the third instance of a recurring meeting for Bitcoin developers will be held in channel ##ctv-bip-review on libera.chat IRC server. The topics proposed to be discussed include Bug Bounty Updates, Non-Interactive Lightning Channels, CTV's "Dramatic" Improvement of DLCs, PathCoin, OP_TXHASH, Emulating CTV for Liquid, and General Discussion. The meeting is expected to take approximately two hours. There have been no requests to add content to the agenda. Jeremy Rubin, who sent the email, encourages feedback, proposed topic changes, additions, or questions to be pre-registered on the agenda. He also promises to send a reminder with a finalized agenda in advance of the meeting. The Non-Interactive Lightning Channels topic will include discussions on https://rubin.io/bitcoin/2021/12/11/advent-14/ and https://utxos.org/uses/non-interactive-channels/. Meanwhile, the CTV's "Dramatic" Improvement of DLCs section will cover https://zensored.substack.com/p/supercharging-dlcs-with-ctv, https://lists.linuxfoundation.org/pipermail/bitcoin-dev/2022-January/019808.html, and https://rubin.io/bitcoin/2021/12/20/advent-23/. Furthermore, the PathCoin section discusses a proposal of coins that can be transferred in an offline manner by pre-compiling chains of transfers cleverly. It includes details from https://gist.github.com/AdamISZ/b462838cbc8cc06aae0c15610502e4da and https://lists.linuxfoundation.org/pipermail/bitcoin-dev/2022-January/019809.html.The OP_TXHASH section talks about an alternative approach to OP_CTV + APO's functionality by programmable tx hash opcode, with discussions available at https://lists.linuxfoundation.org/pipermail/bitcoin-dev/2022-January/019813.html. Finally, the Emulating CTV for Liquid topic is covered in https://lists.linuxfoundation.org/pipermail/bitcoin-dev/2022-February/019851.html. The meeting will conclude with 15 minutes of General Discussion.
