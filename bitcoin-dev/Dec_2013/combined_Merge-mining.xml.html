Summary:

The importance of merged mining for the security of altcoins is a topic of discussion in the context. The author argues that supporting merged mining can make it less straightforward for an individual or group to carry out a 51% attack on these coins. A 51% attack is when an attacker gains control of the majority of the hashing power in a network, allowing them to manipulate the blockchain and potentially double-spend coins. David Vorick, a prominent figure in the cryptocurrency community, notes that attacking a significant currency would not be beneficial as it would be expensive and traumatize the whole system. He suggests that if any large currency were to be attacked, it would probably be litecoin miners attacking dogecoin. However, he argues that such an attacker would likely have a vested interest in cryptocurrencies doing well. In response to Vorick's comments, Jorge Timón disagrees with his assumptions about the interests of a potential attacker and questions his stance on merged mining and whether or not it applies to Dogecoin. Vorick had previously stated that compromising even a currency like Dogecoin could cause questions to be raised, drop the value of Bitcoin, and traumatize the entire cryptocurrency system. He also addressed the use of scrypt in Dogecoin, which is a popular algorithm among scamcoins.Despite concerns about potential attacks, the author concludes that the rational decision for a non-scam altcoin is to take advantage of merged mining to maximize security. It is unclear from Vorick's comment about his stance on merged mining and whether or not it applies to Dogecoin.
