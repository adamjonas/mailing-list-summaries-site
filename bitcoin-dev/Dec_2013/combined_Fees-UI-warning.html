The issue of Bitcoin wallet security has been a topic of discussion among the community since December 2013. Suggestions have been made to improve security by using deterministic wallets, making regular backups optional, and replacing importing private keys with a "sweep" function.One possible solution is implementing a confirmation box for unusually high fees triggered if the fee amount is over ten times the default fee or greater than a certain percentage of the sending amount. Recent stories about people accidentally paying huge fees have been reported, leading to discussions around the need for such a feature.MultiBit's developer, Jim Burton, explains that one of the reasons people lose their Bitcoins is because they don't understand change and how it works. To help prevent this from happening, MultiBit hides the ability to delete wallets and always sends change to a separate address. These changes will be implemented in the HD version of MultiBit.Drak suggested that wallet authors should add a confirmation box when the fee amount seems unusually high. Pieter Wuille and Jim are discussing the possibility of no longer reusing change addresses for Bitcoin transactions.Hive Wallet is focused on providing users with a great user experience by constantly thinking about how to make their platform more user-friendly. They prioritize removing extraneous UI and functionality that can be automated. In the case of fees, developers should consider what their application tries to accomplish and how it can do so without getting in the way too much. Lastly, Hive Wallet is considering an approach once floating fees are feasible.There has been a focus on preventing accidental high fees paid by users when sending transactions. However, the danger of badly designed software that looks appealing remains an ongoing concern, and suggestions have been made to warn against certain services and prioritize UX design decisions.MultiBit developers are taking steps to prevent funds being lost, such as hiding the ability to delete wallets and always sending change to a separate address. They are also removing the import of individual private keys in the HD version of MultiBit and considering a potential ban on the reuse of change addresses for safety reasons.
