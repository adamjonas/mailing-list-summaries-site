Summary:

In an email exchange among members of the Bitcoin community, a member named Jeremy expresses concerns about arbitrary covenants and the reluctance of the broader community to embrace them. However, Dave argues that limiting Bitcoin's flexibility to avoid problems associated with covenants is unnecessary when unlimited covenants could offer interesting and useful features. Furthermore, Dave refutes concerns raised in a recent post by Shinobi regarding covenants, stating that they are already present in altcoins and recursive covenants may not necessarily make those problems worse.ZmnSCPxj responds to Jeremy's concerns and emphasizes the importance of respecting the concerns of others without lobotomizing useful tools. ZmnSCPxj further argues that an "inescapable" covenant is equivalent to a `while (true);` loop, making covenants Turing-complete. He suggests encoding an upper bound on the number of recursions to prevent full Turing-completeness while allowing for a large number of use-cases.
