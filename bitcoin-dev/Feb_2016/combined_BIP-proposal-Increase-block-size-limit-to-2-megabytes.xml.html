The proposed hard fork to increase Bitcoin's block size limit has sparked a controversial topic among the Bitcoin community. Discussions on the bitcoin-dev mailing list have covered various issues related to the upgrade, including the 28-day grace period for implementing the upgrade, security considerations, and compatibility with different wallets and clients.While some argue that a capacity increase is urgently needed, others raise concerns about the impact on decentralization and the percentage of the economy dependent on trusting full nodes. There are also debates around the threshold for a block size increase in Bitcoin, with arguments for requiring 75% or 95% of miners to signal their support. The issue of custodians who fail to produce coins on both sides of a fork was also raised, leading to debate over whether they should support the obsolete chain or not.Despite disagreements over certain issues, Bitcoin Classic and Bitcoin XT have implemented an increase in the block size limit to 2,000,000 bytes. However, discussions on the bitcoin-dev mailing list have continued, covering technical aspects of the proposed upgrade, including sigop counting and signature hashing limits, as well as security considerations, testing, rollback plans, and how security would be monitored and managed through the hard-fork.There were concerns about the short grace period and safety of deployment, as well as potential market disruption if miners trigger the hardfork without consent from the economy. In response to the proposed hard fork, some commenters argued that the issue was not whether or not it was a good idea to roll out a hard fork but rather how to do safe hard fork deployment and what the technological requirements are for doing so.Finally, there were discussions around security considerations for hard-forks and the need for custodians to produce coins on both sides of a fork in response to depositor requests. The email conversations discuss the potential risks and uncertainties of a proposed hard fork for Bitcoin and the impact on hash power.
