Summary:

The Bitcoin community has come together to agree on a vision of roughly 2MB of transactions per block, either via Segregated Witness or a hard fork. The technical community is now working towards a safe hard fork and resetting the past aggressions to resolve the contention seen in the community.A proposed outline for a hard fork in the context of segwit includes changing the segregated witness discount from 75% to 50%, setting the block size limit to 1.5MB, and allowing the first four bytes of the previous block hash field to contain any value. Additionally, discussion has taken place around reducing required logic in Bitcoin mining hardware to make it easier to produce competitive hardware.Bitcoin developers are discussing the possibility of a hard fork in response to two major challenges facing Bitcoin today: mining centralization and privacy. The proposed changes involve increasing the extranonce space for ASICs, which could simplify mining control and move complexity back into Bitcoin Core, and adding small increases in decentralization pressure where possible.In a Bitcoin development email thread, Matt Corallo proposed a "somewhat specific" outline for a hard fork in the context of segwit. The proposal aims to decrease the worst-case scenario while keeping the total size in-line with what the network could handle. The cost to validate and prevent significant blowups is also addressed, with transactions being allowed up to 20 non-segwit inputs and MAX_BLOCK_SIGOPS potentially becoming a per-transaction limit. Additionally, the first four bytes of the previous block hash field may contain any value instead of requiring them to be 0s, which would make it easier to produce competitive hardware. Alternatives such as using leading non-zero bytes of the prevhash as additional nonce were also discussed.Various proposals have been made such as limiting scriptPubKeys to 100 bytes in size, restricting scriptSigs to be push-only, and allowing the first four bytes of the previous block hash field to contain any value. However, some changes are incompatible with Luke-Jr's soft-hardfork approach. The limitation on scriptPubKeys to 100 bytes and the prohibition of OP_CODESEPARATOR in them, along with push-only scriptSigs, have also raised concerns regarding native multisig.The Bitcoin community also discusses increasing mining decentralization and privacy while addressing rising transaction fees. The proposed changes aim to make it easier to produce competitive hardware and reduce the number of hard forks visible to lightweight clients. It is suggested that a hard fork should only be considered in response to a major crisis that all participants can agree on, and could potentially be used to enforce decentralized mining pools to address the issue of mining centralization. The proposal aims to balance the need for efficient use of block space with the desire to keep fees low and maintain the security of the network. There have been concerns raised about the limitations placed on non-segwit inputs, as well as the restrictions on scriptPubKeys and push-only scriptSigs, which could disrupt services and invalidate hash time locked transactions.
