Summary:

On February 1, 2016, Luke Dashjr proposed a draft of a Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) that aimed to provide clarity on the Status field for BIPs. The proposal also introduced the ability for public comments as well as expanding the list of allowable BIP licenses. However, Gavin Andresen expressed his concerns regarding the definition of "consensus" described in the proposal, stating that it gives too much centralized control to whoever controls the mailing list and wiki. In various email exchanges and discussions, Bitcoin Improvement Proposals (BIPs) have been reviewed and clarified by Luke Dashjr and others. The focus has been on defining consensus in the BIP process and avoiding confusion between different concepts related to it. Suggestions have been made to find alternative terms for "consensus" and to clarify the criteria for updating the status of BIPs.The author of BIP, Luke Dashjr, has developed a draft that provides clarification on the status field for BIPs, adds the ability for public comments, and expands the list of allowable BIP licenses. Furthermore, there is a discussion about whether dump.bitcoin.it is sufficient public record of contentious moderation or user cross-comment editing. There is confusion between different concepts related to the term "consensus" used in the BIP document. Therefore, Jorge Timón suggested finding another term for "consensus" in the BIP document. Dave Scotese suggests that rules and code define what Bitcoin is, but the process requires consensus and it can come after the release of Bitcoin to the public. Suggestions are welcome to make the concept of "adoption consensus" completely clear. Luke-jr has also proposed changing the term "consensus" used in the BIP document to avoid confusion with other related terms such as "consensus rules" and "consensus code." The goal is to avoid overloading the term "consensus" for two distinct yet related concepts. In a discussion about the BIP, it was agreed that only one application would be standard to avoid confusion in usability, while multiple applications could benefit from coordination encouraged by the BIP and BIP 123. The focus was on accessibility and discussion, with an emphasis on avoiding bias and censorship in moderation decisions.Comments on BIPs should be solicited on the bitcoin-dev mailing list and summarized fairly in the wiki with time given for suggesting edits on the mailing list. Wiki registration and monitoring should not be a required hurdle to participation. It was suggested that any forum used for review should have indisputable records of moderation and user edits.Luke Dashjr made changes to the BIP 2 to create less hard feelings during comments without excessive policy structure. The revised BIP aims to replace references to the mailing list with a "public discussion medium," such as forums or conferences. In a conversation between Ryan Grant and Luke Dashjr, they discussed the process for changing the status of a BIP from Draft to Active when rough consensus is reached on the mailing list. Luke suggested that any forum used for review should have indisputable records of moderation and user edits.Luke-jr plans to open discussion on making this BIP an Active status along with BIP 123 after initial revisions have been made. He requests any objections to be shared now so that he can address them and reach a consensus. The revised BIP can be found on GitHub and further reviews are welcome.
