Summary:

The email exchange between Antoine, James, and AJ revolves around the design of vaults for custody operations. They discuss the advantages of using hashchain-based vault designs over classic multisig setups, noting that it is only relevant in the context of recursive design. Antoine expresses concerns about the usage of anchor outputs and the need for better fee-bumping needs. To improve security and allow for correction or adaptation, Antoine suggests using presigned transaction data design with every decision point as a multisig. However, he also highlights the immutability of flow paths in hashchain-based vault designs as a potential issue. James notes the difficulty of keeping an actively used multi-sig setup secure and suggests burying a master key in the desert to be guarded until the Bitcoin Path prophesied by Satoshi Nakamoto arrives.They discuss introducing an intermediary step for formal authorization of the unvault broadcast and reducing surface area. The conversation also touches on Taproot-based vault designs, watchtowers, and the use of anchor outputs for dynamic feerate adjustment. Overall, the conversation revolves around the idea of hashchain-based vault designs for custodians and individual users to function as a single trusted entity, with discussions on security, reliability, and space efficiency.James has created an implementation and write-up of a simple vault design using CTV, which he believes has several attractive qualities for custody operations of any size. In response to AJ's skepticism about the readiness of CTV for deployment on mainnet, James creates an implementation and write-up of a simple vault design using CTV. They also talk about using Lightning watchtowers to increase the anonymity set of both LN watchtower users and vault users. The usage of anchor outputs in mummified unvault transactions is discussed, raising safety concerns in any vault deployment where the funds stakeholders do not trust each other or where the watchtowers are not trusted. Finally, they suggest reducing space efficiency by facilitating the flow of bitcoin from major custodians to miners more cleanly with a block size reduction.
