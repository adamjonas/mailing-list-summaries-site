Summary:

The Bitcoin Improvement Proposals (BIPs) have been the subject of various email threads and mailing list discussions. One proposal suggested creating BIPs as wiki pages, with the author having the final say in case of any editing disputes. GitHub's Wiki feature was recommended as a suitable platform for this purpose. Some members advocated for discussing potential BIPs on either the current platform or the bitcoin-dev mailing list instead of creating a specialized mailing list for Bitcoin ideas.There were also discussions about ways to manage changes to the BIP repository on GitHub. One proposal suggested that the author or someone they delegate should be the primary contact for each BIP, responsible for commenting on changes and accepting or rejecting them. Another suggestion was to move the BIP discussion to a separate mailing list to reduce the workload on the bitcoin-development list. The importance of open communication and collaboration within the Bitcoin community was emphasized.Several individuals expressed their preference for open list software over Google Groups due to concerns of proprietary nature and Gmail lock-in. While SourceForge was suggested as an alternative, issues with DKIM signatures made it incompatible with Yahoo. Mailmanlist.net was recommended as a satisfactory option, and mail-archive.com was proposed as an alternative to lists.sf.net.Other topics discussed included creating separate repositories for similar proposals to keep them distinct from the core Bitcoin repository, using Discourse for the BIP page to make it look smoother and draw more input, and addressing the issue of low traffic in the Bitcoin mailing list. The community is actively exploring ways to improve the workflow and decision-making process regarding BIPs, while valuing open communication and collaboration to ensure effective peer review of proposals affecting Bitcoin.
