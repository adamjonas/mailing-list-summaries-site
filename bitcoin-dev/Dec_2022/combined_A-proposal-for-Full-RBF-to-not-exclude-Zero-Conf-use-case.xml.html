Summary:

The Bitcoin-dev mailing list has been discussing Full-RBF behavior and its potential impact on multi-party transactions such as coinjoins and multi-party lightning channels. There are concerns about the risks of double spending, but also the significant use case of 0-conf acceptance of transactions. Daniel Lipshitz from GAP600 suggested using First-seen-safe replace-by-fee (FSS-RBF) as a potential solution to balance FullRBF while retaining the important 0-conf use case. However, there are concerns about centralization and privacy in this ongoing discussion.GAP600 is a Bitcoin payment processing service that enables merchants to accept unconfirmed Bitcoin transactions through unique transaction hashes. The company claims to handle about 10% of all Bitcoin transactions, indicating a high degree of centralization. In November and December 2022, there were around 1.5 million and 1.7 million total queried unique Bitcoin addresses respectively, with a total value of around 220 million USD in November and 200 million USD in December. However, there are concerns about the privacy implications of collecting data on real-world entities paying for GAP600's service.There is ongoing debate about the need for full RBF, with some arguing that it should be implemented to increase fees and enable faster block acceptance if required. However, this could limit the significant use case of 0-conf acceptance of transactions. To balance FullRBF while retaining the 0-conf use case, FSS-RBF has been proposed as a potential solution. Some wallets, such as Electrum, may be affected by FullRBF, but OptinRBF already exists as an option with added benefits of continuing to support 0-conf.In email exchanges between Peter Todd and Daniel Lipshitz, concerns were raised about the limitations of FullRBF in multi-party transactions and the privacy implications of collecting data on real-world entities. Todd suggested implementing full-RBF to stop the collection of sensitive data, while Lipshitz clarified that GAP600 does not have access to KYC/AML information or telephone numbers of clients who use Bitcoin for deposit.Despite ongoing debate, 0-conf on Bitcoin is a long-standing and significant use case that should not be ignored. Its demise should be viewed as a major and unnecessary cost to FullRBF as currently implemented. FSS-RBF offers a potential solution to the issue of double spending while retaining the significant use case of 0-conf. However, concerns over centralization and privacy remain important considerations in this ongoing discussion.
