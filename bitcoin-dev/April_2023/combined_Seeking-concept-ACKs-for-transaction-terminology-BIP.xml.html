Summary:

Murch from bitcoin-dev has proposed the creation of an informational BIP that aims to establish a shared vocabulary for various components and aspects of transactions. The proposal is aimed at avoiding confusion due to the many competing terms in use, including contradictory ones such as `scriptPubKey`. The draft of the BIP can be found on GitHub, and Murch has asked for feedback from the community on whether they would be interested in the creation of such a BIP. The proposal does not aim for perfect consistency but rather a shared vocabulary among participants to facilitate conversations about transactions. Andrew Poelstra, Director of Research at Blockstream, expressed interest in such a BIP, leaving the work of refining the terminology to others.Josie, a participant in Bitcoin developer education programs, has suggested having a reference document early in the curriculum to reduce cognitive load and establish a shared terminology. Josie emphasizes the importance of the "synonyms" field as it helps map various resources to a common vocabulary. In response to a conversation about establishing a shared vocabulary for transactions, Murch drafts an informational BIP proposing terminology for various components and aspects of transactions. The proposal aims to establish a shared vocabulary to avoid confusion, acknowledging that there are already contradictory established terms.In a recent email thread on the Bitcoin development mailing list, Murch proposed the creation of a shared terminology document to avoid confusion during discussions about transactions. He noted that conversations often get sidetracked by establishing a shared vocabulary, and that there are many competing terms for transaction components. Establishing a shared terminology will help in minimizing miscommunication and speeding up the process of coming to social consensus on issues.Overall, the proposal seeks to establish a common terminology to avoid confusion among participants in Bitcoin-related conversations and transactions. It acknowledges the existence of contradictory terms and does not aim for perfect consistency, but rather a shared vocabulary to facilitate communication. Feedback has been requested from the community, and the proposal is available on GitHub.
