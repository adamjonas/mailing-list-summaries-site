Summary:

A group of researchers, including Nicholas Gregory and Ray Youssef, is working on a peer-to-peer market system called CivKit. The goal of CivKit is to facilitate censorship-resistant and permissionless global trading. The design of CivKit combines the Nostr architecture with Lightning onion routing infrastructure to ensure a high level of confidentiality for market participants. Market boards, which serve as Nostr relays with a Lightning gateway, operate autonomously and compete with each other.To address concerns raised by Antoine Riard, one of the researchers, front-running resistance has been highlighted as a fundamental property of a peer-to-peer marketplace. The desired level of front-running resistance will vary depending on the types of trades considered. Front-running risks can be mitigated through duplicate announcements of offers to multiple concurrent boards operated by independent identities. Latency anomalies can be monitored to disqualify misbehaving boards. Alternatively, the bulletin boards can operate as a federation, utilizing consensus algorithms such as Honey Badger BFT.The scoring of trades, counterparties, and service operators can be enabled through a Web-of-Stakes, which assigns economic weight based on the Bitcoin UTXO set. This reputation paradigm can be combined with decentralized identifiers and Web-of-Trust techniques to provide a comprehensive scoring system. An incentive framework for service operators is proposed, utilizing privacy-preserving credentials backed by Bitcoin payments to incentivize efficiency.The architecture of CivKit combines the Nostr architecture with Lightning Dev Kit and Nostr libraries. The researchers plan to release code and modules gradually, adhering to Bitcoin's open-source and decentralized standards. They are also open to collaboration with the community to standardize libraries and ensure interoperability between clients.Overall, the researchers aim to develop a robust and secure peer-to-peer marketplace that prioritizes front-running resistance, confidentiality, and censorship resistance. Their design incorporates various security paradigms, reputation systems, and incentive frameworks to create an efficient marketplace. Feedback and collaboration are encouraged.
