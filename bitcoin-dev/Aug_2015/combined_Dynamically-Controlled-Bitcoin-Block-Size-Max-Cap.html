Summary:

In the Bitcoin community, discussions have taken place on the maximum block size consensus rule and its impact on mining centralization. Proposed solutions include a fixed block size, dynamically controlling the maximum block size based on the percentage of blocks in the last difficulty period, using mean block size as a scalability metric, and implementing the Bitcoin Lightning Network for scalable off-chain instant payments. Milly Bitcoin suggested creating a standardized metric to compare decentralized systems.Tom Harding conducted an experiment that showed small miners were not affected by large blocks, but Peter Todd criticized the experiment design for not considering the scenario where a miner needs to get their blocks to at least 33% of hashing power. The email thread also discusses technical solutions to problems such as miners mining empty blocks and how to limit mining centralization. Overall, concrete plans and analysis are needed to address security issues in Bitcoin mining.Upal Chakraborty proposed an algorithmic solution to the problem of miners sabotaging block size increases by mining empty blocks. The proposal suggests that the max block size would be doubled if more than 50% of block size found in the first 2000 of the last difficulty period is more than 90% of the max block size, and halved if more than 90% of block size found in the same period is less than 50% of the max block size. There are concerns that miners could sabotage block size increases by mining empty blocks, and that the random distribution of transaction arrival and block times could lead to false signals. Some argue that the only reason for a maximum block size is to prevent spam while others claim it's to force people into paying higher fees.The discussions also highlight concerns about mining centralization and its relation to the maximum block size consensus rule. Members suggest working on a decentralized metric instead of making baseless claims. Additionally, the conversations propose different solutions and debate potential risks and concerns related to Bitcoin's block size controversy, revenue difference between small and large miners, attacks on miners, and concerns over the safety of minority hash power partitioning in Bitcoin. The author invites opinions on their proposals and provides contact information for further discussion.
