In August 2015, a discussion took place on the Bitcoin-dev mailing list regarding the maximum block size consensus rule and its impact on mining centralization. One member suggested working on a decentralization metric instead of making bald claims. Another member, who had tested a simulation tool built by Pieter, found that small miners were not disadvantaged by large blocks when only testing for hashrate centralization effects and not network partitioning effects.
