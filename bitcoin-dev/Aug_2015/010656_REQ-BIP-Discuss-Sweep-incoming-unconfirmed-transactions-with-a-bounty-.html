Summary:

Breadwallet, a Bitcoin wallet provider, has implemented the Child Pays for Parent (CPFP) feature when spending unconfirmed non-change inputs. The feature was released in May 2015 and is available on the company's Github page. Meanwhile, Dan Bryant via bitcoin-dev offered a bounty for anyone who can put a CPFP transaction creation feature into any wallet featured on the bitcoin homepage. The funds will be dispersed from a multisig address.
