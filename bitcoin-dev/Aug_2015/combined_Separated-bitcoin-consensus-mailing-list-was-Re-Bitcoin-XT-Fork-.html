Summary:

Bitcoin developers are discussing the need for a dedicated forum where technical issues can be discussed without any noise. They suggested having a moderated mailing list where only academic and technical matters are discussed, and offenders can be moderated out. The developers also suggested the need for a bitcoin-announce read-only list, where consumers of Bitcoin Core can subscribe to receive announcements about new versions of Bitcoin Core and any critical warnings.Several developers have been discussing ways to improve the signal-to-noise ratio on the bitcoin-dev list. One solution proposed was to split the list into multiple mailing lists. However, it was pointed out that a less technical Bitcoin discussion list already existed in the past but had not been used. Instead, the discussion turned towards instituting on-topic guidelines for bitcoin-dev. Btc Drak suggested offloading non-technical/academic debate to the Bitcoin list to reduce the off-topic noise of bitcoin-dev.The discussion revolves around creating a mailing list for general discussions about Bitcoin protocol and Bitcoin Core. The existing general discussion list has been inactive for a long time, leading to the idea of creating a new one. Some members suggest separating Bitcoin Core-specific development from general BIPs and consensus discussions, but concerns are raised about too much separation causing confusion.Jeff Garzik thinks that having multiple lists will not make much difference as long as Bitcoin Core remains the reference client. He also suggests that there haven't been any rules in place for -dev to force general discussion over to the Bitcoin list. Garzik believes that offloading non-technical/academic debates to the Bitcoin list could significantly reduce the off-topic/noise on the bitcoin-dev list.In August, Jorge Timón made the most number of posts with 72, followed by Hector Chu with 36 and Thomas Zander with 32. Pieter Wuille posted 27 times, while Eric Lombrozo and Mark Friedenbach each had 24 posts.Overall, there is agreement that a general discussion mailing list is necessary for those developing and maintaining Bitcoin protocol software or services relying on it. It is suggested that every mailing list should have its own internal SNR discussions. The potential benefits of creating a new mailing list for general discussions about Bitcoin protocol and Bitcoin Core are discussed, but concerns are raised about too much separation causing confusion.
