Summary:

Eric Lombrozo, a member of the Bitcoin development community, has proposed adding an extra attribute to BIPs indicating their "level" to help prioritize and reduce contention over disagreements. The proposed levels are consensus (hard/soft fork), peer services, RPC, implementations, and applications. Lombrozo believes that there are different degrees of "standard" and just because many people do something does not necessarily mean it is officially endorsed by other developers. He suggests modifying the current "type" attribute to reflect different degrees of "standard". Lombrozo posted an example of what such a table might look like on his website and invited comments and suggestions from others. A proposal draft has been posted and needs further fleshing out in the subsections. If others agree with this idea, a BIP draft will be worked on.
