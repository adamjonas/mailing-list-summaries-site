Summary:

Two workshops focused on Bitcoin's scalability and proposals to scale beyond its present limits are set to take place in Montreal and Hong Kong. The first event, taking place in September in Montreal, will focus on identifying the general tradeoffs and requirements of any proposal. The second workshop, scheduled for November-December in Hong Kong, will explore actual block size proposals. Both events will be live-streamed with remote participation facilitated via IRC for parallel online discussion and passing questions to the event. Additionally, there is an application process for independent or academic researchers to apply for travel assistance up to $1,000 per qualified presenter who intends to give a presentation. Proposals and expressions of interest will be accepted until 00.00 UTC Sept 1st 2015. Travel, venue details, and accommodation recommendations are available at scalingbitcoin.org.The workshops are designed to facilitate the existing Bitcoin Improvement Proposals (BIP) process and encourage deeper research and testing of existing proposals while motivating future work in this area. Sponsors of the workshops include Cryptsy, BitcoinTalk, Final Hash, Blockstream, MIT DCI, Chaincode Labs, IDEO Futures, Kraken, and Chain.com, with additional sponsors needed.Anyone with research relevant to issues surrounding Bitcoin scalability is welcome to submit a proposal for a presentation at the Montreal workshop. The goal is to explore proposals to scale Bitcoin beyond its current limitations while ensuring that various tradeoffs and requirements are taken into account.
