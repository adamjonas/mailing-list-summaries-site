Summary:

The Bitcoin community has been engaged in discussions about the block size limit's impact on the functioning of the fee market. One research paper shared on the Bitcoin-Dev mailing list is titled "A Transaction Fee Market Exists Without a Block Size Limit" by Peter R. The paper argues that a block size limit is not necessary to ensure a functioning fee market due to the orphaning cost. It presents the concept of block space supply and mempool demand curves to show how a rational Bitcoin miner should select transactions from their node's mempool when creating a new block to maximize profit in the absence of a block size limit. The paper also shows that an unhealthy fee market cannot exist since it requires communicating information at an arbitrarily fast rate.Another email exchange involves Adam Back proposing the idea of a prediction market in block-size, where Bitcoin.org could run the exchange and use profits to fund Core development. However, Back warns that there is no guarantee of a secure parameter set and that security is a continuous variable that must be balanced against other concerns. He cautions that if security is threatened due to gradual block size increases, users' concerns may result in a bearish market.In another discussion, Hector Chu proposed that miners should be able to vary the block size to maximize their profits. However, Greg Maxwell and Mark Friedenbach suggested the flexcap idea which can achieve similar effects without giving miners complete control. Giving miners control over block sizes could lead to cartelization, selfish mining, and other security issues.The context also discusses various email exchanges related to mining profitability with respect to block size and Shannon Entropy. The result is that an individual miner's profit per block is maximized at a finite block size Q* if Shannon Entropy is communicated during the block solution announcement. However, concerns are raised about the lower bound of expected revenue being surpassed, leading to unhealthy fee markets. Additionally, the possibility of block makers orphaning their own blocks is discussed, with Dave Hudson and Gavin Andresen disagreeing on whether it happens.The discussions warn researchers of the highly political and controversial nature of the debate around the block size limit. The author cautions that some developers may lack the necessary knowledge or experience beyond their Bitcoin expertise, while others may be more interested in drama-laced posts on Reddit. Finally, there are debates about the profitability advantage that miners with a larger percentage of the hash rate have and the possibility of centralization pressures always being present due to the monotonically increasing marginal profit curve of miner hashrate.
