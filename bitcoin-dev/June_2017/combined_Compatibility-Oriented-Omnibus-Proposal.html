Summary:

The Bitcoin community is currently facing multiple proposals and potential hard forks. A recent proposal, the Compatibility-Oriented Omnibus Proposal, seeks to fulfill the commitments of the Consensus 2017 Scaling Agreement by combining several proposals into a single patch set.The proposal suggests phasing in a gradual blocksize increase beyond the initial 2MB cap included in Luke-Jr's proposal and committing to it to ensure unity, prevent chain-split risks, and guarantee a successful SegWit activation followed by a hard fork. The proposal aims to activate Segregated Witness (SegWit) at an 80% threshold signaling at bit 4 while activating a 2MB hard fork within six months.The proposal includes protectionary measures to ensure the safest possible fork activation within the constraints of the requirements outlined in the Scaling Agreement. It also maintains full legacy consensus compatibility for users up until the HardForkHeight block height. However, it is unlikely that a safe hard fork can be coded and tested within six months.The proposal assumes that the signatories to the Consensus 2017 Scaling Agreement are genuinely committed to the terms of the agreement. In addition to the proposal, the context provides a link to a mailing list for Bitcoin developers hosted by the Linux Foundation, which serves as a platform for developers working on Bitcoin to discuss and collaborate on various issues related to the cryptocurrency. The email attachment that follows the link appears to be an HTML file, which may contain additional information or resources related to Bitcoin development.There is controversy brewing over the decision to include a secondary limit on the block size increase in the segwit + 2mb agreement, as it may not be what most users had in mind. One potential fork, a rapid 2MB+Segwit hard fork coin, has the potential to fragment the network if miners and exchanges collude to enact it and call it "Bitcoin" on major exchanges.Overall, the context suggests that the Bitcoin development community is active and engaged, with a public forum for discussion and collaboration hosted by a respected organization in the tech industry. The availability of such resources and platforms is likely to contribute to ongoing innovation and advancement in the field of cryptocurrency.The proposal aims to minimize the chain split risk and network disruption while maximizing backward compatibility, urging miners to upgrade their nodes to support segsignal and BIP148. There are concerns about LukeJr's 2MB cap not being compatible with the NY agreement, but some users suggest incorporating a gradual blocksize increase beyond the initial 2MB cap included in his proposal.In a Bitcoin-dev email thread discussing the Omnibus Proposal, some users expressed concerns that LukeJr's suggested 2MB cap is not compatible with the NY agreement. One user noted that with the current 1MB+SegWit, normal usage results in around 2 or 2.1MB blocks and that increasing the Base Size to 2000000 bytes and Total Weight to 8000000 bytes would result in a linear increase to ~4 or 4.2MB blocks. Another user suggested incorporating a gradual blocksize increase beyond the initial 2MB cap included in Luke-Jr's proposal, similar to BIP103, to satisfy the conditions of the Scaling Agreement while ensuring maximum safety, minimum code discrepancies.The author calls upon the Segwit2x team to consider and honor the Omnibus Proposal as the guiding approach to their development effort, believing that committing to this proposal will ensure unity and prevent costly fractures in the Bitcoin community. The proposal is unlikely to be merged into Bitcoin Core in the immediate future, but it is written with the intent to remain cleanly compatible with future network updates and changes.
