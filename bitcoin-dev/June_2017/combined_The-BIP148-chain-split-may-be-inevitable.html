Summary:

Bitcoin developer Jacob Eliosoff has provided an update on the likelihood of BIP91 avoiding a chain split. Although he believes that time is not as crucial as previously suggested, he warns that unless there is concrete progress with miner support, a split is inevitable. There have been some refinements noted to speed up the deployment of BIP91, such as reducing the 80% threshold to 65%, or starting to signal bit 1 when bit 4 reaches lock-in. However, it will still require significant convincing to get miners' support for any of these solutions to reach near 50%. The deadline for BIP148 is already deployed and unlikely to be postponed. Thus, it will start orphaning non-segwit blocks on August 1st. To avoid the split, more than 50% of hash power needs to be from BIP91 miners, signaling bit 1 and orphaning non-BIP91 by June 30th. The author believes that 80% of hashrate must run BIP91 by June 30th to lock in by July 13 and activate by July 27. If SegWit is locked in by August 1st with or without BIP91, no split will occur even if SegWit is not yet active. An email conversation discussing James Hilliard's BIP91, Splitprotection, Segwit2x, and the BIP148 UASF reveals that it is unlikely that BIP91-type logic can activate SegWit in time to avoid a BIP148 chain split. Therefore, ensuring the BIP148 split is as painless as possible becomes necessary. Unless concrete progress on a plan takes shape, particularly with miner support, a split is certain. Several refinements could deploy BIP91 faster, but changing these factors might not change miners' minds if they prefer to block SegWit. The email concludes that the best course of action is to convince miners to stop blocking SegWit, upgrade to SegWit-capable code if they haven't already, and signal bit 1 to activate it. It is expected that few parts of the ecosystem will join the fork, resulting in bearable disruption. Nonetheless, the BIP148 split is inevitable. To avoid the split, either 95% of hash power needs to be from BIP91 miners, signaling bit 1 and orphaning non-BIP91 by June 30th, or BIP91 must deploy, and more than 50% of hash power is activated by June 30th. No one should underestimate the steps required to meet the deadline: coordinate on a solution, implement and test it, convince more than 50% of miners to run it, and upgrade to the new software and begin signaling.
