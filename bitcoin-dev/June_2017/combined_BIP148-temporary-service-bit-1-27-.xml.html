Summary:

Bitcoin Knots, a Bitcoin software implementation, is taking measures to prevent network partitioning risks on August 1st by using the temporary service bit (1) to signal readiness for Segregated Witness (SegWit). This proposed protocol upgrade has been controversial in the Bitcoin community but has gained support from many businesses and users through the BIP91 proposal. Luke Dashjr, a developer of Bitcoin Knots, recommends that other software implementing the BIP148 should also use this service bit to avoid network partitioning risks and minimize the possibility of a chain split attack by miners. Tom Zander, another developer, requested information on how to act on finding a peer with or without the set bit, and although it is unclear where the implementation can be found, the discussion was held on the bitcoin-dev mailing list. Overall, Bitcoin Knots' decision to use the service bit (1) is seen as a positive step towards ensuring a smooth transition to the proposed protocol upgrade while also serving as a reminder to other Bitcoin software implementations to take similar measures to prevent a chain split and maintain the integrity of the Bitcoin network.
