Summary:

Martin has proposed a plan to improve UASFs (User Activated Soft Forks) with the use of smart contracts. The proposal aims to incentivize users to enforce the soft fork while ensuring that a significant part of the economy agrees to it and reducing the risk of support being too low. The contract would signal the intention to miners and other users, making it expensive for sybil attackers. Additionally, Martin suggests a unique way to pay developers for the development of the soft fork code.
