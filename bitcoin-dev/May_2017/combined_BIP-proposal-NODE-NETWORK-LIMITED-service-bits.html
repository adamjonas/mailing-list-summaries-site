Summary:

On May 11, 2017, Jonas Schnelli proposed a draft proposal for a Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) to improve the signaling of pruned peers who have no way to signal valuable services. The draft proposal suggests allowing clients to use peers with headroom and advising nodes to serve headers for their entire best chain. It also proposes disconnecting the least useful peers that aren't syncing to make more room for ones that are. Feedback on the proposal is welcomed.The discussion in the Bitcoin-dev mailing list centered around the usefulness of defining a deterministically chosen set of historical blocks within a certain timeframe, which could be undefined or at least 2016*2 blocks. There was also confusion about whether Core guarantees the 288 blocks post-segwit activation. Additionally, it was noted that light clients who are not checking the nServiceFlags from a relayed addr-message may unwillingly connect to a pruned peer and ask for filtered blocks below their pruned depth.To avoid this problem, peers should connect a limited amount of their available outbound connections to peers signaling one or both of the NODE_NETWORK_LIMITED_* service bits if they expect to request fewer blocks than the signaled number. The draft proposal can be found on GitHub under the name "NODE_NETWORK_LIMITED-BIP-DRAFT". Further discussion revolves around whether peers following the BIP should connect a limited amount of their available outbound connections to peers signaling one or both of the NODE_NETWORK_LIMITED_* service bits if they expect to request less blocks than the signaled number. Light clients who are not checking the nServiceFlags (service bits) from a relayed addr-message may unwillingly connect to a pruned peer and ask for (filtered) blocks at a depth below their pruned depth.Links were also shared for simple Zcash and Bitcoin wallets, torrent blocklists, and anti-spy software.
