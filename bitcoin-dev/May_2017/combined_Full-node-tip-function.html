Summary:

The Bitcoin-dev mailing list has been discussing ways to incentivize individuals to run full nodes on the Bitcoin network. A full node provides various services to the Bitcoin network, such as broadcasting blocks and transactions publicly or privately, filtering information for SPV nodes, and providing historic blockchain and state information to new nodes. However, incentivizing individuals to run full nodes has its challenges, such as the risk of sybil attacks.Several proposals have been made on the bitcoin-dev mailing list, including generating a template block that miners could buy for proof-of-uncensored-selection and proof-of-goodwill, allowing operators to charge for better service, and having full nodes advertise a Bitcoin address for users who need to download the blockchain from it. While these ideas could provide incentives for quality, long-term hosting, they may also represent a barrier for some people to host their own full node due to added costs.One proposal to incentivize users to host their own full nodes is to encourage them to send a tip to the peers that served them after downloading the blockchain. Tips of 10mbit are recommended, and full nodes can also require a tip for downloading the blockchain. This approach could discourage "installation spam" attacks on the network and encourage stable nodes to stick around longer. However, there are concerns about paying for services leading to centralization or sybil attacks, so other proposals have been made.Another proposal suggests full nodes generating and broadcasting a template block containing transaction selection, a coinbase output with a predetermined portion of the block reward to themselves, and a nonce for proof-of-work (PoW) of a predetermined hash function. This would allow miners/pools to outsource transaction selection using a different PoW, buying proof-of-uncensored-selection and proof-of-goodwill for a small fee. The template with the highest PoW since the last block would be leading, and a miner/pool can choose to use it instead of their own, adding the rest of the reward and SHA nonce themselves.Discussions have also taken place regarding the feasibility of Proof-of-Unique-Blockchain-Storage (PoUBS) and replicating IP addresses. Despite the concerns, there are potential benefits to incentivizing quality, long-term hosting, and discouraging installation spam attacks on the network.Some concerns were raised about adding a cost to the blockchain, as this could lead to centralization. In addition, the Lightning Network's killer use case is micro-payments for online network services, which could incentivize full nodes even further. If this protocol is available around the same time as LN, it could incentivize full nodes even further than LN does, and allow the network to be strongly DDOS resistant. These discussions highlight the ongoing challenges of maintaining a decentralized network like Bitcoin and finding solutions that balance competing interests and priorities.
