Summary:

In various email conversations and discussions on the Bitcoin development mailing list, Russell O'Connor and Peter Todd discuss the implementation and security of the SHA256 compression function and its role in building Merkle trees. O'Connor proposes using the hash of tags in Sha256Compress's first argument to avoid the need for SHA256's padding while maintaining the Merkle-Damgård property. Todd points out that this is similar to using SHA256 directly and suggests computing the midstate sha256Compress(IV, t) rather than sha256(t). They also discuss the idea of using safe tags to avoid collisions between the Sha256 and MerkleRoot functions but argue against depending on tags being unique as it could lead to collisions.In addition, they discuss the math operations used in a Merkle root calculation and the challenges of ensuring uniqueness across different systems. Todd also mentions his newer rust-proofmarshal work commitments, which use blake2b hashing algorithm with a large enough block size to fit most use-cases into a single block. Finally, O'Connor describes the SHA256 compression function and its usefulness for building Merkle Trie, suggesting an approach that skips unary hashes for improved performance. The discussions provide insights into the technical details of Bitcoin's cryptography and the challenges of ensuring security and efficiency in Merkle tree implementation.
