Summary:

On January 22, 2015, U. Mutlu pointed out the lack of support for Hierarchical Deterministic aka BIP32 wallets in Bitcoin Core which posed a risk as only one private key could be backed up per backup. Pavol Rusnak suggested Electrum or Multibit as an alternative software wallet that does support such functionality. The author of the text then proposed the idea of printing a wallet's account number in base16 or base64 format on paper for physical storage in case of a computer hardware disaster. However, to ensure security against bank fraud and theft, the output on paper should be encrypted, and the owner should store the key somewhere else. The author emphasized the need for developers to create this functionality for users.
