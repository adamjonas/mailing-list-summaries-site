Summary:

Gavin Andresen, a prominent figure in the Bitcoin community, has sent an email inviting members of the Bitcoin development mailing list to a meeting to discuss several topics, including the status of BIP 16 support and a protocol change scheduled for February 20. The meeting was planned for February 14 at 21:00 UTC on #bitcoin-dev on Freenode IRC. However, one member named Slush expressed concern that the timing of the meeting conflicted with Valentine's Day and that some members had girlfriends.In response, Gavin suggested that after the technical discussion, members could send each other bitcoins with addresses containing cute Valentine's Day messages. Additionally, Gavin included a promotional message for LearnDevNow, an online learning library for Microsoft developers. The chat will address several important topics related to the cryptocurrency, such as the progress towards achieving 50% hashing power, and a protocol change coming up on February 20th regarding checksums in version messages. Furthermore, Gavin plans to propose a solution involving the inclusion of block height in the coinbase to address the issue of duplicate coinbase. Attendees are invited to exchange bitcoins with addresses featuring cute Valentine's Day messages after discussing these technical matters.
