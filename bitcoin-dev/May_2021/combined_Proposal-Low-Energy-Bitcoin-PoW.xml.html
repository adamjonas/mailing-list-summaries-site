Summary:

The context discusses various proposals and discussions related to Proof-of-Work (PoW) mining in the Bitcoin network. One proposal is the introduction of a new type of PoW called "Optical Proof of Work" (oPoW), which uses photonic co-processors to perform MAC operations to increase energy efficiency and security. The oPoW algorithm is designed to reduce Bitcoin's reliance on massive energy consumption, which has led to mining centralization and limits participation in the network. However, there are concerns about the potential for creating centralization pressure by privileging those with access to chip manufacturers/foundries, and the complication of building, distributing, and operating hardware at scale.The conversation between Michael and ZmnSCPxj discusses the technical description of ASICBOOST, which reduces power consumption for the same number of hashes attempted by selectively varying bits in the first block of SHA256 while reusing the computation of the second block. ZmnSCPxj shares a writeup on ASICBOOST, suggesting that anyone who dreams of changing PoW algorithms should read and understand it. Phasing in oPoW slowly starting at a low percentage of rewards is proposed as a solution to mitigate the issue of ASICBOOST, but additional layers mean additional possibility of layer-crossing violations like ASICBOOST.The unpredictability of advancements in PoW mining due to technological developments is also discussed, along with the potential risks of large improvements that could overwhelm the difficulty adjustment mechanism and pose a risk of centralization and extinction for Bitcoin. Changing PoW should be avoided without serious study, but changing SHA256 for SHA3 on the txid and Merkle trees is more likely. It is suggested that instead of adding complexity to proof-of-work, a more direct approach should be taken, and detailed analysis comparing one alternative with the other is needed to determine the best mix of proof-of-work and proof-of-stake systems.The proposal of oPoW as a new type of PoW that uses optical computing to increase energy efficiency and security is discussed in detail, with the team planning to release open-source hardware designs for their oPoW miner prototypes. The introduction of oPoW could potentially allow billions of new miners to enter the market simply by investing in a low-energy photonic miner. However, there are concerns about the privilege of those who have access to chip manufacturers/foundries, and the potential complications of creating a new PoW algorithm.Overall, the context highlights the foundational concept that all currency security derives from energy consumption and ultimately everything resolves down to proof-of-work. While decentralization is crucial, it is necessary to strive for better solutions that consider all effects until reaching the base physics of the arrow of time. Energy expenditure is foundational to the system, and competition will employ ever more resources until it is unprofitable to do so.
