Summary:

The conversation among members of the bitcoin-dev mailing list focuses on the proposal of a new opcode called OP_CAT in Bitcoin. The proposal suggests increasing the size of two inputs to OP_CAT to 260 Bytes each, which would increase performance. Implementing tagged SHA256 as a new opcode is also discussed as a solution to issues with the use of Merkle trees in Bitcoin blocks.However, there are concerns about the worst-case scenario behavior of OP_CAT when allocating stack values. In addition, a 64-byte limit feels too small for Merkle tree proofs, while a ~500byte limit proposed elsewhere may be excessive. An alternative proposal was discussed involving the use of an OP_SHA256STREAM opcode that would allow for the concatenation of an unlimited amount of data to be hashed. However, implementing this proposal could expose raw SHA256 midstates, which is not secure as SHA256 is not designed for situations where adversaries control the initialization vector.Removing `SIGHASH` flags from signatures and putting them on public keys instead is proposed, and a new opcode, `OP_SETPUBKEYSIGHASH`, is suggested that retains the old feature where the sighash is selected at time-of-spending rather than time-of-payment. This would make it possible to hide public keys until they are needed, exemplified in MAST-inside-Taproot.The conversation also discusses the limitations of the size of the script and the possibility of using midstate attacks. There is also discussion about TumbleBit and Lightning without script, and how these protocols could benefit from OP_CAT.Ethan Heilman and Andrew Poelstra both expressed the importance of OP_CAT, as it would allow for the concatenation of stack values, enabling the creation of Merkle trees, manipulating signatures, enforcing EC points come from a hash with no known discrete log, and more. However, careful consideration must be taken to ensure its security and efficiency.In conclusion, these proposals could unlock numerous possibilities for Bitcoin's development. However, it is important to carefully consider their implications and potential risks.
