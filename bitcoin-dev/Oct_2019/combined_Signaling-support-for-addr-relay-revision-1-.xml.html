Summary:

The email proposes a solution to decouple address relay considerations from light/full node/block-relay-only in the Bitcoin network. The author suggests two ways to implement this: 2 new service bits or per-link-direction negotiation using BIP-155. The proposed solution involves making it explicit whether a node promises to participate in address relay by forwarding unsolicited messages and responding to GETADDR. The author believes that having more flexibility with per-link-direction negotiation is better for the future of the protocol, where some nodes might want to opt-out of addr relay for a subset of their links.The author suggests extending BIP-155 with per-link-direction negotiation to provide more flexibility in the future of the protocol. They don't have a strong opinion but feel that per-link variant makes more sense as they cannot imagine a future requirement for opting out completely. Two references are included in the email for further reading. The author is interested in the opinion of the community on implementing the proposed solution.
