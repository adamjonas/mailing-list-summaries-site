Summary:

The email conversation among Bitcoin developers covers a range of proposals to improve the current state of mempool transactions. One proposal involves tweaking Lightning's commitment transaction to have two small-value outputs that are immediately spendable, allowing each channel participant to chain children off without unrelated third-parties doing the same. This addresses the CPFP security model considerations and avoids adding complexity. Another proposal suggests a small tweak to the anti-DoS CPFP rules in Bitcoin Core/BIP 125, which would allow adding a large transaction to each output of the unconfirmed parent instead of adding a 1 CSV to all outputs. The recently released RC for bitcoind 0.19 includes a carve-out rule aimed at paving the way for more robust CPFP of on-chain contracts, particularly Lightning commitment transactions. However, concerns remain about defining criteria for "near the top of the mempool" for Lightning's requirements.Other discussions focus on optimizing fees and ensuring efficient computation of transactions with high indegree or outdegree while preventing attacks and maximizing miner profit. There are proposals to appropriately assess Replace By Fee update fees and optimize algorithms used in the mempool to compute descendants or ancestors for transactions with high indegree or outdegree. One suggestion is to cache which nodes have been visited on a run to avoid inefficiencies. To prevent RBF-pinning attacks, transactors could mark their transactions as "likely-to-be-RBF'ed," enabling a relay policy where children of such transactions would be rejected unless the resulting package would be "near the top of the mempool. "Another concern raised by developers is the cost of running a full node in the Bitcoin network. Increasing limits too high could make it more bandwidth expensive, making it easier for attackers to take advantage of the system. Developers are cautious about raising limits and are working on lowering the default minimum in Bitcoin Core, which would make the attack proportionally cheaper. Overall, the email exchange reveals ongoing discussions and proposals aimed at optimizing the mempool, preventing attacks, and improving the efficiency of Bitcoin transactions.
