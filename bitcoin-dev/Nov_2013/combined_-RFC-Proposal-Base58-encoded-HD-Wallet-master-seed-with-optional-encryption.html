Summary:

Jean-Paul Kogelman proposed a Base58 encoded HD wallet master seed with optional encryption in July 2013. The proposal is intended for use on paper wallets, and each encoded master seed contains all the information required to verify and reconstitute an HD wallet, except for the optional passphrase. The encrypted version uses salting and scrypt to resist brute-force attacks. The proposal provides two encoding methodologies in three lengths each, resulting in a compact representation of the master seed that is easier to handle.To implement the proposal with alt-chains, suggestions are given. The proposal also suggests a modification to BIP0038, which proposes a way to encrypt and protect Bitcoin private keys with a passphrase. The proposed modification is to allow the creation of encoded master seeds that do not start with "WS" or "ws". Additionally, a two-factor version of the master seed record allows for safe storage and the creation of paper wallets by third parties.Jean-Paul Kogelman has made an update to the proposal, adding a 2-byte 'weeks since 2013-01-01' field and updating the prefixes, ranges, and test vectors. However, the author of the post states that there have been no changes to the proposal since it was last discussed on the list during the BIP procedure discussions. The context is discussing the usability of SPV wallets, which require a birthday in order to function effectively. Without a birthday, scanning the entire blockchain can be slow or finding a fully indexed copy of the blockchain can be expensive and more centralized. In a forum discussion from July 2013, JP suggested adding a 2-byte 'weeks since genesis' or 'months since genesis' feature that would work for SPV wallets. But Mike Hearn pointed out that it wouldn't be usable unless it had a birthday. Additionally, Jean-Paul has updated a proposal and changed the checksum to be a double SHA256 of the private key rather than the public address string. He also added support for third-party KDF computation. The biggest difference between their proposal and BIP38 is that BIP38 allows for a third party to generate the encrypted private key and confirmation code from a passphrase code, while their proposal encrypts a random value fed into HMAC-SHA512 and includes a partial hash of the root address, making this not possible.Andreas M. Antonopoulos replied to the proposal, stating that he had a beta BIP0038 compliant paper wallet and was currently working on BIP0032 paper wallets. Antonopoulos also expressed interest in Kogelman's proposal, calling it necessary and a great approach to combine BIP0038 and BIP0032. Kogelman asked for feedback on his proposal and provided a link to the draft. However, in response to another participant's question about whether the encoding/decoding functions would suffice, Kogelman stated that the proposal was not yet in shippable form.
