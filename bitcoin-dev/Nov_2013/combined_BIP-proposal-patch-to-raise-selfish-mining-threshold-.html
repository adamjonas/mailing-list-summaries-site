Various email conversations and discussions on the Bitcoin mailing list have been taking place regarding the potential vulnerabilities and flaws in the Bitcoin protocol. In particular, a paper published in 2013 introduced a mining strategy called Selfish-Mine, which allows colluding miners to earn more than their mining power and potentially manipulate the decentralized currency. Proposed solutions to address these vulnerabilities have been put forward, such as raising the selfish mining threshold, but concerns have been raised about the potential harm to miners when all are honest, no new vulnerabilities being introduced, and complete backward compatibility.Ittay Eyal and Emin Gun Sirer proposed a Bitcoin Improvement Proposal to raise the threshold on Selfish Mining attacks by replacing the arbitrary choice among equal-length chains with explicit randomness controlled by the protocol. However, Peter Todd has expressed concerns about the proposal, stating that it could lead to larger mining pools withholding blocks to split the network.
