Summary:

Taylor Gerring has suggested extending the Payment Protocol with optional vCard support to improve user experience in wallets supporting PP and enhance usability of Payment Protocol for user-to-user transactions. Gerring drew on BIP 0070 and Mike Hearn’s Payment Protocol FAQ to outline the concept in detail and hopes that this will encourage a discussion on how to make wallets more friendly in a standard way, as there is interest in “contact exchange” functionality from the Hive team. However, in an email exchange with Hearn, he expressed skepticism about the usefulness of vCards and instead suggested social network integration as a better alternative. According to Hearn, social network integration utilizes the IDs people already have and could embed a social network URL into a payment request to associate a name/photo with a payment. He encourages experimentation and prototyping of different extension ideas. In another email exchange, Wendell expressed confusion about the use of a vCard in passing around data, but Hearn mentioned that all an extension requires are some tag numbers and they aren't in danger of running out anytime soon.
