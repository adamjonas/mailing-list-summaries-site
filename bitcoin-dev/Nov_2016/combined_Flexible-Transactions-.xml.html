Summary:

In the bitcoin-dev mailing list, an email was proposed to change the semantics of OP_CHECKSIG in version 2 of script. However, the proposal was criticized for being too naive as it did not include the data indicating which input is being signed, resulting in identical signatures on different inputs sharing the same public key due to key reuse. Additionally, the Flexible Transactions proposal opens up a new line of attack against Bitcoin that doesn't currently exist. Tom Zander from the bitcoin-dev mailing list responded to an email from Russell, thanking him for his review and mentioned that the issue had already been fixed quite some time ago. Russell expressed his happiness regarding the solution and looked forward to seeing BIP 134 updated with it. It is important to understand why things are the way they are before proposing changes.
