Summary:

In a recent email thread on the bitcoin-dev mailing list, a proposal was made for introducing an auxiliary proof-of-work (POW) to mitigate mining centralization pressures and introduce an intermediary confirmation point. This proposal involves a soft-fork introduction of a new POW system, which is not considered to be a hard-fork. The proposal suggests introducing aux-POW blocks between normal blocks, and each block would contain transactions like a normal block.Peter Todd reviewed the draft proposal and argued that it should be called a "pseudo-soft-fork" due to its different security model. The proposal aims to reduce the risk of a hard-fork while introducing a new POW system. The proposer argues that the total transaction rate and block size remain unchanged, but this argument is refuted by the fact that the expected time to find each type of block will be halved, resulting in the doubling of the orphan rate.The reward for the auxiliary POW would increase over time and be transferred via a soft-fork restriction requiring a coinbase output to an address published in the auxiliary-POW block. The heaviest chain rule changes from sum of difficulty to sum of mainDifficulty^x * auxDifficulty^y. Difficulty adjustments remain independent for the two POWs. The difficulty of the aux POW is adjusted based on the average time between normal block found to aux block found.Peter Todd, a Bitcoin developer, provided feedback on the proposal and argued that this could not be called a soft fork and instead should be referred to as a "pseudo-soft-fork" due to its different security model. Todd noted that the proposal would decrease the block interval, leading to increased orphan rates and potential security implications. Paul Sztorc agreed with Peter's comments and gave his support.The discussion also touched on increased orphan rates due to decreased block intervals. The proposal presents several challenges and opportunities that require further analysis and evaluation. The choice of a suitable POW will require deep analysis due to potential pitfalls such as botnet mining, unexpected/covert optimizations, and POWs that appear ASIC resistant but are not. Overall, the proposed soft-fork introduction of a new POW system involves introducing auxiliary POW blocks between normal blocks with each aux-POW block pointing to the previous normal block and containing transactions like a normal block.
