Summary:

The article explores the concept of Payjoin, a payment method that enables two parties to exchange Bitcoin value without revealing identifying information. It also introduces Payjoin2swap, which allows participants to swap Bitcoin value without compromising their transaction privacy. Participants in Payjoin2swap need to own two UTXOs onchain, and they must agree on various parameters for the swap, including the amount to be swapped, safe confirmation depth for anchoring, future block heights, and the exchange of private keys. The article further discusses Linking (and Overlinking) Payjoin2swap and how it can potentially erode privacy if not implemented correctly. To address the issue of hodlers not being able to utilize Payjoin directly, the article suggests using bitcoins in a Payjoin to buy bitcoins of similar value or implementing Payjoin2swap using Lightning Network operations if the network supports Wumbo channels and dual-funded channels. However, the author notes that existing LN implementations may not apply ZeroLink for onchain funds, which can be a concern. Overall, Payjoin2swap is a promising protocol that enhances transaction privacy by allowing two parties to swap their bitcoin without revealing any information about their transactions.
