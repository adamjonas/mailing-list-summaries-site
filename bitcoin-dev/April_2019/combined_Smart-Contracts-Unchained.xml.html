Summary:

A proposal has been made to implement smart contracts without launching a separate blockchain or sidechain, offering benefits to privacy and smart-contract-patching. The proposal involves creating a new transaction type called "Vault Transactions" using Bitcoin's script language that allows for locking funds for a specified period and releasing them only if certain conditions are met. Various channel mechanisms can invalidate or replace old states without requiring a blockchain to keep track of all states. A concern was raised regarding how an escrow would know if a contract state is old and the operation is disallowed. It is suggested that the use of covenant system can enforce creating an off-chain sequence of transactions. The author of the email discusses concerns about the centralization and security of sidechains. They propose a decentralized system to store intermediate states instead of relying on a blockchain-like structure, suggesting the use of a distributed escrow. The conversation between Aymeric and ZmnSCPxj revolves around the possibility of recovering funds in case smart contract platforms disappear, with ZmnSCPxj suggesting that recovery is possible if all participants agree to a fair distribution of funds without dispute. They also discuss the difference between colored coins and sidechains, their opinions on Ethereum and Bitcoin, and their views on Lightning Network.The conversation revolves around the comparison of Ethereum and Bitcoin sidechains. The writer emphasizes the importance of precision in terminology, adding that a sidechain is essentially a blockchain. The discussion also touches upon colored coins and their mechanism, and the need to give humans the option to do the right thing. Regarding Lightning Network, the author explains that it does not have the characteristics of a blockchain or a sidechain. However, the network and federated sidechains share a "federated" off-chain structure. Each channel in LN is a 2-member federation, which increases the security of the mechanism, requiring consensus (2-of-2) instead of a quorum (m-of-n).ZmnSCPxj proposes an alternative method for implementing smart contracts without launching a separate blockchain or sidechain, achieving similar security to federated sidechains, along with added privacy and smart-contract-patching benefits. The proposal enables the federation to be selected by participants, allowing them to advertise themselves as trusted executors of smart contracts by locking some bonded amount on the blockchain. Participants then select trustworthy executors and decide what voting quorum of selected executors is sufficient to convince them of the correct execution of the smart contract.The email conversation highlights concerns about centralization and the use of sidechains, suggesting a distributed escrow solution for storing intermediate states. The author acknowledges the difficulties associated with this proposal but believes that sidechains claiming to be decentralized are misleading people. Additionally, the author raises concerns about platforms supporting smart contracts going down.Finally, the email discusses the incorporation of lightning's benefits into smart contracts. The proposal involves using funds locked up in a lightning channel to create a 2/3 MultiSig output for an escrow scheme, offering solutions for Smart Contracts in a Lightning Channel, Single Hop Smart Contracts, and Fully Routed Smart Contracts. Overall, these discussions and proposals highlight the ongoing efforts to improve the security and decentralization of smart contracts in the blockchain industry.
