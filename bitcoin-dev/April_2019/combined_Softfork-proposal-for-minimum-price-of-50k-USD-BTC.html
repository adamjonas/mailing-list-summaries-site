Summary:

On April 1, 2019, Luke Dashjr proposed two Bitcoin Improvement Proposals (BIPs) aimed at addressing the issue of some parts of the community selling bitcoins for unreasonably low prices, which had halted Bitcoin's valuation at $20k and even driven the price down below $15k. The first BIP suggests adding a signed price field to Bitcoin transactions, allowing the sender to affirm the value of bitcoins used in the transaction. Meanwhile, the second BIP defines a minimum price of $50k USD/BTC for Bitcoin transactions and is set to be implemented as a soft fork beginning with block height 622370, expected around April 2020.The reference implementation for the BIPs is available on GitHub, but concerns have been raised about the vulnerability of the proposals and the arbitrary nature of using USD as the reference currency. Some members of the Bitcoin developer community have criticized the proposals as being too conservative and pointed out serious vulnerabilities in the proposal, such as there being no limit on the maximum supply of USD. Despite these concerns, Dashjr urges developers to review the proposals as soon as possible.There are limitations in representing the true value of USD in BTC under the current encoding proposed in BIP, while some members of the community suggest using USDT instead of USD. Peter Todd pointed out the serious vulnerability in the proposal - there is no limit on the maximum supply of USD.The activation for the soft fork in the second BIP was set a full year into the future to ensure time to upgrade both nodes and wallet software. Ricardo Filipe expressed concern that one year seemed too long and suggested that they could easily get there in six months at most, to which Luke Dashjr did not respond.The context also included a message from someone offering their tech services for free to prove their value and mentioning their ownership of Litmocracy and Meme Racing, as well as their webmaster role for The Voluntaryist and coding work for The Dollar Vigilante.Overall, the BIPs proposed by Luke Dashjr have received support from some members of the Bitcoin community, but others have criticized the proposals as being too conservative and pointed out vulnerabilities in the proposal.
