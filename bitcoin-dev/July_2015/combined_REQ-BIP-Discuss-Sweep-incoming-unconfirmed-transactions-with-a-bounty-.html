Summary:

The Bitcoin community has been discussing the concept of Child Pays For Parent (CPFP) transaction creation feature, which allows users to boost incoming transactions. A bounty has been offered for anyone who can add this feature to any wallet featured on the Bitcoin homepage. There are concerns regarding the compatibility with coinjoin and double-spends, but some miners do use CPFP. PR#1647 was created to implement CPFP, but it only addressed miner policy, and Matt Whitlock pointed out that wallets also need to offer users a way to increase incoming transactions for CPFP to be effective.Peter Todd proposed Replace-by-fee scorched-earth without child-pays-for-parent as a countermeasure to address the risks associated with CPFP. The risks associated with not enabling CPFP included the possibility that child transactions may enter the blockchain without their parent transactions. During a conversation between Aaron Voisine and an unknown person on July 9, 2015, Aaron asked if the person knew how much hashing power was using CPFP. This information would be helpful for wallet developers to determine their confirmation times when utilizing CPFP.The Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) nn by Dan Bryant is a draft that could add functionality to the Bitcoin-Core reference implementation and also add flexibility into any future fee schedules. It addresses the need of incentive inclusion of unconfirmed transactions, which creates new high fee transactions (or bounty) that spend the desired unconfirmed transactions. This BIP could be highly desirable for merchants who may have little control over the type of wallets their customers use.The discussion on Bitcoin development mailing list is about child pays for parent (CPFP) and Replace-by-fee (RBF). The feature called 'child pays for parent' allows users to boost incoming transactions. PR#1647 only addresses miner policy, and the BIP is addressing the user-facing side of this functionality. However, the sweep transaction UI points are out of scope for the BIP. The proposed BIP would have two implementations: an automatic sweep of incoming unconfirmed transaction setting, and a manual sweep of unconfirmed transaction setting. Both would have the ability to set the fee amount the user would like to pay for the sweep.Creating a transaction with high fees can protect against double spending in payment services. However, it requires RBF and would not be compatible with first seen safe. Additionally, a 1BTC fee transaction in nearly every block would boost network security and avoid concerns about mining pools making secret deals with payment services.In conclusion, the proposed BIP would add flexibility into any future fee schedules and provide merchants with the ability to sweep all their POS transactions currently in the mempool into one high fee sweep, thus greatly increasing the chance that they are in the next block. The proposal is not addressed in PR#1647, which only addresses miner policy, and may require agreement among wallet authors to implement.
