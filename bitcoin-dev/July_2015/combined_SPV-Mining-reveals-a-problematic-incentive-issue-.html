The issue of SPV mining and its impact on the robustness of Bitcoin's network is a topic of concern. The cost of verifying transactions has incentivized miners to forego transaction verification, increasing the risk of mining atop an invalid block. This behavior weakens the network against forks and allows consensus-violating forks to persist. To address this problem, a reward system could be implemented to incentivize miners to verify transactions. It is important for miners to validate transactions to prevent the possibility of SPV mining, which requires producing empty blocks to prevent hashing hardware from wasting power when it isn't needed. However, mining on top of potentially worthless blocks poses security risks, and increasing the orphan rate makes the network less secure. In addition, there is a risk of malicious miners re-mining previous blocks and dropping some transactions, offering everyone else an extra reward if they mine on top of the re-mined block. However, if useful fees are always available to mine immediately after a block is found, the incentives to deliberately orphan the last block are reduced. Overall, the discussion calls for a solution to address the problem of SPV mining and its impact on the robustness of Bitcoin's network.
