Summary:

The importance of running full nodes in the Bitcoin network is emphasized in this context. It is suggested that education is needed to make businesses aware of the significance of full nodes for ensuring security and integrity for client funds. Companies with large Bitcoin ecosystems are urged to insist on running their own full nodes and cross-checking them to reduce risk. The lack of understanding of how Bitcoin works and security engineering is identified as a significant contributor to recent issues surrounding Bitcoin security.Despite having a huge funding of $30M USD, even big companies do not run a single Bitcoin Core node. The author of the given context argues that companies like blockchain.info and Coinbase are not running even a single node to sanity check against, despite their large funding. The author points out that these companies only run BitcoinJ and blindly follow invalid chains, which does not involve proper consensus checking. Additionally, the author mentions that a bad peer from 37.187.136.15, owned by blockchain.info, is connected to his nodes, which does not relay inventory but downloads everything. Furthermore, the author questions if people will be able to trust such companies in the future if the load goes higher and highlights that even blockchain.info doesn't behave like a full node, showing invalid data during the BIP66 fork.The CryptoCurrency Security Standard (CCSS) is an open standard designed to share industry best practices with the community at large. Eric is working on a post inviting everyone in the mailing list to participate, but the CCSS website and GitHub repository are already available for contributions. The belief behind CCSS is that sharing knowledge and best practices benefits everyone.Decentralization and more layer 2 work are also seen as solutions to the centralization problem. However, it is noted that major centralization-related failures will help improve understanding of the importance of full nodes, but there is only one shot at this due to the ease of killing PoW systems at birth.In contrast, the argument that the decrease in node count is due to block sizes is only speculation and not based on facts. As part of the current active work on decentralization and layer 2 scaling solutions, a modest blocksize increase to smooth out the transition to layer 2 may be considered. The specific question asked concerns whether a 1MB increase to 2MB would be considered "modest" according to BIP 102.The author concludes by saying that it is expected of such companies to run at least one node to ensure proper consensus, which can be achieved with a mere $5 VPS. There is pushback from some quarters against the idea of requiring full nodes, but there is pretty much unanimous agreement that they should be required at higher levels of certification.
