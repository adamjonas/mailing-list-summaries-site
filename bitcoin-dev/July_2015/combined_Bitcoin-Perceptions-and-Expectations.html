Summary:

The Bitcoin community is currently discussing the issue of potentially misleading terminology used to describe Bitcoin transactions. While Bitcoin transactions are fast, they are slower than Visa transactions that are considered "instant." Moreover, terms such as "free," "instant," and "zero or low fees" can create unrealistic expectations for users. A pull request has been made to remove these misleading terms from Bitcoin.org, and Vincent Truong, a member of the Bitcoin-dev mailing list, has suggested using finance terminology such as "settlement" instead of "confirmation" to better align with traditional financial systems.Truong also recommends a percentage-based system for showing transaction confidence rather than displaying a meaningless number of confirmations. He believes that the term "confirmation" is arbitrary and implies finality and safety, which can be misleading to merchants and consumers. Additionally, Truong suggests avoiding misleading language that implies a transaction takes ten minutes to travel the globe when describing Bitcoin's speed compared to traditional payment methods like Visa.An email thread from July 2015 highlights the importance of educating users about the risks associated with different validation stages in Bitcoin transactions. The breakdown provided in the email shows that "validated" means the transaction has been seen on the network, while "settled/cleared" requires one confirmation and "finalized" requires six confirmations. This underscores the need for continued development in the Bitcoin ecosystem to improve security measures against potential attacks, such as Finney attacks where a miner could double-spend a transaction before it gets confirmed on the blockchain. Overall, the Bitcoin community is focused on improving understanding among merchants and consumers by redefining Bitcoin terminology to better align with finance terminology and accurately reflect how the system currently works.
