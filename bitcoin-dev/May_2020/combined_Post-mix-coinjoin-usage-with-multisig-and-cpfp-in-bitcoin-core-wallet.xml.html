Summary:

ZmnSCPxj has proposed a protocol for post-mix usage using multisig and CPFP to Prayank in a Medium article. The protocol involves participants Peer 3 as the payee, Peer 2 as the payer, and Peer 1 as an enabling trusted third party. The goal is for Peer 2 to pay 0.006BTC to Peer 3 through a 2-of-3 address with their public keys and Peer 3's public key. The author suggests JoinMarket has a superior technology that doesn't require a trusted third party, allowing the market taker to decide how much the equal-value outputs are and define the address it goes to. Prayank discusses implementing a peer-to-peer system to provide liquidity for others and incentivize participation with small fees but raises concerns about the trustworthiness of Peer 1 in ZmnSCPxj's proposal.They also suggest using different mixing options to make it harder for spy companies to analyze transactions and mention the potential use of multisig for privacy, which could improve with Schnorr and Taproot upgrades. The email conversation concludes by clarifying that the proposal is about spending coins after coinjoin in a secure manner, using multisig transactions with three parties involved and reviewing outputs before confirming the transaction. In case of disputes, the system can become complex by locking some amount of money in a 2 of 2 multisig or using incentive structures. The focus is on post-mix usage as it is equally important as coinjoin and some users do not follow best practices which make coinjoin less effective. The end goal is to create more options for users to spend UTXOs easily in different ways after coinjoin, making it difficult for people trying to analyze transactions or algorithms monitoring and flagging reporting txs 24/7. The author has shared a proof of concept in a Medium article, demonstrating the use of multisig and CPFP for post-mix usage. They are seeking feedback on whether it would make sense to add these options to the Bitcoin Core wallet, particularly with the upcoming taproot upgrade and its potential impact on privacy for multisig transactions.
