Summary:

Ruben Somsen and Dmitry Petukhov had an email conversation discussing the Lightning Network protocol, specifically the potential issue of Bob giving success_tx to a friendly miner while refund_tx_1 is in the mempool. Ruben clarified that this would not be an issue unless both Alice and Bob misbehaved. They also discussed the cooperative spend of refund_tx #1 by Alice and Bob, which ensures that Bob can spend the money before Alice once he receives her key at the end of the protocol.The author of the post created a formal specification for the Succint Atomic Swap (SAS) contract using TLA+. The model encoded in the specification can detect the violation of 'no mutual secret knowledge' invariant when one participant bypasses mempool and gives the transaction directly to the miner. However, there is one transition that was unclear how to model. The temporal properties checking works fine, but more work is needed to devise and add the invariants.In the future, an established framework for modelling the behavior in Bitcoin-like blockchain networks would be great. In particular, having a model of mempool-related behavior would help to reason about difficult RBF/CPFP issues. The author believes that TLA+ is a suitable tool for specifying and checking smart contract specifications.The specification covers all relevant behaviors of the participants and even has an option to enable 'non-rational' behavior so that transactions punishing bad behavior can be utilized. Despite some limitations, such as only having one miner and not modeling fees and mempool priorities, the author hopes that this specification can be used as a basis for other contracts and increase interest in using formal methods in this area.Lastly, the author hopes that their specification can be used as a basis for other contracts and increase interest in using formal methods in this area. Experienced people in formal methods and TLA+ are encouraged to give their comments, suggestions, and corrections. Thanks were given to Ruben Somsen for designing the contract and providing helpful descriptions and diagrams that made it possible to create this formal specification.
