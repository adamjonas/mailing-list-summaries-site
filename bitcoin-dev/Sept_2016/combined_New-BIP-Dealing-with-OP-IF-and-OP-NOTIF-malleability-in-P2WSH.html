Summary:

Several discussions have taken place on the Bitcoin-dev mailing list regarding transaction malleability and potential solutions to prevent it. One proposed solution is to introduce a soft-fork and add an opcode, OP_PROGSIZE, that pushes the size of the segwit program being evaluated onto the stack. Another proposal is to implement a separate CHECKBOOLVERIFY opcode that ensures the top item on the stack is a minimally encoded true or false value, allowing script writers to opt into this behavior. Additionally, BIP146 with null dummy value for CHECKMULTISIG ("NULLDUMMY") will eliminate all malleability for simple multi-sig in P2WSH and BIP146 + NULLDUMMY + minimal IF argument ("MINIMALIF") will eliminate malleability for any Lightning Network scripts.The discussions also touch on concerns about miners altering witness data and the need to enforce MINIMALIF in some cases. In addition, there are debates about making proposed changes a relay policy first before considering a softfork and whether relay rules should be relied upon solely. There are also concerns about potential legal issues and increased fees with tampering with SegWit transactions as a whole.Another conversation proposes changes to Bitcoin script validity rules to prevent malleability related to OP_IF and OP_NOTIF in P2WSH scripts, with a proposed solution being a soft fork and the addition of an opcode OP_PROGSIZE. Johnson Lau also proposes a new BIP to address OP_IF and OP_NOTIF malleability in P2WSH scripts, with changes affecting the combination of "OP_SIZE OP_IF" or "OP_DEPTH OP_IF".Concerns are raised about the potential loss of bitcoins due to IoT devices rejecting transactions with witness programs too large for their implementation-imposed limits. Sergio Demian Lerner suggests invalidating transactions with a higher witness size than expected by the sender. The context also includes discussions around the fees required for replacing transactions, with BIP125 and mempool eviction requiring a higher fee for the replacing transaction to compensate for the cost of relaying the replaced transaction(s). Finally, there are proposals to enforce OP_TRUE or OP_FALSE with the option to save bytes and push potential DoS costs onto the network, but concerns are raised about the costs for those who want to evaluate pushdata. Overall, minimizing areas of transaction malleability should be easy to resolve and well-understood by script writers in the future.
