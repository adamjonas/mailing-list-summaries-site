Summary:

A researcher named Murch has compiled a Master's thesis on the Coin Selection problem, which has been accepted by Scaling Bitcoin. The thesis includes a framework to simulate wallet behavior based on a sequence of payments and re-implemented multiple coin selection strategies of prominent Bitcoin wallets including Bitcoin Core, Mycelium, Breadwallet, and Android Wallet for Bitcoin. Murch has invited feedback and requests anyone with another sequence of incoming and outgoing payment amounts to run his simulation on, to share it.Murch also posted on the bitcoin-dev mailing list on September 21, 2016, clarifying the name of the Android wallet for Bitcoin as "Bitcoin Wallet" or "Bitcoin Wallet for Android". The bitcoin-dev mailing list is a discussion forum for developers working on the Bitcoin network and related projects.When building a coin selection algorithm, there are three goals to consider: minimizing cost, maximizing privacy, and minimizing UTXO footprint. However, it is difficult to achieve all three goals simultaneously. Murch has analyzed the Mycelium coin selection algorithm and explained that it selects UTXO in a FIFO approach but prunes by removing the smallest selected UTXO until the excess beyond the spending target is minimized. This post-selection step seems to be the likely reason for Mycelium's small UTXO build-up.A balanced approach between BreadWallet's and Mycelium's approaches might be that instead of pruning all small inputs, a few of the small inputs could be allowed to be selected to slowly drain low-value UTXO out of the wallet by spending them over time.
