Summary:

On October 15, 2016, members of the bitcoin-dev community discussed the best-suited license for Bitcoin Improvement Proposals (BIPs). Marco Falke suggested that BIPs should be licensed under a public domain license such as CC0 with a CC-BY option. However, Tom Zander disagreed with Falke's suggestion of dual-licensing BIPs under CC0 and CC-BY and instead suggested that "Share alike" is required while "Attribution" is optional. Despite this, some parts of BIP 2 appeared to conflict with these views and suggested that public domain is not universally recognized as a legitimate action.Luke-jr made updates to BIP 2 and reworked it as a replacement for BIP 1. In addition, he clarified that BIP2 does not forbid releasing work under PD in legislations where this is possible, despite the goal being to no longer allow PD as the only copyright option. Lastly, Luke Dashjr recommended using the "Share alike" requirement and making "Attribution" optional and suggested offering users the choice between CCO.Zander questioned the inclusion of MIT/BSD in the list of licenses for BIPs, which led to a discussion about licensing requirements for BIPs. The conversation also touched upon BIP 2 and its requirement for copyright options besides public domain. While some members expressed concerns over this requirement, it was clarified that BIP 2 does not prohibit PD in jurisdictions where it is recognized as legal.Bitcoin Improvement Proposals (BIPs) are being discussed and updated by members of the bitcoin-dev community. Luke-jr has updated BIP 2, which is now replacing BIP 1. OPL will no longer be an acceptable license and Markdown format is not permitted for BIPs. In addition, email addresses are now required for authors, non-image auxiliary files are permitted in the bip-XXXX subdirectory, and implementation is required before BIPs can proceed to Proposed Status. Furthermore, BIP Comments have been introduced and the Resolution header has been dropped. The License preamble headers have also been added.The discussion ended with Falke suggesting to move forward with BIP 2 in the next couple of days/weeks if there were no objections. However, suggestions were made for improving the process of gathering community consensus, including reaching out to more people and making clear any proposed changes with reasoning.
