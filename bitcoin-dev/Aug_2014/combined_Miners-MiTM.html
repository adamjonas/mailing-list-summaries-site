Summary:

In August 2014, a discussion was held regarding potential security threats to Bitcoin mining pools. The conversation revolved around protecting Bitcoin mining traffic, with SSL and certificate validation on the client side being suggested as methods of protection. However, due to difficulties with certificate revocation and updates, most pools do not use them, leaving the traffic vulnerable to interception through the BGP protocol. The impact of such interception is not fully understood, and there are no known cases of it being used against Bitcoin specifically.The conversation involved various Bitcoin developers and open source evangelists discussing the best ways to protect Bitcoin mining traffic. One suggestion was to implement something similar to what OpenSSH does, where a certificate fingerprint is stored and verified automatically upon further connections to the server, but the initial connection needs to be verified manually by the operator. In August 2014, enabling TLS was discussed as a potential solution, but it could also function as a denial-of-service attack on pool servers. A more lightweight solution was proposed to prevent client payout redirection and server impersonation.There were concerns about continuous and active BGP feed manipulation by organized crime elements, but no documented cases of it being used against Bitcoin specifically. Luke Dashjr responded to concerns raised about an article detailing how someone was abusing the BGP protocol to intercept miner traffic and profit from it. He stated that both BFGMiner and Eloipool had already been hardened against it, but no Bitcoin pools had deployed these measures so far.Mike Hearn questioned the necessity of certificate validation unless the attacker could perform a direct man-in-the-middle attack during connection time, which is harder to maintain than injecting a client.reconnect. Jeff Garzik also participated in the discussion. Some believe that referring to other cryptocurrencies as 'scamcoins' was part of an organized marketing/psychological operations effort to marginalize competitors and that the altcoin BGP hijacks were likely testing the system for future use.Overall, there is a need for stronger protection measures in Bitcoin mining traffic, but challenges remain in implementation and adoption. The conversation highlights the importance of finding lightweight solutions that do not impede the functionality of Bitcoin mining.
