Summary:

The context discusses various Bitcoin payment protocols and proposals. One of the proposals is called Bustapay, which aims to make Bitcoin transactions more fungible and avoid information leakage during transactions. It proposes a simple payjoin transaction that requires no changes to Bitcoin and creates transactions that are indistinguishable from normal ones. The sender creates a signed transaction paying the receiver, known as the "template transaction," and gives it to the receiver who adds their own input, known as the "contributed input," before returning a partially signed coinjoin to the sender. The receiver validates, re-signs, and propagates the transaction on the bitcoin network.There are notes for receivers and sending applications to ensure proper implementation. However, some concerns have been raised about the complexity and practicality of the protocol. Members discuss the need for protocol versioning and the inclusion of PSBT/BIP174 in the standardization process. Additionally, there is debate over the utility of including information such as the transaction version and locktime in the protocol.Another conversation takes place regarding a protocol used to prevent denial-of-service (DoS) and spy attacks in Bitcoin transactions. The sender of the message expresses concern about the complexity of the process and questions the validity of the anti-DoS/anti-spy tactic used in the protocol. They suggest simplifying the protocol by dropping the signing in the first step to increase privacy and enforce completion or failure of the transaction. The implementer agrees with this suggestion and emphasizes the importance of keeping the protocol as simple as possible to avoid complications.In an email discussion, James MacWhyte suggests that the current Bustapay spec doesn't need to be complicated. He argues that signed transactions are simpler because they are more standard and easier to validate. He also points out that the proposed anti-spy feature isn't effective against a DOS attack and could be easily bypassed by double-spending the template transaction before it's propagated. MacWhyte suggests keeping the protocol simple to avoid complicating the job for the receiver, but warns that dropping the signing requirement in the first step would introduce transaction malleability issues.Overall, the discussions revolve around the need for simplicity in Bitcoin payment protocols and the importance of standardization and versioning. The Bustapay proposal offers a practical solution to address fungibility concerns and blockchain analysis by creating Bitcoin transactions that are indistinguishable from normal ones without changing the Bitcoin system. However, some concerns have been raised about its complexity and practicality.
