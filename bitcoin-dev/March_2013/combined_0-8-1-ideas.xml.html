Summary:

In March 2013, there was a discussion among the Bitcoin development community regarding a critical bug in versions 0.7 and earlier that would allow a malicious miner to generate an alternative blockchain, causing the Bitcoin network to split. Luke-Jr proposed a solution by lowering the block size limit from 1MB to 24391 transaction modifications for the next 131072 blocks or approximately six months. But, other developers argued that this proposal would still require a hard fork. The community also discussed the difficulty of getting people to update their client software. Some users may not want to upgrade due to the risk of breaking behavior changes, while others may isolate their production nodes from the internet using upgraded nodes. The community suggested a coordinated way for miners to remove the above patch in the future. The community also discussed the idea of the client detecting warning about non-trivial forking.
