Summary:

The Lightning Network and Bitcoin communities are currently discussing potential attacks on the Lightning protocol. One issue is RBF Pinning HTLC Transactions, where a bidding war can occur between two parties to get their version of the transaction committed on-chain. Another possible attack is spending an HTLC using the preimage with a low-fee, RBF-disabled transaction. Proposed solutions involve adding RBF carve-outs, changing contract text in Poon-Dryja channels, and implementing independent pay-to-preimage transactions.There are concerns about reliability and practicality, and discussions also cover potential centralization risks associated with obtaining information from miners' mempools. Proposed changes to policy rules include requiring lightning nodes to have an active full node with an in-sync mempool and limiting the size of low-fee-rate packages in the mempool to prevent DoS attacks on Bitcoin nodes.Developers have suggested various solutions to these issues, such as implementing an RBF carve-out output to HTLC-Timeout, allowing B to exponentially increase the fee as L+1 approaches to prevent C from stealing the HTLC value. However, concerns have been raised about the incentives issues associated with pay-to-preimage transactions, which may lead to miners charging for information about their mempool and increasing centralization. David A. Harding proposes making pay-to-revealed-adaptor-signature possible as an alternative solution.Another proposed solution involves limiting the size of low-fee-rate packages in the mempool to prevent DoS attacks on Bitcoin nodes. Another proposal suggests allowing transactors to mark their transactions as "likely-to-be-RBF'ed" to prevent low-fee-rate packages from growing. The Lightning Network's anchor outputs spec has also raised concerns about potential RBF pinning vulnerabilities.To mitigate the potential policy changes that could be exploited by attackers, proposals include implementing changes described in the open lightning-rfc PR that involves mempool-watching or adding a base version of anchors. Bitcoin developer Jeremy Rubin suggests increasing resources towards finishing mempool issues, including testing construction, refactoring algorithms, and package relay.Possible solutions to the HTLC bidding war issue include implementing mempool-watching, adding a BIP61 reject message with an extension that returns the txids of any conflicts, and upgrading Poon-Dryja channels to Decker-Russell-Osuntokun channels. While there are technical solutions suggested to mitigate these attacks, there are also incentives issues associated with some of these proposals. The discussion acknowledges the difficulties in mitigating such attacks and suggests potential solutions involving eltoo and transaction relay metadata.
