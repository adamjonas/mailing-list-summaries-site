Summary:

Payjoin is a technique that consolidates UTXOs through "snowballing" and enables merchants to receive payments from customers. In this process, Alice and Bob can do a payjoin with Alice paying 0.01 BTC to Bob. However, there are concerns about the effectiveness of payjoin and whether it would be easy to break the privacy of transaction chains if there was just one consolidated UTXO after each payjoin. Mr. Lee Chiffre expressed these concerns and gave an example where Alice payjoins to Bob and later on, Clark payjoins with Bob. Based on the payjoin between Clark and Bob, Clark now knows what UTXO was actually Bob's and which one was Alice's. The email response to Mr. Lee Chiffre's concerns added that payjoin breaks the common-input-ownership heuristic, which is a significant reason why blockchain surveillance is so effective. Satoshi mentioned this assumption in the whitepaper, where he lamented that the privacy loss is unavoidable. However, the fact that we have technology to break this assumption is a massive deal, and that's a big value-add of PayJoin. It also provided a link to a paper on the unreasonable effectiveness of address clustering.Moreover, the example given is that if Alice has a UTXO of 0.05 btc and Bob has a UTXO of 1.15 btc, Bob can be assumed to have more balance because he is a merchant and his customers payjoin him payments a lot. Bob can hold a 0.05 BTC UTXO in a holding wallet while taking the 1.16 UTXO from Alice and transferring 1.12 BTC to the holding wallet. This looks like one of the "customer pays Bob" transactions but is different. Bob then uses the holding wallet to pay out to employees, suppliers, and shareholders in a single large batched transaction, leaving behind another 0.05 BTC in the holding wallet for the next time Bob has to pay to employees/suppliers/shareholders.In conclusion, Payjoin has numerous advantages and disadvantages that need to be considered. It breaks the common-input-ownership heuristic, which is a significant reason why blockchain surveillance is so effective. However, it also enables merchants to consolidate UTXOs, making it easier to pay out to employees, suppliers, and shareholders in a single large batched transaction. While payjoin has the potential to improve privacy, it may not be foolproof and there are still concerns to be addressed, such as the possibility of breaking the privacy of transaction chains. It is recommended to shuffle inputs and outputs or use BIP39 consistently.
