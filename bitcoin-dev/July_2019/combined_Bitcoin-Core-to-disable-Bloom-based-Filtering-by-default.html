Summary:

The recent Bitcoin Core release has sparked mixed reactions on the bitcoin-dev mailing list. There have been ongoing debates about how to ensure security and scalability while maintaining the decentralized nature of Bitcoin. The use of Basic Block Filters (BIP158) for Bitcoin transactions is also being debated, with concerns about its impact on network traffic and potential privacy tradeoffs. Additionally, the NODE_BLOOM feature in Bitcoin is relied upon by over 10 million wallets, but there are concerns about its use as a DoS vector.There have been discussions about improving current filtering for segwit. Andreas Schildbach expressed concerns about a hardcoded filter in the Bitcoin protocol that could encourage or enforce address re-use. He suggested improving the current filtering for segwit by testing scripts themselves and each scriptPubKey spent by any input against the filter. However, there was a consensus among protocol developers that BIP37, a public server-based transaction filtering system, was a conceptual mistake, and alternatives like Stratum and Neutrino need more review before being widely implemented.Some users express concerns about custodial wallets and light wallets being harmful to the network, with one user suggesting that BIP 37 is the only scaling technology that has proven successful and could help against the trend of bitbanks. The Bitcoin Improvement Proposal 157/158 causes more traffic than BIP 37, but it provides the ability to filter blocks without depending on serving peers who may compromise your financial privacy.There were also concerns raised about the implementation of the BIP158 compact block filter, which may encourage address re-use if the server decides on the filter and false positive rate. However, Luke clarified that wallets do not need to match every single address they've used, only outstanding addresses that haven't been paid. This means that wallets can still use multiple addresses without facing the issue of having to wrap their key chains back to the beginning.Peter Todd discussed the proposed removal of NODE_BLOOM, arguing that privacy should be a matter of individual choice and that people should be allowed to provide this network service if they choose to. However, the patch would still allow users to provide bloom filtering services if they set -peerbloomfilters=1.Finally, there are concerns about the increasing popularity of less trustful and privacy-violating alternatives to Bitcoin and the push towards custodial solutions that may further erode privacy and trust in the cryptocurrency. Developers should make users aware of the implications of filter usage and provide them with choices depending on their privacy and bandwidth needs.
