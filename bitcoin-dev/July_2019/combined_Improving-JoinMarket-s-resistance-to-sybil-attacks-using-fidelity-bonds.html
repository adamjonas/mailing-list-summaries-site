Summary:

JoinMarket is a platform for coinjoins that aims to improve privacy in transactions but is vulnerable to Sybil attacks. One proposed solution is the use of fidelity bonds, which require makers to lock up bitcoin as collateral to ensure their participation in the transaction process.However, there are concerns about the risks associated with timelocking bitcoins and potential centralization risks associated with rented TXO fidelity bonds. Coin age has been suggested as an alternative to timelocks, providing significant sybil resistance. Other proposals include shared ownership rent schemes, using contract law as a half-baked implementation of Bitcoin cryptographic smart contracts, and using transaction malleability as protection.Another issue is the potential market of locking UTXOs in exchange for payment, which could drive down the cost of attack to manageable levels for attackers. Efforts to expand ECDSA to more than two-party n-of-n "true" multisignatures are ongoing, and aggregation is still possible to ensure by off-blockchain agreements, possibly with legal consequences.The discussion also includes various solutions for preventing rented TXOs from destroying privacy and preventing Sybil attacks, such as revocation schemes, requiring spendability by any key in a 'bond TXO package', and imposing penalties or revoking participation of TXOs in the market by publishing special signatures to spoil an attacker's consolidation via rent.ZmnSCPxj proposed a solution to the problem of rented TXO ownership on the blockchain through renting funds from another person and agreeing to pay him for this service of putting up BTC from his UTXO. The discussion also touched upon the issue of sybil attackers taking over the market for maker bots on decentralized exchanges, with arguments for and against.In a recent discussion, JoinMarket explored the possibility of accepting three methods of proving a sacrifice, including burning, timelocking, and aging. However, there are concerns regarding the potential attack against aged coins using address reuse. Aged coins must be on an unused address, or the value of the fidelity bond becomes zero.Users can use their past filters to determine which blocks may contain a spend from their UTXO. Dmitry Petukhov raised concerns about deanonymizing joins in JoinMarket through "maker outsourcing." While capturing a significant portion of the market, an attacker cannot rent out UTXO proofs and change the privacy properties due to the quadratic term in the fidelity bond formula.Additionally, JoinMarket takers always use multiple makers, so there is no chance for a single maker to deanonymize a coinjoin just because the takers have chosen them a lot. A sybil attacker would take a substantial quadratic hit in their effectiveness. It is important to note that pseudonymous entities cannot be sure how much of the market the attacker controls.Overall, the community is focused on improving privacy and security measures in decentralized systems through solutions such as fidelity bonds, time-locked addresses, and mandatory fees for makers, while considering potential risks such as increased complexity and entry costs. The issue of renting out transaction outputs (TXOs) has also been discussed, with concerns about the possibility of centralization and aggregation by off-blockchain agreements. Despite vulnerabilities, the benefits of using JoinMarket outweigh the risks, especially when implementing solutions to improve resistance to Sybil attacks.
