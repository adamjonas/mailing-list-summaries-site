Summary:

The BIP70 process may become complicated in certain business scenarios where the payment request needs to be canceled or rejected. Sending a "Cancel Payment Request" message and a "Reject Payment Request" message could be necessary in such cases. These scenarios require a different approach than the simple process described in BIP70.Merchants cannot send a "Cancel Payment Request" to the customer's wallet when paying with Bitcoin over HTTP. Similarly, sending a "Reject Payment Request" to the merchant is useless if the customer does not want to send it. However, in cases where the payment is no longer valid, the merchant can refund the money, or the customer can contact the merchant with a signed request to return the funds.Tim Tuxworth has raised concerns about common business scenarios that may require a "Cancel Payment Request" or a "Reject Payment Request". These include canceling a payment request before it is sent due to charging the wrong amount or sending the request to the wrong customer. Cancelling the transaction after the payment request is sent may occur due to alternative payment methods, insufficient funds, or changing one's mind.In an email exchange, Tuxworth questions whether BIP70 is limited to HTTP only and not applicable in face-to-face scenarios, real-time transactions like ticket sales or gambling, and socket and/or bluetooth connections. Christophe Biocca responds that adding a need for unsolicited merchant-to-consumer messages to the payment protocol wouldn't be usable in the majority of cases, and explains why.Tuxworth argues for the need for a "Cancel Payment Request" message and suggests that a "Reject Payment Request" may also be required in certain situations. The post author implemented BIP70 using combinations of either QR-code or NFC plus Bluetooth, and provides a link to a working preview app.In conclusion, the BIP70 process needs to have a provision for handling situations where the payment request needs to be canceled or rejected. The scenarios mentioned above are common and require a different approach than the simple process described in BIP70. Merchants and customers need to be aware of these scenarios and know the appropriate actions to take.
