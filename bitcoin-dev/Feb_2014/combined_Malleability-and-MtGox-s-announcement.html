Summary:

The conversation discussed concerns about market manipulation and lack of transparency at MtGox, a leading cryptocurrency exchange. Members suggested that off-list discussions about scalability and usability issues in Bitcoin could lead to potential market manipulation, and proposed making MtGox's code stack public under an AGPLv3 license to allow the community to find solutions. However, some members argued that it was not MtGox's obligation to publish their private code.In another thread, Pieter Wuille responded to an email regarding transaction malleability, stating that it had been a known issue for years, but it made infrastructure interactions with Bitcoin more challenging. He proposed defining a standardized way to identify transactions unambiguously using SHA256^2(normalized_tx + 0x01000000). Some members also discussed a potential attack on MtGox involving renaming transaction IDs before they were confirmed, leading to refunds to virtual Gox-wallets.There were also concerns about market manipulation being handed over to company insiders who discussed off-list, as well as the lack of transparency regarding MtGox's code. Some members suggested that if MtGox published their code under an AGPLv3 license, the community could work together on finding solutions and cementing their position as a market leader in transparent cryptocurrency trading.In addition, the conversation touched upon the issue of customers experiencing stuck transactions due to MtGox spending immature coins and producing transactions with invalid DER encodings. There were discussions on how attackers could exploit MtGox's stalled transactions with a direct connection to the exchange, including the use of a PHP script that returned base64 data for all the stalled transactions.
