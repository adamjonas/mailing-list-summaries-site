Summary:

A proposal has been shared on the bitcoin-dev mailing list to increase the throughput of the Bitcoin network while retaining the small block feature. The proposed solution involves allowing miners to include transactions until the block is filled, creating a "brick." Once fully filled, it would be broadcasted and nodes would have it on a separate fork. The accumulated PoW calculated using mathematics would determine if the full "brickchain" is valid as a block. This new structure aims to increase throughput without increasing block size, which could reduce congestion and keep transaction fees low.However, concerns have been raised about spam and centralization, and some argue that reduced settlement speed is a desirable feature, and layer 2 protocols like MWEB should be the focus instead. Layered financial systems are considered vastly superior, and L3 projects like TARO and RGB are building on Lightning with less risk.It is critical not to increase the workload of full-nodes, reducing the chance of some of them giving up and leading to negative centralization effects. Additionally, there are questions around where the block reward goes, how brick miners get the reward, and who gets the fees. Two weak block proposals, "bobtail" and "tailstorm," have been suggested but are unlikely to be implemented.Ultimately, the focus should be on layer 2 protocols that allow the ability to hold and transfer uncommitted transactions as pools/joins so that layer 1's decentralization and incentives can remain undisturbed. The proposed solution has its concerns about the workload of full-nodes and the potential to undermine layer 2 systems like LN. Therefore, it is suggested that layered financial systems are vastly superior, and protocols like MWEB could be used to build on lightning with less risk.While the idea proposed by the developer could flush mempools and increase throughput, it requires changes in the PoW algorithm and further definition and validation. The proposal also raises concerns around the workload of full-nodes and the potential to undermine layer 2 systems like Lightning Network (LN). Ultimately, the focus should be on refining the proposed idea further and determining its feasibility while also exploring other options such as layered financial systems like MWEB.
