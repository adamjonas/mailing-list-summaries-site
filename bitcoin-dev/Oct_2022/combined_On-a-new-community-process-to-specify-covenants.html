Summary:

Discussions on covenants within the Bitcoin technical community have been active, with proposals for new contracting primitives to enable multi-party contract protocols and empower Bitcoin's fundamental value propositions. However, there is a lack of consensus on any new set of primitives satisfying the requirements of known covenant-enabled use-cases. To address this, a covenant open specification process is proposed, similar to BOLTs or dlcspecs, with regular meetings and an open agenda. The proposal suggests having rotating meeting chairs and multiple covenant documentation archivists to enable scalable payments pools design like CoinPool. There are ongoing discussions about defining a communication channel for the group, with options including IRC, Slack, Discord, and Discourse. The proposal involves six phases with iterations to reach a consensus, aiming to narrow down the set of proposals worth pursuing.Antoine Riard has proposed a new covenant open specification process on the bitcoin-dev mailing list. The proposed process involves regular meetings, an open agenda, and documentation artifacts built with time driven by consensus. Additionally, Antoine has initiated a new repository on Github that aims to gather use cases and known design constraints for the proposed "Bitcoin Contracting Primitives WG" community process. Overall, the context provides insights into the ongoing efforts to improve Bitcoin's scalability, privacy, and functionality through discussions, proposals, and papers related to Bitcoin development, while also proposing new open and decentralized community processes for Bitcoin contracting primitives R&D.The bitcoin-dev mailing list recently discussed the use of `OP_` SCRIPT opcodes as a means of experimenting with new codes or modifying existing ones. Specifically, the conversation revolved around defining and providing examples of capabilities in relation to Bitcoin. The group proposed analyzing the trade-offs of different contracting primitives or even new Bitcoin fields as building blocks for various use-cases. Among the topics discussed was whether capabilities would be included in a covenants proposal. One example cited was the potential benefit of vaults functioning more effectively if payments were immediately locked up in them rather than being accepted first. The discussion also proposes using IRC for meetings and organizing in-person meetups every few meetings to facilitate communication.
