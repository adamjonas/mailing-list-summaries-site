Summary:

A group of Bitcoin developers recently proposed the establishment of a Bitcoin Legal Defense Fund (BLDF) to provide legal support for developers facing lawsuits related to their activities in the Bitcoin ecosystem. The fund is free and voluntary for developers to take advantage of if they so wish, and it will start with a corps of volunteer and part-time lawyers. The board of the fund will determine which lawsuits and defendants it will help defend. However, concerns were raised about the potential impact on developers' freedom to express their opinions, particularly in contentious issues.Participants in the discussions suggested that a more neutral name be chosen for the fund, potentially covering other cryptocurrencies than just Bitcoin, and referring to Satoshi's paper where everything started, to avoid confusion among new users. There were also suggestions to prevent the fund from financing lawsuits irrelevant to Bitcoin and people involved in such important funds commenting or influencing soft fork-related discussions. Suggestions were made to establish board operating principles that publicly share the criteria for how cases are chosen to protect against the board and fund influencing direction. Additionally, there were suggestions to create a fund to educate developers about the risks they face and how to mitigate them to avoid legal pressure entirely.Blockchain Commons has started a project led by Namcios and Christopher Allen to document the best practices of pseudonymous development. This project is part of their 2022 roadmap and is currently a work-in-progress. While the BLDF aims to defend developers, concerns have been raised about its potential impact on developers' freedom to express their opinions, particularly in contentious issues. Furthermore, the name of the fund has been suggested to be confusing for newbies, and suggestions have been made to expand the legal assistance beyond the US and Europe to accommodate the growing number of developers located in other countries.The BLDF is a non-profit entity established to minimize legal challenges faced by Bitcoin developers. Its primary objective is to protect developers from lawsuits related to their activities in the Bitcoin ecosystem by providing support such as finding and retaining defense counsel, developing litigation strategy, and paying legal bills. The board of the fund will determine which lawsuits and defendants it will help defend. While the main purpose of the fund is to defend developers, it remains to be seen if it will expand its services to include education. The first activity of the Fund is to coordinate the existing defense of the Tulip Trading lawsuit against certain developers alleging breach of fiduciary duty.
