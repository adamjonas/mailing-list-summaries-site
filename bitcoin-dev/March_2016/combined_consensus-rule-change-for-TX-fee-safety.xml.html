A recent Bitcoin transaction involving a 15 BTC (worth approximately $130,000) fee has sparked discussions about the need for safety rules in handling transaction fees during hard forks. Alice Wonder suggested implementing a safety rule for transaction fees in the next hard fork. Two proposed solutions were given to prevent incidents like this from happening again: TX fee may not be larger than the sum of outputs or TX fee per byte may not be larger than 4X largest fee per byte in the previous block.Henning Kopp agreed with the idea of implementing the rule as a safety mechanism in the client rather than doing a hard fork. However, there are legitimate use cases where one would want to pay a large transaction fee, such as convenience for miners to collect fees instead of sending back change on their own transactions.
