Summary:

The Bitcoin community has been engaging in discussions about implementing covenant functionality in the protocol. One proposal is CheckTemplateVerify (CTV), but there are concerns about its usage and maintenance burden, and lack of consensus around any concrete proposal for covenants. Developers suggest using a transitory soft fork to collect real-world usage data about proposals, while others raise safety risks and the need for more consensus before making permanent changes. There is no clear consensus on the best approach to covenants, with some suggesting a UASF client compatible with Speedy Trial release for BIP 119 as a better way to activate CTV.David Harding proposed an idea to automatically revert CTV as a consensus change with an option to renew after five years, allowing experimentation with new features without committing to them permanently. However, there are several downsides to consider, such as the risk of losing money for those who use CTV without adequate preparation and miners possibly censoring spends of the reverting opcode near its reversion date. The criteria for adding a covenant to Bitcoin include demonstrated use-cases and intent, alignment with Bitcoin's design and goals, technical feasibility, and well-reviewed implementation.There are multiple covenant designs that have been proposed, but none of them have actual implementations or sample usages, except for CTV. Despite its limits relative to more "general" designs, CTV is a simple and well-reviewed system. However, there are concerns about making CTV an automatically reverting consensus change with an option to renew, creating a big footgun and incentivizing miners to censor spends near its reversion date. Matt Corallo suggested starting with simple and general approaches and optimizing for demonstrated needs, acknowledging that some people prefer application-optimized designs.Throughout these discussions, there is a common theme of balancing innovation with careful design and ensuring that any proposed changes align with Bitcoin's goals and design principles. While there may be disagreement on the best approach, the importance of collecting real-world usage data and optimizing for demonstrated needs is emphasized. The Bitcoin community is actively engaged in exploring different covenant designs and considering the potential impacts of any proposed changes on the network's security and consensus.
