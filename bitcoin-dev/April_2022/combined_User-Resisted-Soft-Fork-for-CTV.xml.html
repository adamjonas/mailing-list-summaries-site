Summary:

The Bitcoin community is currently discussing the impact of an increase in use cases for Bitcoin on users and transaction fees. Zac Greenwood argues that any change made to Bitcoin must benefit all users and have transparent benefits, suggesting that only those changes that benefit layer 2 solutions, such as Lightning Network, are acceptable. This argument is being challenged in the case of Consensus-enforced Transaction Validity (CTV), with some members of the Bitcoin development community suggesting that changes benefiting the majority of users without negatively affecting the minority should still be implemented.The adoption of CTV could provide significant benefits to all users. Firstly, it would allow much easier and cheaper ways of improving security through wallet vaults, DLCs, channels, and other use cases. Secondly, CTV would unlock wallet vaults, making it substantially easier and cheaper to hold funds in a multi-key vault, which could lead to increased self-custodiation by users. However, Michael Folkson opposes the attempt to activate CTV without community consensus, arguing that changes to Bitcoin's consensus rules should only be made with community support.The controversy surrounding the attempted soft fork activation of CTV using an alternative client has also led to discussions on where active resistance efforts should take place. Michael Folkson recommends discussing this in an alternative client outside of Bitcoin Core, as getting anything consensus-related into Bitcoin Core is extremely difficult. He advises against directing people to have this discussion on a particular PR in the Bitcoin Core repo and suggests using the ##ursf channel on Libera IRC instead.ZmnSCPxj, a Bitcoin developer, expressed his support for the CTV proposal, stating that it could benefit layer 2 users and is simple to implement. In contrast, signature-based schemes require at least 32 bytes + change on the output and 64 bytes + change on the witness if they use the Taproot format. Moreover, he thinks that recursive covenants deserve more general research and thinking before enabling them. ZmnSCPxj views `OP_CTV` as the "AND" to the "OR" of MAST.Other discussions in the community include whether increasing the utility of the Bitcoin network through more use cases would benefit or harm it. Billy Tetrud believes that the CTV proposal provides substantial benefits to all users, but suggests creating a table of proposed opcodes and use cases with ratings for maturity and efficiency. Keagan McClelland suggests finding ways to activate non-invasive changes should be everyone's goal, while Ryan Grant expresses his belief that it is ethically inappropriate to make personal attacks on the trustworthiness of participants on a list.The Bitcoin-dev community is currently discussing the implementation of a User Resisted Soft Fork (URSF), which would provide direction and incentive to miners in opposition to the activation of a soft fork proposal on bitcoin core. Michael Folkson has set up a ##ursf channel on Libera IRC to monitor developments and discuss an additional release that may ultimately reject blocks that signal for CTV. He encourages constructive discussion and debate within the community and advises against rushing the process.Overall, while there are differing opinions within the Bitcoin development community about the implementation of CTV, there is consensus that any changes made must benefit all users and have well-defined and transparent benefits. Additionally, efforts are being made to prepare for the possibility of a URSF and to encourage constructive discussion and debate within the community.
