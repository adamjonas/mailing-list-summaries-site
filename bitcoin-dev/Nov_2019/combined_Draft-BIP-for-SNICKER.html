Summary:

Recently, a proposal was made by Riccardo Casatta in a Bitcoin development mailing list to solve the "watch-only issue" in CoinJoin transactions. The proposal suggests that the proposer creates two transactions: the CoinJoin transaction and another encrypted 1-to-1 transaction that spends their output to another of their key. When the receiver accepts the proposal, they can create their own 1-to-1 transactions spending their tweaked output to pure bip32 derived keys. However, this would add complexity and is not being considered at the moment due to cost implications.The SNICKER (Simple Non-Interactive Coinjoin with Keys for Encryption Reused) proposal aims to provide increased privacy benefits over traditional coinjoin through a two-party coinjoin implementation in Bitcoin, which trades off size for convenience/low effort/even zero effort mixing. However, there are no privacy guarantees, and frequent two-party mixes across a broad spectrum of wallets can severely degrade blockchain analysis. Nonetheless, the SNICKER recovery process requires only a small amount of communication between the hot and cold wallets, making it suitable for regular interaction with a watching-only wallet.The discussion on the bitcoin-dev mailing list revolves around the issue of watch-only wallets and the key tweak (`c` as per draft BIP) that must be known by Proposer and Receiver but must not be known by anyone else for the coinjoin function to work effectively. Classic watch-only use cases of monitoring a wallet in real-time with no privkey access are incompatible with this. However, recovering from zero information and monitoring in real-time as new SNICKER transactions arrive are deemed feasible.In conclusion, effective implementation of SNICKER requires addressing the issue of watch-only wallets and the key tweak `c`, which is not completely compatible with classic watch-only use cases. The recovery procedure from the SNICKER documentation involves transferring the set of candidate transactions produced to the corresponding cold wallet using the same means used for PSBTs, where the cold wallet could perform step #3 using its private keys and return a file/QRcode/whatever to the hot wallet telling it any shared secrets it found. This process allows the recovery of a cold wallet without compromising its security.
