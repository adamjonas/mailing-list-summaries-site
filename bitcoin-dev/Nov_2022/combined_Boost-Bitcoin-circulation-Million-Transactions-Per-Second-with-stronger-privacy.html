Summary:

The Sabu protocol is a proposed layer 2 solution for Bitcoin that aims to improve circulation and privacy. Unlike Lightning, another off-chain solution, the Sabu protocol eliminates overhead costs of transactions and motivates people to circulate transactions in the network while ensuring anonymity by not disclosing how much money is transferred. Transactions are recorded on the Bitcoin blockchain only in case of fraudulent activity by an issuer. The Sabu protocol offers cost savings, ease of use, privacy, and scalability compared to Lightning.However, critics have raised concerns about trustworthiness and security, with some members suggesting using Coinbase instead. To address these concerns, the article investigates real-life scenarios, general usages, corner cases, and the consequences of attacks or buggy implementations of the Sabu protocol while comparing Sabu with Lightning transactions. The author explains that Sabu has different design, network architecture, security model, and implementation compared to Lightning. The Sabu protocol was designed to improve Bitcoin TPS and privacy while providing self-sovereignty, and decentralized pure peer-to-peer communication network. There are discussions around vulnerabilities in the Bitcoin protocol, including sybil attacks and proxy attacks, and potential solutions such as using distributed/trusted nodes and certificates. The bitcoin-dev mailing list serves as a platform for developers and enthusiasts of the Bitcoin cryptocurrency to discuss these issues. While there is no easy solution, the community will continue to explore ways to make Bitcoin more secure, private, and accessible for everyone who uses it.The Sabu protocol proposes a fixed transaction fee and restricts arbitrary UTXO spending by the issuer or self-paying transactions. The Sabu network is pure peer-to-peer, so there is no centralization or routing, and it uses a doc-watcher network for mobile wallets to prevent fraudulent activity. The security model of Sabu is based on the guarantee transaction, which both issuer and creditor sign alongside the main transaction. However, there are concerns about security, trustworthiness, and feasibility.The vulnerability of sybil attacks was addressed in the context of a consensus protocol, and some members of the community have raised concerns about the assumptions made in the proposal and the feasibility of implementing such a protocol. ZmnSCPxj also raises concerns about the trustworthiness of the Gazin wallet, which Raymo plans to use as an implementation of the Sabu protocol.Despite these criticisms, Raymo remains committed to realizing the Sabu protocol and improving Bitcoin circulation while providing self-sovereignty and decentralized pure peer-to-peer communication network (with some resolvable privacy issues). One solution to prevent attacks on cryptocurrency transactions is the use of watchtowers, which can provide an always-on service to monitor and fix any attempted attacks in progress. By maintaining Sabu state, parties can prevent attacks and ensure the safety of their cryptocurrency transactions.
