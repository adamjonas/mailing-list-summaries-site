Summary:

In December 2012, Pieter Wuille and Amir Taaki had a conversation about hashed point serialization. They discussed the use of compressed format before going into the hash function. Wuille suggested standardizing on just compressed keys instead of uncompressed keys, as they take up more space in the blockchain without adding any value. He explained that software should support the compressed encoding, which involves just 0x02 or 0x03 followed by the 32-byte encoding of X, and can use any representation internally. Decoding compressed public keys is somewhat harder, as Y must be reconstructed, but this is only necessary when someone wants to import an extended public key for watch-only wallets.Taaki had also sent an email to bitcoin-development at lists.sourceforge.net suggesting that accounts should be labeled instead of numbered in BIP 32 HD wallets. Wuille responded that incrementing numbers were assigned to detect new accounts that come into use for disaster recovery purposes, but he would not mind adding the ability for non-standard derivations using arbitrary strings if recoverability is not compromised.Overall, the conversation and email exchange centered around improving the practicality and efficiency of Bitcoin wallet account categorization and point serialization.
