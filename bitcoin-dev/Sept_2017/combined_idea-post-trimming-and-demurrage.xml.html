Summary:

On the Bitcoin-dev mailing list, Patrick Sharp proposed an idea to limit the maximum length or size of the blockchain by introducing demurrage and trimming. He argued that without such limits, the blockchain becomes unsustainable in the long run and more centralized as it grows unwieldy. Sharp proposed limiting the blockchain's length to either 2^18 blocks (slightly less than five years) or 2^19 blocks (slightly less than ten years), and trimming the oldest block(s) beyond this limit each time a new block is mined. This would keep the costs of miners balanced with the costs of users and prevent the blockchain from becoming too large.However, developer ZmnSCPxj responded saying that implementing demurrage on the mainchain is impossible, and it would be better to put additional features like demurrage on a sidechain instead. He suggested using OP_CHECKLOCKTIMEVERIFY to create an "in trust for" address that would prevent spending until a certain block height or date has passed.In response, Patrick Sharp withdrew his proposal for demurrage and trimming, but he suggested another idea. He proposed storing the hash of the current or previous UTXOs in the block header so that pruned nodes can verify their UTXOs are accurate without having to check the full chain. He also suggested including a snapshot of the UTXOs every x blocks to make more efficient use of space on the blockchain.During the discussion, developer Greg Maxwell also proposed using OP_CHECKLOCKTIMEVERIFY to create an "in trust for" address, and he recommended putting additional features like demurrage on a sidechain rather than on the mainchain.
