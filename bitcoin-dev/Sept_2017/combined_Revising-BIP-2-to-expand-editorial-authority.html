Summary:

During discussions on the bitcoin-dev mailing list, Luke Dashjr proposed modifying BIP 2 to allow editors to merge changes without involving authors. However, Bryan Bishop expressed concerns that even minor revisions could impact the meaning of the text and suggested exercising caution by mentioning edits in the changelog or notifying primary authors before merging. Sjors Provoost suggested @mentioning authors in the PR and giving them a week to object before merging. The need for careful consideration when making changes to BIPs was highlighted, as small alterations can have significant impacts on the meaning of the text. Cdecker suggested having a sign-off mechanism for changes to BIPs, where 2-3 community members attest that the meaning of the BIP did not change. Luke prepared a draft PR for the proposed modification to BIP 2, allowing editors to merge spelling corrections and other obvious editorial changes without involving the authors. However, if anyone opposes this change, they have been requested to speak up within the next month. The discussion also suggested the idea of the BIP editor signing and timestamping all changes for clarity on who made the change. The draft PR can be found on Github at https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/pull/596.
