Summary:

The scalability issues of the Bitcoin network were discussed on the Bitcoin-dev mailing list. One member, Cserveny Tamas, suggested that increasing block size or reducing complexity would be necessary for scaling with traffic and lowering fees. Another member disagreed, stating that reducing complexity would not compromise security as long as confirmation is adjusted accordingly. The discussion also touched on the issue of miners adding capacity to the network. Tamas claimed that it would only increase their share of the block reward without increasing transaction speed. However, it was pointed out that adding more space to blocks does increase throughput speed without affecting the block reward. Tom Zander provided further insights on his personal blog and vlog channels, explaining that with the introduction of xthin/compactblocks, the network bottleneck has been removed, allowing for continuous parallel validation of transactions.The writer of the discussion is contemplating how to scale blockchain and suggests horizontal scaling if the problem can be partitioned. Two partitioning key alternatives are considered: ordering on inputs or ordering on transaction ids. Both methods would affect block reward and complexity, which needs to be adjusted. Activating partitions could be done gradually according to a schedule. The writer acknowledges that there are pros and cons to both partition keys and asks for opinions on the matter.In addition to discussing potential solutions, the discussion also highlighted concerns about rapidly growing fees and waiting times for Bitcoin transactions. Some members suggested increasing block size up to 40MB, while others believed that technology would continue to push limits as successful products like YouTube and Netflix have done. The benefits of splitting the ledger were compared to IOTA's system, but more details were needed to judge its effectiveness. Ultimately, the discussion demonstrates that there are multiple perspectives on how to address the scalability issues facing the Bitcoin network, and that further exploration and testing is needed before any definitive conclusions can be drawn.
