Summary:

Mark Friedenbach, on the Bitcoin development mailing list, shared his desire to abstract out the UTXO and block databases so that a variety of key/value stores could be used as a backend. Mike Hearn responded by mentioning HyperLevelDB, a forked version of LevelDB with some changes, and Sophia, a competitor to LevelDB. In 2013, Hearn expressed his opinion on the lack of development in creating a better format for Bitcoin wallets. He stated that no one has written code to use a better format and migrate old wallets. However, several alternatives have emerged over the years such as the Multi-Account Hierarchy for Deterministic Wallets (MAHDW) and the Blockstream Green Wallet. MAHDW offers enhanced privacy features and allows users to create multiple accounts within a single wallet. Meanwhile, the Blockstream Green Wallet provides a user-friendly interface and added security features, such as two-factor authentication.Despite these advancements, the adoption of new Bitcoin wallet formats has been slow, with many users still relying on HD wallets. The reluctance to switch may be due to concerns around compatibility, difficulty in migrating funds, or simply a lack of awareness about the benefits of alternative wallet formats. In conclusion, while there have been some developments in creating better Bitcoin wallet formats since Mike Hearn's comment in 2013, there is still much room for improvement. The adoption of new wallet formats remains a challenge, but it is crucial to ensure the security and usability of Bitcoin funds in the long run.
