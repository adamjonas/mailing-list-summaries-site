Summary:

Bitcoin developers have been discussing ways to prevent Sybil attacks on the network. While it was noted that using fake addresses to launch an attack would be difficult, attackers could still use other techniques such as BGP hijacking or IP spoofing. The suggestion of removing the IRC bootstrapping mechanism was also considered to prevent botnets from controlling nodes on the network. However, there were concerns about the effectiveness of this approach in preventing all attacks.Pieter Wuille, a developer for the Satoshi bitcoin client, created a specialized address manager to protect against Sybil attacks. The new design includes two tables for addresses that have had connections with and untried/new addresses, separated into several limited-size buckets. Despite concerns about the possibility of a Sybil attack from a large botnet filling all buckets in both tables, Gavin Andresen expressed his support for Pieter's design and suggested including it in the upcoming 0.6 release of Bitcoin.The discussion also revolved around the possibility of adding trusted nodes or a single connection to the 'good' network to prevent Sybil attacks rather than increasing outbound connections. Current mechanisms of DNS and hardcoded seed nodes are susceptible to blocking. The developers recommended combining Bitcoin with Tor and anti-censorship tools or using nodes/addr.txt to address the issue. A DHT can also maintain the list through node expiry rules, but it wouldn't be any more resistant.In an email exchange between Gavin Andresen and Michael Hendricks, they discussed the susceptibility of the Bitcoin network to Sybil attacks. A command-line option for increasing the maximum number of outbound connections could be added to decrease susceptibility. Additionally, the possibility of removing the IRC bootstrapping mechanism was considered. In an email conversation between Gregory Maxwell and Michael Hendricks, Gregory pointed out that someone who can spin up a large number of nodes as TCP forwarders to a system they control can capture all of a node's outbound connections. However, Michael still believed that the new address manager was a valuable improvement and should be included in the next release. Finally, Michael noted that there is room to allow more outbound connections in some circumstances.
