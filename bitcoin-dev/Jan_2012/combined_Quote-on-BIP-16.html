Summary:

Gavin Andresen, a prominent figure in the Bitcoin community, has raised concerns about the usability of very long Bitcoin addresses. He argues that addresses longer than 70 characters are not workable for regular users and developers due to difficulties in copying, pasting, scanning, generating QR codes, upgrading website or database code that deals with Bitcoin addresses, and more.The concern is related to the current solution for multisig transactions, which is P2SH (Pay-to-Script-Hash). P2SH reduces output sizes to the absolute minimum without inflating total data size, making it more efficient than P2S (Pay-to-Script). P2S involves encoding the full script specification instead of just its hash, resulting in much longer addresses. Without some form of P2SH, a personal choice for payment methods would require the use of P2S.However, there is also debate over the implementation of BIP 13/16/17 and the potential abuse of plain multi-sig. While longer addresses may offer improved security, they may also pose challenges for users and developers. The debate around very long Bitcoin addresses highlights the importance of usability in the adoption and use of cryptocurrencies.In a conversation between Amir Taaki and Gavin Andresen, the former questioned the possibility of having a 70-byte long address without using a P2SH scheme. However, Gavin clarified that P2SH prevents the need for such long addresses. As the Bitcoin community continues to evolve, it will be interesting to see how this issue is resolved.
