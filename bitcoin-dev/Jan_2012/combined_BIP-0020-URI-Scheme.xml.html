Summary:

Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) 20, which was finalized according to BIP 1 standards in January 2011, is still in draft status due to a lack of consensus. There has been disagreement over its validity among members of the Bitcoin mailing list, with one side claiming it had already been implemented and the other arguing against extraneous information in the wiki version.Luke-Jr pointed out that the implementation of BIP 21 had already been implemented in many clients throughout 2011, but Bitcoin-Qt was considered lagging behind and not relevant. Bitcoin Wallet for Android only implemented parts of this specification, leaving out the hexadecimal and exponent notations due to their redundancy. Lastly, the "send" feature was not yet implemented.During a discussion on Bitcoin transactions, Wladimir argued for minimal completeness in the spec and against redundancy, while declining to reopen debate over BIP 20 due to a biased poll and lack of links to an alternative version. He acknowledged that if new transaction types are added, the spec would need to be extended but not with multiple ways of writing the same thing. Amir Taaki suggests revisiting the URI scheme for revision once P2SH is deployed and eventually replacing or superseding BIP 20. It is worth noting that the vote on the spec took place three months after a consensus was reached and multiple implementations existed.
