The conversation revolves around the use of hash functions in generating QR codes for payment requests. The first suggestion is to truncate the output of SHA256 to 128 bits, but it's pointed out that the length should be determined by the person generating the QR code. It's suggested that implementers experiment with different lengths to find the optimum balance between security and convenience, which could depend on the transaction amount.
