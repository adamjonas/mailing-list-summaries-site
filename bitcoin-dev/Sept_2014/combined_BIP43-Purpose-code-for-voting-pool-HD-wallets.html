Summary:

In the Bitcoin community, BIPs (Bitcoin Improvement Proposals) are used to propose changes or improvements to the network. In this context, the HD multisig structure that will be used for Bitcoin wallets used for voting pools has reserved two purpose codes. Two draft information BIPs have been documented - one for the wallets suitable for storing bulk bitcoin deposits and the other for storing colored coin deposits. The primary difference is that bulk deposit wallets use cold storage and are allowed to incur significant administrative overhead, whereas colored coin wallets do not use cold storage because they must be capable of being generated on the fly.Alan, a member of the Bitcoin-development mailing list, suggests adding a "Purpose" node to identify different tree structures in a wallet file. He proposes a non-hardened first-layer derivation for common trees and a "No-collision mode" for multisig BIP32 trees. These could be supported by changing the BIP43 "Purpose" index, and wallet software could recognize and react to the Purpose node for various tree structures. Gregory Maxwell sent an email to a group of people regarding a proposal related to the BIP process. He believes that the process is broken in the case of informational BIPs because proposals that require action from others do not logically belong in the same process as purely informational proposals that do not. It is possible that non-subscribed users were unable to access the file through sourceforge due to an error with the web interface. Bryan's contact information was also included in the email.Justus Ranvier sent an email attaching two draft information BIPs and requested feedback from the recipients of his email, both privately and on the list. No additional commentary was provided in the email. Justus Ranvier also signed off a message related to the HD multisig structure with his public key ID.
