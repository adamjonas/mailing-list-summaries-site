The role of miners and full nodes in enforcing consensus rules in Bitcoin is complex and multifaceted. Consensus rules are enforced by both miners and full nodes, with full nodes validating transactions and blocks according to the rules and miners adding validated transactions to the blockchain.Soft fork signaling is often considered voting, but it is simply an indication of readiness for a soft fork. Without majority hash power support, activation of a soft fork will result in a chain split if the majority of miners decide to mine a chain that does not follow the consensus rules. Game theory can provide insight into the various outcomes possible in this scenario.Eric Voskuil recently expressed his inability to provide evidence of a valid game theoretic model applicable to Bitcoin, and clarified that mining pools and exchanges do not necessarily hold discussions before signaling for a soft fork.
