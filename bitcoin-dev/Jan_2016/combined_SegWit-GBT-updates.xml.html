There is a debate over whether "default_witness_commitment" should be included in the standard protocol or not. While some argue that it could lead to centralization of mining pools and vulnerability to DoS attacks, others believe that it can simplify adoption and provide a known-correct value. The decision may depend on specific implementation and goals, as libblkmaker can handle the heavy lifting for mining software.Luke-jr has created an initial draft of a Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) for updating getblocktemplate for segregated witness. Interested parties can find the draft on GitHub at the provided link. Luke-jr is seeking feedback, particularly regarding sigoplimits handling. However, he notes that libblkmaker's reference implementation is currently incompatible with the "last output" rule in this BIP. He expresses gratitude in advance for any feedback received.In an email conversation between Luke Dashjr and Cory Fields, they discuss the default witness commitment for GBT (GetBlockTemplate) protocol. Dashjr argues that omitting the default witness commitment from the standard protocol would be better in terms of security, but Fields believes that providing a known-working commitment would be beneficial for adoption. Dashjr also expresses concern about introducing bugs into working code and the need to consider the sign bit when serializing heights. The conversation also addresses potential DoS vectors and the use of libblkmaker to handle the burden on mining software.The conversation between Dashjr and Fields also addresses potential security concerns and the use of libblkmaker to handle the burden on mining software. They also discuss the omission of the "default_witness_commitment" key in the Bitcoin protocol. While the two approaches of having clients create their own commitment or providing a known-working commitment are not at odds with each other, allowing for simpler cases could encourage laziness and complicate server-side implementation, as well as increase the risk of bugs in the process. Despite this, Fields supports the argument for leaving out the key, while Dashjr suggests leaving it as a bitcoind-specific extension to encourage adoption.In summary, the discussion focuses on whether the "default_witness_commitment" should be included in the standard protocol for GBT.
