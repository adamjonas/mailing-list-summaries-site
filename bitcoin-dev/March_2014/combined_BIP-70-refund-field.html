Summary:

In March 2014, members of the Bitcoin development community engaged in email conversations discussing various topics related to the Bitcoin payment protocol. One of the primary issues discussed was the lack of an appropriate time limit for refunds and warranty expiration times, which could make it difficult for wallets to keep track of refunds for payments made years ago. Andreas Schildbach and Mike Hearn proposed a fixed expiry time for warranties in the spec, with two and a half years being suggested as an appropriate length of time to allow for shipping.However, concerns were raised about whether there was enough RAM available for such a lengthy period. To address this problem, there were suggestions to add a refund field that embeds a PaymentDetails structure or implement a wallet-specific swapping process. The conversation also highlighted the need for a protocol that deals with the concept of business relationships rather than just individual payment requests. Suggestions included using tags in contact lists and stealth addresses with a per-order UUID as solutions to improve scalability and privacy.The speakers emphasized the importance of creating a clean and efficient protocol for repeated payments in both directions, with intelligent wallet UIs to display refunded payments in a user-friendly manner. Incremental development was seen as important, but caution was advised against adding too many features at once and creating insurmountable problems. There were also discussions surrounding the potential implementation of BIP70 by BitPay or BopShop and Trezor, with some expressing their lack of enthusiasm towards it until the specification went further than what was possible before. Additionally, there were proposals for recurring payments from the KillBill folks that BitPay has yet to review.In another email exchange, members discussed the issue of refunds in relation to BIP70, suggesting a new protocol be implemented that focuses on business relationships rather than individual requests. The current payment protocol was deemed to have a narrow scope that does not fit the concept of business relationships. The conversation highlights the need to expand BIP70's scope beyond just basic buyer-to-seller relationships to create a protocol that deals with the concept of business relationships.Overall, the email conversations demonstrate the Bitcoin community's efforts to improve the payment protocol by addressing its limitations and proposing new solutions.
