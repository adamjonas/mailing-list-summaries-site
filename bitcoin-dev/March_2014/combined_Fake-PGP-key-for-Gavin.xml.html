Summary:

On March 22, 2014, Mike Hearn warned Bitcoin users about the creation of fake PGP keys to sign popular crypto software, which could be used for MITM attacks. Users were advised to verify their downloads and ensure they are using the correct key. The production of these fake keys is likely due to corporate industrial espionage or organized crime outfits rather than intelligence agencies. Developers in the censorship circumvention problem space have experienced similar attacks in the past, with speculation that intelligence agencies may have motives beyond acquiring Bitcoins for attempting a cybil attack against developers. Gavin Andresen suggests that Windows binaries be codesigned, as AV scanners learn key reputations to reduce false positives. Linux does not support X.509 code signing, but real operating systems use package managers with PGP instead of pre-compromised X.509. In an email conversation, Gavin Andresen confirmed that -setup.exe installers are Authenticode signed.
