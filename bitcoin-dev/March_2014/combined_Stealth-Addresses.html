Summary:

Developers have been discussing the use of stealth addresses in Bitcoin transactions to allow for low or no overhead communication between senders and receivers without revealing their identities. Technical issues related to the use of pubKeys in multisig and ECDH mechanisms for generating shared secrets to recover funds were discussed. A suggestion was made to include a second pubkey in stealth addresses for communication purposes and outsource scanning the blockchain to a third-party. There were also debates over whether to integrate stealth addresses into OpenPGP keys or x.509 certs. Developers aim to improve Bitcoin's privacy and security features while finding a neutral and appropriate name for new features.During a conversation, members of a cryptocurrency development group discussed possible names for a new feature. The group considered "stealth addresses" before proposing "privacy addresses" and "incognito addresses". Ultimately, they decided to use "stealth addresses" for public marketing purposes and "incognito addresses" within the wallet software itself. They also discussed ensuring backwards compatibility for any updates to the address format and raised concerns about the term "reusable address" and its benefits. Some members suggested using "public address" instead as common terminology.In a separate conversation, members discussed the color and potential names for a shed, including "privacy address". However, some felt this name was too closely associated with "private key", and had negative connotations with American military hardware and criminal activity. The conversation ended without a decision on a name.There was also discussion surrounding the term "routing address" and its association with network routing rather than banking. Members expressed difficulty in translating it to local languages, causing concern over its use in finance. Throughout the conversations, the group showed consideration for how their choices might be perceived and understood by users, emphasizing the importance of clear communication and avoiding potentially negative associations. Developers are focused on ensuring efficient, private, reliable, and non-interactable payments while maintaining ease of use for all users.
