Summary:

A proposal to modify the RBF eviction policy was discussed in a bitcoin-dev conversation. The proposed change would allow for the feerate separation between transactions in the mempool to be calculated, enabling the sweep transaction+parent to be evicted by the fee bumped replacement if it has a different feerate. This would create a higher fee block template overall for miners.The email thread also discussed Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) 125, which allows users to increase the fees of unconfirmed transactions. However, issues arise when the receiver spends from an unconfirmed output, leaving the sender to pay an unbounded amount or not fee bump at all. To promote a stable fee market, a proposal was made to increase the utility of BIP125 by making transactions that spend an unconfirmed BIP125 output non-standard by specifying a max chain depth of 1 for any BIP125 transactions. This means receivers will need to wait until the transaction has confirmed before spending from it.To support a healthy fee marketplace, another proposal suggested creating a rule that to spend from an unconfirmed BIP125 output, the transaction feerate needs to be higher than its unconfirmed parent's effective feerate. This could potentially solve most of the problematic low feerate sweeps and preserve the ability for receivers to use CPFP if they wish.Rhavar proposed a BIP to prevent chaining off replaceable transactions and simplify feebump logic to create a more stable fee market. Critics have called the proposal paternalistic and contrary to miner income, but Rhavar argues that it is no more paternalistic than non-BIP125 transactions and that miners who use the policy would make more money than those who don't. The proposal aims to create a more efficient fee marketplace while addressing the issue of receivers spending from unconfirmed outputs.However, one individual questioned the desirability of the proposal, suggesting that it is better to simply replace the unconfirmed payment rather than trying to increase the fees. Instead, it is proposed that a BIP be created which strongly advises parties to not spend unconfirmed outputs from third parties while making a payment to third parties in order to achieve the same end.The discussion also highlighted the challenges faced by low-fee transactions, especially in the case of batched sends with multiple independent receivers. The proposal aims to promote a stable fee market and enable users to dynamically increase their fees when dealing with withdrawals. Overall, there are differing opinions on the best approach, and it remains to be seen which proposals will be implemented.
