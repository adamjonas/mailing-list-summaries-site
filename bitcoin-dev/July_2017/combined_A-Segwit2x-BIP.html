Summary:

The proposed hard fork of the Bitcoin network has been met with mixed responses and ongoing discussions. The latest Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) suggests an increase in non-witness block space with Segwit activation, but concerns have been raised about urgency, testing, and potential fund loss. Some have suggested alternative solutions such as Spoonet or BIP103. There are also debates about soft-forking solutions to the N² hashing problem, including a maximum transaction size limit. While not ideal, it is seen as a good start, but there are still technical and ethical issues with the hard fork proposal. Despite these concerns, the Bitcoin community continues to discuss and debate the best path forward.There is also disagreement among developers about the impact of larger block sizes on fees, split risks without replay protection, and proper testing before implementing a hard fork. Some argue that code to support the hard fork has been tested for over a year, while others claim that this is not true and that the current code has not been properly tested yet. Additionally, there are timing issues with the BIP, which could result in two chains without replay protection.In recent discussions on the Bitcoin-dev mailing list, Jorge Timón expressed his opinion that any hard fork should wait for at least a year after the release of tested code by some implementation. However, Tom Zander responded with good news stating that code to support 2x, which is the hard fork part of the proposal, has been out and tested for much longer than one year. Nevertheless, there are still concerns about the urgency and lack of detail in the proposal, which could lead to transaction reversals and other issues. Despite Core's support, many people would still oppose the hardfork, which could lead to its failure. It is unclear what actions those opposing the hardfork could take, with or without Core's support. More discussion and testing is needed before any final decisions can be made. The proposed hard fork of the Bitcoin network is being deployed with "emergency" speed, which could result in two chains without replay protection. The BIP should be modified to provide evidence and justification for the timeline that is consistent with the level of risk the network would bear if enacted.In conclusion, while the proposal satisfies the requests of a large part of the Bitcoin community for increasing the non-witness block space coupled with the activation of Segwit, it also carries risks and limitations that need to be addressed and justified properly. More than 80% of miners and users are willing to go in the Segwit2x direction, but there are still many uncertainties and disagreements surrounding the proposed hard fork of the Bitcoin network.
