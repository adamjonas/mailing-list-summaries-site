Summary:

In various email exchanges and discussions on the bitcoin-dev mailing list, members of the Bitcoin community have debated the adoption of drivechains and sidechains as solutions for scalability. While some argue that they are necessary experiments, others criticize them for compromising Bitcoin's security guarantees and not being scalable improvements. There have also been disagreements over roadmaps, with some advocating for their creation to better synchronize efforts and others criticizing them for implying promises and lacking reliable communication channels.Bitcoin developer Paul Sztorc has suggested that the 2015 scaling roadmap is outdated and should be revised. His draft of a new roadmap includes completed items such as VersionBits (BIP 9), Compact Blocks (BIP 152), FIBRE Network, Check Sequence Verify (BIP 112), Segregated Witness (BIP 141) and Lightning Network, while upcoming improvements include Transaction Compression, Schnorr Signature Aggregation and Drivechain. In an email exchange with Chris Stewart, Sztorc discussed the readiness of drivechains for Bitcoin core; Stewart suggested there was still much to do, but private individuals and businesses could help parallelise the remaining work due to incentives for competitiveness.In various email exchanges and discussions among Bitcoin developers, the topic of roadmaps, scaling proposals, and security concerns have been debated. Some developers have criticized the current roadmap for not being updated and relevant to new high-likelihood improvements, while others argue against hard deadlines on functionality and grand top-down roadmaps. There have also been debates about the security model of sidechains and drivechains, with some expressing concerns about miners potentially stealing bitcoins. Additionally, the usage of anyone-can-spend outputs in Drivechain has been discussed, with contrasting opinions about its riskiness.Bitcoin-dev mailing list, the two debated the merits of Drivechains as a scaling solution for Bitcoin. CryptAxe argued that Drivechains offer a different security model than other contracts and that most users will not have to go through the Drivechain withdrawal process. However, Tao Effect expressed concern about the use of anyone-can-spend outputs in Drivechain, which is encouraged by design and could compromise Bitcoin's security. The discussion also touched on the need for better options for sidechains, and the challenges associated with implementing such solutions.Bitcoin developer Paul Sztorc believes that Bitcoin's 2015 scaling roadmap needs to be revised as it is now 19 months old. Sztorc suggests removing completed items and introducing new innovations and approaches, such as Transaction Compression and Schnorr Signature Aggregation, and updating deadlines and projections. If these capacity improvements are not sufficient, then a hard fork may be necessary to increase the block size by a moderate amount. Gregory Maxwell, another Bitcoin expert, expressed his opinion that the security model of drivechains/sidechains is not compatible with Bitcoin's and instead suggested supporting experimentation with alternative technologies rather than promoting them as major solutions for Bitcoin.In an email thread, Greg Slepak and Chris Stewart discussed the security risks associated with Drivechains, highlighting the ongoing debate over the best way to ensure security in cryptocurrency transactions. In a discussion between Tao Effect and CryptAxe on the bitcoin-dev mailing list, they debated the security model of adding consensus rules via softfork in Drivechain. While both agreed that it was a different security model, CryptAxe disagreed that it would encourage more risky behavior. The discussions also touched on topics such as the validation cost to auditors, increased miner trust for preventing the other chains' coins from being illegally transferred, communication about research directions and projects, and criticism being misinterpreted as hostile in Bitcoin's review environment. Overall, there is a consensus that communication and collaboration are crucial in Bitcoin development, but opinions on how to achieve it vary.
