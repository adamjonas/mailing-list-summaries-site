Summary:

Bitcoin developers are discussing the potential quantum threat to Bitcoin and Taproot's security. While some argue that there is no significant difference between pre-Taproot and post-Taproot in terms of Bitcoin's vulnerability to quantum computing, others believe that Taproot loses an important safety protection against quantum. The loss of hash-based addresses would make it difficult to protect Bitcoin against quantum attacks, but the use of Taproot keys with hashes of their internal keys provides better quantum resistance than legacy outputs.In addition, developers have discussed measuring progress in quantum computing-based EC key cracking. Breaking a single key may take months initially, but once a quantum computer is available, it becomes a straightforward engineering challenge. The consensus seems to be that once a QC-resistant spending procedure has been developed, it could be added as a backup spending policy as a new tapleaf version. Adding another hash would be redundant and not necessary.There is debate about how quickly an attacker can steal coins using a quantum computer, but regardless, it does not equate to mining and would not be seen as acceptable since many old outputs still have owners with the keys. Exposed keys allow for ring signatures over outputs, which can enable private proof of funds via Provisions. However, there are concerns about the security of hash-based addresses in light of quantum computing.Developers also discussed the possibility of quantum computing being used to break an ECC key, with some proposing creating output scripts with lower difficulty that could be used to measure the progress of QC-based EC key cracking as an early warning system. While some members believe there is no significant difference between pre-Taproot and post-Taproot in terms of Bitcoin's vulnerability to quantum computing, others are concerned about the potential risks.The usage and development of Bitcoin Core in non-English speaking parts of the world were also discussed. Bitcoin Core is a decentralized, open-source software used to operate the Bitcoin network, and its development involves contributions from developers worldwide who work together to improve its performance, security, and usability. Understanding how people in non-English speaking regions use and develop Bitcoin Core is crucial for its continued growth and success.Overall, the issue of quantum computing and Bitcoin remains a topic of discussion among developers. While there are concerns about the vulnerability of existing addresses to QC, there are also arguments against quantum computing itself and its inevitability. Planning for quantum computing should be limited to a provable and written ability to upgrade if necessary.
