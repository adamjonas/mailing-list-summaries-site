Summary:

Peter Wuille has proposed changes to the Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) 62 rules, including better names for the rules, clarification on the interaction of BIP62 with P2SH, and that known hash types are required despite not being part of DER. However, Peter Todd expressed skepticism about the use of nVersion==3, preferring to disconnect TX and block version. Additionally, a discussion has been initiated regarding the "clean stack" requirement in BIP62, which disallows passing more inputs to a script than necessary. A pull request has been submitted to clarify this issue in BIP62.Mike Hearn and Pieter agree that the desire for soft forks sometimes leads to ugly hacks. They also believe that hard forks should be possible when they are useful. For introducing a new transaction type that won't be used before the soft fork takes place, it's not necessary to take the risk of a hard fork. The problem of using scripts-which-are-not-scripts in Bitcoin can be solved by implementing a hard fork upgrade known as "script 2.0." Peter Todd expresses skepticism towards the choice of nVersion==3 and argues that there is no reason to believe that future block.nVersion increases will have anything to do with transactions. Jeff Garzik agrees with Todd's argument and shares his previous experience with bumping tx version due to an increase in block version which resulted in ambiguity. Although Garzik acknowledged the change, he prefers to disconnect TX and block version.
