The email thread revolves around the comparison of Taproot's design with simpler alternatives. It also proposes an alternative path to deploying the technologies described in BIP-340, BIP-341, and BIP-342, and suggests a modification to Taproot to reduce some of the overhead. The email highlights that Taproot incentivizes protocols and implementations that can use the key path by ensuring that the cheapest way of spending is also the most private one.However, an article raises concerns about the Taproot upgrade for Bitcoin and its potential alternatives. The author questions if Taproot is more cost-effective or private than using MAST and Schnorr separately. They argue that while Taproot is more efficient when delegation occurs at the top level, it may not be a good assumption as delegation can occur at different levels. Additionally, the article suggests that a semi-randomized MAST structure could increase privacy.On the other hand, a group of anonymous developers has raised concerns about the Taproot upgrade for Bitcoin, questioning its benefits over simpler alternatives and urging a more conservative deployment plan. The developers propose three separate soft forks for Merkle Branch Witnesses, Schnorr Signatures, and Taproot and Graftroot to enable Taproot as a follow-up to changes one and two. They also suggest modifying Taproot's specification with a public NUMS optimization to allow users who don't need the key path to have a cheaper spend path. However, the group does not strongly advocate against deploying Taproot at this review cycle and acknowledges the well-designed mechanisms of TapScript and MAST. Overall, there are varying opinions on the merits of Taproot and its alternatives. While some believe that Taproot is efficient and incentivizes protocols and implementations that use the key path, others raise concerns about its assumptions and suggest modifications for increased privacy and decreased fees. The proposed alternatives and deployment plans highlight the need for careful consideration and evaluation before implementing any significant upgrades to the Bitcoin network.
