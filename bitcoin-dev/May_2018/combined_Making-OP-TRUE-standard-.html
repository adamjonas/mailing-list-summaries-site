Summary:

In recent discussions among Bitcoin developers, a major concern has been the issue of predicting future fees in the Lightning protocol. Rusty Russell proposed including a 546 satoshi OP_TRUE output in commitment transactions to allow for minimal fees and CPFP, but this would require P2SH at present as raw OP_TRUE is non-standard. Johnson Lau suggested creating a "0 fee tx with exactly one OP_TRUE output" standard to ensure that CPFP will always be necessary, but Peter Todd argued that this won't work as a zero-fee transaction won't get relayed even with CPFP. The discussion also touched upon the possibility of using OP_CSV with a relative locktime of zero to enforce RBF on spending transactions. Meanwhile, the Lightning network is also facing challenges in predicting future fees and finding efficient ways to handle fee-bumping.Bitcoin developers have proposed various solutions to address these issues, including using OP_TRUE outputs, CPFP, SIGHASH_NOINPUT, and consensus rules for expected behavior in transactions. Luke Dashjr suggested a consensus rule and a template to address the potential risk of being unable to use OP_TRUE to CPFP a transaction if someone spends it with a low-fee non-RBF transaction. Some developers have suggested implementing a more flexible SIGHASH system, such as SIGHASH_NOINPUT, to allow for easier addition of inputs and outputs. Additionally, there have been proposals to prevent coins from being spent in the same block they are created, but some argue that SIGHASH_NOINPUT could address this problem.The discussions also involved proposals to replace the current RBF policy with a new rule that requires the replacement package fee rate to be higher than the original transaction's fee rate and an absolute fee bump that covers the min-fee rate times the size of the mempool churn's data size. Rusty Russell suggested implementing a solution that delays processing of transactions by 30-60 seconds if a replacement doesn't meet certain criteria but increases the feerate by at least minrelayfee to address the DoS potential directly.There were also discussions about using ANYONECANPAY to sign transactions and having a more flexible SIGHASH system to allow people to easily add more inputs and outputs. However, some proposals would require policy changes or P2SH, while others may not be suitable for multi-transaction contracts due to invalidating the txid of the root commitment. The group anticipates that Eltoo and SIGHASH_NOINPUT will provide alternative solutions in the long-term.
