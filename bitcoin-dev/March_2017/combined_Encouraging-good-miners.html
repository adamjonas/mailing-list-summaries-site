Summary:

The bitcoin-dev mailing list is currently discussing how to promote efficient transaction patterns on the blockchain. One proposed solution is to incentivize miners to mine blocks that reduce the UTXO set size or promote efficient use of the blockchain. While xthin/compact blocks have helped decouple the relation between block size and propagation speed, there are still concerns about centralization pressure due to withholding transactions and the economic advantage of latency. To prevent miners from attacking the chain by mining small or empty blocks, it has been suggested to add a preference for mined blocks with more transactions. However, this could also lead to miners filling up their blocks with junk transactions, leaving larger and more space-efficient transactions in the mempool. Another issue that has been discussed is that miners may not have an incentive to build upon a block with a set of transactions similar to their mempool, as they may be better off building upon a block that leaves valuable transactions in the mempool.To address this problem, a soft-fork could require miners to pay a portion of their fees to future miners. The use of xthin/compact blocks has already solved the problem of orphaned blocks due to fewer transactions. If a miner tries to hurt the network by mining empty blocks, the rest of the miners will start rejecting their blocks, causing them to lose the reward incentive and join the behavior of the rest of the miners.Despite these proposed solutions, there are still many ways in which a chain can be attacked. Therefore, it is crucial to consider various metrics to incentivize miners towards promoting efficiency in transaction patterns on the blockchain. Overall, the discussion highlights the importance of promoting efficiency in transaction patterns on the blockchain and considering various metrics to incentivize miners towards this goal.
