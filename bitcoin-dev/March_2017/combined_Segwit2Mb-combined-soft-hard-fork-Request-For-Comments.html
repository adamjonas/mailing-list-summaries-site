Summary:

Bitcoin developer Sergio Demian Lerner has proposed a solution to the current Bitcoin deadlock called Segwit2Mb. The proposal aims to combine segwit with a 2MB block size hard-fork that will only be activated if segwit is activated by 95% of miners signaling at a fixed future date. The primary goal is to reunite the Bitcoin community and avoid a cryptocurrency split. The hard-fork is conditional on 95% of hashing power approving the SegWit2Mb soft-fork after SegWit has been activated. The tentative lock-in and hard-fork dates are April 29th, 2017, August 29th, 2017, and December 14th, 2017, respectively. However, there are concerns about the aggressive hard fork parameters and the risk of significant delays in software projects and user upgrades.Contributions to the Segwit2mb project are welcomed, but any patch should be simple to audit. Improvements unrelated to a 2 Mb increase or segwit should not be part of Segwit2Mb. The proposal is only a starting point, and community feedback is expected and welcomed.After the implementation of segwit, it has been suggested that 8 MB weight is a more clear way to talk about block size than 2 MBs. The discussion around the proposal is centered on the importance of miner signaling and node upgrades in preventing Bitcoin forks. Sergio Demian Lerner argues that the nodes should not be disregarded in favor of miners, as they are integral to the functioning of the network.SegWit2Mb locks-in before the hard fork occurs, and all Bitcoin nodes should be updated to a SegWit2Mb enabled node to prevent them from being forked away in a chain with almost no hashing power. The simple restriction that transactions cannot be larger than 1MB has been kept to prevent worsening block verification time because of the O(N^2) hashing problem. Lightweight wallets should not be affected as they generally do not check the block size.Sergio Demian Lerner believes in the strength of a unified Bitcoin community and welcomes contributions to the SegWit2Mb project, with the only limitation being to stick to the principle that the patch should be as simple to audit as possible. Improvements unrelated to a 2MB increase or SegWit should not be part of SegWit2Mb. The proposal is only a starting point, and community feedback is expected and welcomed.
