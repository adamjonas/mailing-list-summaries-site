Summary:

The Bitcoin-dev mailing list has been discussing the potential effects of rising fees on the Bitcoin network and its users. The topic of blockchain sharding was brought up, with doubts expressed about its feasibility due to potential conflicts between shards and transaction commitments. Jared Lee Richardson expressed interest in the concept of blockchain sharding to increase efficiency but doubts its trustless implementation. The conversation then turned to the issue of transaction fees, with some arguing that increasing fees would force home users to stop using the network. However, it is believed that most home purchases will still fit within the blocksize limit without a blocksize increase. Predictions vary on what level of transaction fees will become unappealing for consumers, with some suggesting above $1 per tx, while others predict above $10 as being niche-level. Jared Lee Richardson suggests that if node operational costs are going to be highly weighted, there needs to be a solid justification with mathematical models or examples.Tom Harding responded to concerns over rising fees affecting small miners, stating that they use pools for smaller and more frequent payments. However, rising fees could potentially cause them to give up mining altogether, leading to centralization of mining power. The centralizing effect of rising fees on small miners using pools was discussed, with concerns raised about the potential loss of nodes leading to centralization of mining power. Finally, the discussion emphasizes not sacrificing Bitcoin's core innovation of monetary sovereignty in pursuit of supporting only a small fraction of daily transactions, as an altcoin with both lower fees and monetary sovereignty would take over Bitcoin's crown. More consensus is needed for any blocksize increase to be agreed upon, as the proportion of users believing no blocksize increases are needed is larger than the hardfork target core wants (95% consensus).
