Summary:

Luke Dashjr, a Bitcoin Core developer, has announced his plan to update the status of BIPs 9, 68, 112, and 113 to Final in one month. The discussion was held on the Github BIPs repository about whether BIP 9 required implementation and fell under Draft/Accepted/Final classification or Draft/Active classification like process BIPs. Dashjr believes that since BIPs 68, 112, and 113 have already been deployed successfully, BIP 9 now falls under the Draft/Accepted/Final class as an informational BIP. Therefore, BIPs 68, 112, and 113 also ought to be Final status. Dashjr needs at least one author from each BIP to sign-off on promoting them to (and beyond) Accepted, as all four BIPs are currently in Draft status. The conversation is about BIP 9 being labeled as only "Informational" in the first place, as it is part of the consensus logic. The links to the background of the discussion can be found on the Github BIPs repository. Daniel Cousens proposed that BIP 9 should move to the Accepted stage, but there is confusion about its classification. It's unclear whether it falls under Draft/Accepted/Final or Draft/Active proposals. Reviewing the criteria for status changes, it was determined that BIPs 68, 112, 113, and 141 are implementations of BIP 9, making it part of the Draft/Accepted/Final class.
