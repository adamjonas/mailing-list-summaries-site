Summary:

Bitcoin-Qt version 0.8.3 has been released to fix a denial-of-service attack that can lead to node crashes. The new release contains measures to avoid memory exhaustion attacks by truncating over-sized messages and fixing a regression that caused excessive rewriting of the 'peers.dat' file. To report any bugs, users are encouraged to use GitHub's issue tracker, where Peter Todd was credited with responsibly disclosing the vulnerability (CVE-2013-4627) and developing the repair. On June 25th, 2013, Gavin Andresen announced the availability of Bitcoin-Qt version 0.8.3 on SourceForge, which can be downloaded from the provided link. The "splash" banner displays "0.8.3-BETA." An email conversation on June 26th, 2013 at 01:13:25 AM between Jesus Cea and Pieter revealed that the "splash" banner of a certain release showed "0.8.3-BETA," but Pieter said that this is nothing unusual and follows the trend of every other release until they reach version 1.0.
