Summary:

In a discussion on June 11, 2013, Luke-Jr and Melvin Carvalho raised concerns about the nature of bitcoin addresses and their compatibility with other URI schemes. Luke-Jr stated that whether an address is opaque or not depends on context, while Melvin argued that URIs are the global variable of the internet and need to be compatible with other schemes.The wiki page on bitcoin addresses was also discussed, with some suggesting that it may not accurately reflect how addresses work in most software within the Bitcoin ecosystem. However, developers working on components that interact with addresses may find the information relevant. It was noted that there may be changes to the base58 encoding used for bitcoin addresses to address known limitations, but Luke-Jr was opposed to making any changes until the wider implications were fully understood.Despite appearing opaque to humans, bitcoin addresses have meaning from a code perspective. They are made up of an "address type + hashed public key, base58 encoded." However, this does not reveal any information about geographical location, bank details, or personal identity.During the discussion, Melvin also asked about investing in vanity addresses, but Luke-Jr did not provide a clear answer. To ensure accurate information about bitcoin addresses, it is essential to clarify what an address is believed to be and update the wiki accordingly.
