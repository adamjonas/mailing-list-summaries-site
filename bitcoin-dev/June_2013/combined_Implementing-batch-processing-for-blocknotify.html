Summary:

In May 2013, Bitcoin application user Rune Kjær Svendsen faced an issue while using the -blocknotify option with bitcoind. His app was unable to finish its work before a new block arrived, resulting in multiple instances of the app running simultaneously and inefficient utilization of resources. Svendsen discussed the problem with wumpus on the bitcoin-dev forum, and together they proposed a solution that could replace -blocknotify or be included in a new function called -batchblocknotify.ZMQ (ZeroMQ) was suggested as an excellent fit for high-speed notifications by Wladimir, who also recommended integrating zmq directly into Bitcoind through a pull request on Github. Chris Double expressed his interest in seeing something official and offered to test out the patch in the pull request. Other members of the thread agreed with Chris's approach of queuing outside of bitcoind.In addition, Dr. Andy Parkins suggested creating a unix-domain socket when the program started, and if it could not create one, then it should connect to it. This method would have two modes: the creator of the socket which listens to the socket for more incoming data and adds it to some sort of internal block queue, and the client to the socket which pushes output of -blocknotify to the socket and exits. Therefore, concurrency problems would be eliminated because only one process would be doing something with the data.The email thread also contained an advertisement for AppDynamics Lite, a free troubleshooting tool for Java/.NET code.
