Summary:

The discussion revolves around implementing a hardfork warning system for both full nodes and light nodes in cryptocurrency. Johnson Lau proposed changing consensus rules related to block nVersion and creating a generalized block header to detect and warn users of potential hardforks. The proposed solution is capable of detecting various types of hardforks but does not guarantee their success or avoidance of two permanent incompatible forks. There are concerns about the impact on legacy nodes and ongoing softfork support signalling processes. A new Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) is suggested for warning users of a hard fork when a block is invalid by holding back at the best-common block between the best-valid chain and the invalid chain, forcing the user to decide whether to reject the invalid chain permanently or upgrade to a version that understands the chain as valid.It also proposes using a prohibited bit to signal a soft-hardfork while distinguishing it from a regular hardfork, but there are concerns about how this may disrupt the old network if it doesn't fully consent to the hardfork. Developers should pay attention to the fact that BIP9 is disabled when any of the hardfork notification bits are set, which may interrupt any ongoing softfork support signalling process. They may redefine the counting of signal or move the signalling bitfield to a different location. Legacy nodes would not benefit from this softfork and warning system, but no additional risks are introduced to them either. There is also debate about whether a prohibited bit should be used to signal a soft-hardfork rather than an invalid one, and concerns about the unintended behavior of the proposed soft fork.Overall, the discussions center around the use of softforks and hardforks in cryptocurrency and how best to implement warning systems for them. It is important to note that a hardfork may have lower difficulty but higher total proof-of-work, such as by decreasing the average block interval. The hardfork warning system is only responsible for informing users that a hardfork might be happening and prompting for further instructions. It does not guarantee that the hardfork will be successful and not end up with two permanent incompatible forks. This requires broad consensus of the whole community and is not solvable with technical means alone.
