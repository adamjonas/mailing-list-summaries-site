Summary:

The email thread centers around the topic of blockchains and their scalability. The participants discuss how sidechains can be used to prototype new features that improve blockchain scaling and propose creating a federated sidechain as a demonstration and eventually implementing it into the Bitcoin blockchain. However, concerns are raised about incentives not lining up to prevent theft in Drivechain, which is discussed along with associated BIPs. Articles and videos related to Bitcoin's security, transactions, fees, critiques of Drivechain, and the differences between RSK and Ethereum are shared during the conversation. Further, there is a debate on whether Stacks is a Bitcoin sidechain or not. While some argue that it is not, as it has its own native token which is not pegged to BTC and was premined, others believe that the marketing of Stacks contains a lot of misinformation and ultimately, none of these things benefit Bitcoin. In terms of scaling, the article compares Liquid and Lightning, focusing on the trust model and on-ramps/off-ramps. It is suggested that creating more blockchains may not be the best solution to scaling issues. Instead, a 2-of-2 federation with atomic swap mechanisms is proposed as an alternative, similar to Lightning Network. The conversation also touches on the security of Bitcoin and expected fees on layer 1 due to Drivechain. The importance of improvements such as Segwit, Layer 2, Batching, UTXO consolidation, Fee estimation, Coin selection, Exchanges, Wallets, etc., which affect fees, is highlighted. Sidechains can be used for smart contracts, which are limited on the Bitcoin Layer1. Increasing the Drivechain security parameter leads to slower sidechain->mainchain withdrawals, effectively creating a bottleneck on how much can be transferred sidechain->mainchain. Against 51% attack confiscation, Paul Sztorc proposes a "nuclear option" where mainchain full nodes are upgraded to ignore historical blocks created by the 51% attacker. In terms of federated sidechains versus Drivechains, Federated sidechains are custodial and use a predetermined signing set for their federation, while Drivechains use mainchain miners as custodians. It is suggested that if sidechains are for prototyping new features, it may be better to create a federation with developer friends. LN does not require any changes at the base layer anymore.
