Summary:

The Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) process is currently under review to determine if it can be improved or at least inform why certain things operate the way they do. Two IRC meetings have been organized for September 14th and September 29th on the Libera IRC channel #bitcoin-dev to discuss possible topics ranging from relatively mundane issues such as spelling changes to more controversial ones such as the role of BIPs in informing the community of soft fork activation parameters.Participants are encouraged to address common misunderstandings and subtleties of the process. The meetings are being organized with the goal of improving a resource that the community all relies on. A BIP process wishlist has already been created and contributors are invited to attend the meetings to make progress. The current BIP process is outlined in BIP 2, which involves several steps. First, a proposal is submitted to the Bitcoin community for discussion and feedback. If it gains traction, a draft is created and reviewed by the community. Once the draft is finalized, it is submitted to the Bitcoin Core developers for implementation. Finally, the change is tested and integrated into Bitcoin's codebase. This process ensures that changes to the network are well-vetted and thoroughly considered before being implemented. The BIP process is an important aspect of Bitcoin's decentralized governance structure, as it allows for community input and consensus-building around proposed changes. In terms of improving the BIP process, suggestions have been made on the bitcoin-dev mailing list. Prayank proposes the creation of a bot in the bitcoin/bips repository to notify about pull requests based on different things, and shares his experience in creating a BIP Gallery to help newbies understand the importance of BIPs in Bitcoin, which was not well-received. Michael suggests the use of Git version control system in the repository, instead of relying solely on the mailing list for editing and revision. Additionally, a BIP directory that links to the proposals shared on the mailing list could be created, which can be updated without changing the original post. There is no need to worry about the perception of authority in the bitcoin/bips repository, as there are limits on the ability of BIP editors to unilaterally merge in contentious code changes. A previous attempt at creating a BIP Gallery was made but was not considered helpful by many. However, an informational BIP could supplement the standard BIP for new implementers or casual readers.
