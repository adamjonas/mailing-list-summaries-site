Summary:

Tamas Blummer discusses the importance of exit guarantees for those who trust their coins to unchained platforms. He suggests that covenants for debt tools may be a solution, but also proposes an alternative method involving smart contracts embedded in public keys.Blummer acknowledges making an error in proposing a new covenant and suggests that the original example should remain unchanged. He also notes that there remains the question of why the rightful owner is not enforcing the covenant instead of having it enforced by on-chain consensus.In response to ZmnSCPxj's suggestion of unchained execution of transfers with cut-through exit transaction, Blummer acknowledges how it made the example much stronger. The concept of generalized covenants can be used to create a federated side chain embedded into the Bitcoin block chain, with a covenant that offers alternate control paths inherited from Taproot.Blummer introduces a generalized covenant for reducing control by the current owner and exploring new use cases. He mentions taproot as a means for offering alternate control paths. He proposes a covenant that would put a coin under the control of Transfer and Exit keys along with the script controlled by the current owner. This would result in a federated side chain embedded in the Bitcoin blockchain.ZmnSCPxj questions the usefulness of covenants if there is an alternative that does not involve fixed Transfer signers, but still requires a covenant. He suggests Smart Contracts Unchained as a potential solution. In response, Tamas provides an alternative covenant that offers unrestricted flexibility to the unchained platform while maintaining the exit option.
