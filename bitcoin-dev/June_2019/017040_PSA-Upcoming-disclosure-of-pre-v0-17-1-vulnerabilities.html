PSA: Upcoming disclosure of pre-v0.17.1 vulnerabilities

    Author: Luke Dashjr 2019-06-22 23:42:52
    Published on: 2019-06-22T23:42:52+00:00

  • Click here to read original discussion on the bitcoin-dev mailing list

Summary:

Two vulnerabilities have been identified in Bitcoin Core software. The first vulnerability, CVE-2017-18350, was introduced in version v0.7.0 and affects all versions released until the fix was included in v0.15.1. No versions prior to v0.15.1 are expected to be fixed. The second vulnerability, CVE-2018-20586, was introduced in v0.12.0 and affects all versions released until the fix was included in v0.17.1. As of now, this fix has not been backported to older versions.It is important to note that the fixes for these vulnerabilities have not been made to the git branches at this time. While a dedicated release is not expected for the second vulnerability due to its minor severity, it may be included in future releases of v0.15.3 and v0.16.4.As these vulnerabilities pose a potential risk to users, it is recommended that they upgrade to a fixed version no later than August 1st.
