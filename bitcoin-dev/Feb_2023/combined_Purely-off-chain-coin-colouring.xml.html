In a recent discussion on the bitcoin-dev mailing list, users are exploring the challenges of creating non-fungible tokens (NFTs) with off-chain content. Keeping jpegs on-chain can increase blockspace, driving up costs for everyone, so an alternative approach is proposed where people pay sats over lightning to host an NFT, eliminating the need for miners to collect fees and allowing the host to incur the obligation to preserve the NFT on behalf of its owners.Another proposal involves using a Nostr relay and proof of work to provide a spam-free feed of minted digital artifacts. The email discusses the challenges of blockspace, user protection, and the lack of scarcity for collectors. One suggestion is to require proof of work or payment over Lightning for hosting an NFT to make creators pay for their digital asset's preservation.The conversation also considers the possibility of adding support for social legitimacy and lightning-based patreon-type setups. The author proposes an off-chain NFT protocol that allows for easy tracking of collections and individual artworks and supports social legitimacy by linking all assets created by the same public key as an artist's portfolio.The email also discusses the appropriateness of the term "inscription" for an off-chain NFT protocol, proposing an off-chain scheme where the "digital asset" includes the ordinal that controls ownership and is identified by the hash of its contents, including that ordinal's identity, making it indelibly associated with a particular satoshi despite being an off-chain NFT protocol.Users also discuss the possibility of using third-party platforms such as IPFS and Arweave for off-chain data storage for NFTs. However, limitations are highlighted, and it is suggested to use well-known third parties such as GitHub or Twitter to assure the availability of snapshots even if they get censored or disappear. Changes to the OP_RETURN max size are proposed to overcome current limitations.The email provides insights into the possibilities and limitations of NFTs, including the challenges of creating NFTs with off-chain content and tracking individual satoshis throughout their lifetime using ordinals. The discussion also covers the differences between semi-fungible tokens and stable coins and the use of single-use seals for tracking assets anchored to Bitcoin.Overall, the email sheds light on the potential issues and possible solutions related to the use of NFTs on the blockchain network, emphasizing the need to optimize bitcoin transactions for the transfer of value. The email also includes links to various projects related to Bitcoin, including a universal coin swap system based on Bitcoin and a bitcoin NFT system.
