Summary:

Codex32 is a proposed seed-sharing scheme that provides an alternative to SLIP-0039 for generating BIP-0032 master seeds. The project allows secret data to be split into up to 31 shares, each consisting of a string similar to a Bech32 string, enabling periodic recollection on paper using pen and some lookup tables without electronics to verify that the individual recalled the string correctly. The ability to verify the integrity of each share independently without using a computer is a significant advantage of this proposal.The Codex32 system does not support nested SSSS, unlike SLIP39. Test vectors are provided to illustrate how to convert a valid codex32 string into an HD master seed, how to split a master seed into multiple codex32 shares, and how to recover the master seed from a set of shares. The project also proposes a new Bech32-based format for encoding BIP-0032 HD master seeds and shares. Using only uppercase alphabets and numbers in codex32 can significantly reduce the size of QR codes, making them more efficient.A big selling point of Codex32 is its ability to enable hand-computation, longer and stronger checksums, and more compact encoding. However, preventing deliberate modification of recovery codes remains a challenge. Potential solutions proposed involve creating a MAC for verification purposes or using extra data that is not needed for recovery but can verify shares. In an email thread, the possibility of taking master secret handling functions out of hardware wallets has been discussed. The Codex32 project is mentioned as a way to verify the integrity of shares and seeds without exposing them to threats associated with active use. The idea of partial verification is suggested, using a "quick check" to verify each Codex32 share independently without using a computer. Additionally, another method for verification is proposed by using a simple mod N = 0 checksum. Overall, Codex32 provides alternative ways to ensure the security of shares and seeds. The discovery of the Codex32 system used by the Cult of the Bound Variable may also be of interest to the Bitcoin community and those interested in cryptography. However, preventing deliberate modification of recovery codes remains a challenge, and potential solutions proposed involve creating a MAC for verification purposes or using extra data that can verify shares.
