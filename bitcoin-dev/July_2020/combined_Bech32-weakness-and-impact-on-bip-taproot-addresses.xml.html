Summary:

The Bitcoin development community is currently discussing potential modifications to the Bech32 SegWit address format in preparation for taproot activation. One of the issues being addressed is unencumbered witness v1 outputs of lengths other than 32, which could result in outputs that can be spent by anyone. Several proposals have been made, including outlawing v1 witness outputs of length 31 and 33 or fixing the issue in the bech32 algorithm at the consensus/standardness layer.Pieter Wuille proposes amending BIP173 to restrict witness programs to lengths of 20 or 32, while developing a variant of bech32 with better insertion/erasure detecting properties for programs of different lengths. Russell O'Connor proposed amending BIP173 to restrict "bc" and "tb" segwit address formats to require witness programs of size 20, 23, 26, 29, 32, 36, or 40 and suggested creating a new human-readable-prefix for length-prefixed bitcoin witness programs. There are ongoing discussions about improving the Bech32 algorithm to better detect insertion/erasure errors, as well as using alternate checksums for Segwit outputs of different lengths. The root of the issue is in Bech32, and it's simplest to fix things there, preferably by changing the constant 1 that is xor'ed into the checksum before encoding it to a 30-bit number or implicitly including the length into the checksummed data.The proposed modifications aim to prevent potential issues related to changes in the Bitcoin protocol. It is crucial for developers to stay up-to-date with the latest developments and address any vulnerabilities in the system. While the specifics of the proposed amendment are not provided in the given context, it can be assumed that it represents an effort to improve and update existing legislation to better serve the needs of society.
