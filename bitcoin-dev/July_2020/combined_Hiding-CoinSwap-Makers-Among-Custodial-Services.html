Summary:

Bitcoin developer, ZmnSCPxj has proposed a technique to improve the privacy of CoinSwap users by having makers use transaction batching to service multiple clients at once with a single transaction. This approach reduces overall transaction costs for makers and spreads taint around, which increases the effort that chain analysis must go through to identify what really happened. In a recent email exchange, Chris and ZmnSCPxj discussed the idea of CoinSwappers slowing down the CoinSwap process to allow makers to use batching, potentially improving user privacy. Additionally, by taking longer, takers can save on miner fees. However, ZmnSCPxj later realized that the same kind of batching could also be done by the taker by contacting two makers in parallel, potentially providing extra privacy and reduced cost for the taker. The conversation highlights the importance of optimizing transaction processes in the world of cryptocurrencies.
