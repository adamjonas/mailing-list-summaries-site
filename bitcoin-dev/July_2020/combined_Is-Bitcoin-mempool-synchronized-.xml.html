Summary:

Hilda recently questioned the synchronization of the Bitcoin system, specifically regarding mempools. Although heuristics can assist with limiting the number of transactions pushed, they can also create desynchronization between mempools leading to potential problems. Fortunately, the blockchain itself is synchronized, making it impossible to spam blocks without completing the work necessary to reach the difficulty target. It is vital to note that consensus rules ought only to refer to data inside blocks, not mempools, which are not synchronized across all nodes and are ephemeral. Hilda sought input on whether the Bitcoin system's synchronization, including mempools, could be verified without checking every miner in real-time.
