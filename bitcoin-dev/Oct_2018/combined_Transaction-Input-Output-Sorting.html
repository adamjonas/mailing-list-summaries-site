Summary:

The effectiveness of blockchain analysis and the widespread use of centralized analytics services raise concerns about the fungibility of bitcoin. This could potentially harm the value of bitcoin as determined by its history. The boundaries between different wallets are easily identifiable due to the way wallets sort transaction inputs and outputs.Some wallets prefer deterministic sorting, while others object to it due to privacy loss during the transition period and future incompatibility issues. To address this issue, a potential solution suggested is deterministic sorting based on a semi-secret. Wallets would use a standardized sort order based on the semi-secret `sortingSecret = hmac(walletSeed, "sortingSecret")`.Ryan Havar wrote a script to test the popularity of bip69 and randomization in transactions. After shuffling inputs and outputs, he found that only 42% of transactions followed bip69 when randomized. He believes that randomization is more popular than bip69. Even though bip69 is used, it appears to be a small minority. Gregory Maxwell commented that bip69 follows around 60% of transactions, but Ryan's findings contradict this claim as only a minority of transactions follow bip69.Ryan also attempted to calculate the false positive rate for bip69 usage using math, but he admits that his math skills are limited. It was noted that a two input randomly ordered transaction has a 50% chance of following bip-69, which could explain why only a small percentage of transactions use bip69.
