Summary:

Kenshiro has requested the creation of a Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) to implement Confidential Transactions (CT) in Bitcoin Core. CT would provide privacy and fungibility for normal users, as it has already been implemented in Grin and Monero, indicating its maturity for implementation in Bitcoin. Kenshiro suggests that if the CT transaction size is three times the size of a regular transaction, the block size could also be increased by three times or keep the current block size and make CT transactions optional. However, SomberNight responded with doubts about achieving consensus on CT implementation due to concerns around computational binding and the potential for undetectable inflation. They cited section 4.6 of the Bulletproofs paper which stated an adversary who can break the binding property of the commitment scheme or soundness of the proof system could generate coins out of thin air and render the currency useless. Additionally, they provided a link to the Bulletproofs paper for reference. A cryptocurrency's confidentiality can be destroyed by quantum computers, but its effects could be mitigated by splitting the blockchain into a public part and a confidential-transactions part. In this way, the amount of total money in the confidential part is the sum of all lockboxes. If there was a cryptographic break in the CT rangeproof protocol, then the first owner of a quantum computer can claim all the lockboxes but the damage is bounded to only those UTXOs in the CT part. The transaction structure from payer to payee remains visible on-chain under MimbleWimble, which helps reduce the ability to see historical data. However, Scriptless Script should be sufficient for Lightning Network.
