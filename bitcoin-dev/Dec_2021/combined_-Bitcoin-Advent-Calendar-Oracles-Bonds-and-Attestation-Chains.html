Summary:

The post shared by ZmnSCPxj discusses how covenants like CTV can resolve issues related to timeout/rollover and collusion on punishment, improving the functionality of on-chain bitcoin signing oracles. The author also proposes a protocol that can create 'branch-free' attestation chains where equivocation results in burned funds. This creates an incentive for stakers to sign the "correct" statement in line with peers. However, if they equivocate to switch and get tokens, they will burn their collateral. ZmnSCPxj suggests using musig keys for staking in a blockchain network, which allows users to sign with a, b, or c until they equivocate once after which they would have to switch to the other keys. If a staker equivocates three times, they are out. While lying cannot be punished, stakers who sign with the majority receive allocated reward tokens for that slot.The author provides a link to Robin Linus's whitepaper which delves deeper into the concepts described in the post. Bonds are better if the bonder cannot recover their funds, and with a covenant, the bonder can definitely lose funds. While an oracle can still lie, it needs to consistently lie and not equivocate. If an oracle signs an invalid sidechain block, it could still do so, but it is prevented from later denying this by signing an alternative valid sidechain block. If it sticks to its guns, the sidechain stops operation, and everyone owning sidecoins loses their funds, while the bond remains safe.The context discusses the issue of punishing stakeholders for signing the wrong thing and making changes to answers without risking funds. The author notes that a single equivocation can lead to unbounded equivocation by third parties, allowing for the complete rewriting of the signature chain. Additionally, if a musig key is used for staking and an individual equivocates three times, they are out. The author is uncertain about the practical application of this. Overall, the post and email exchange provide valuable insights into improving the functionality of on-chain bitcoin signing oracles and incentivizing stakers to sign the correct statement in line with their peers while avoiding burning their collateral.
