Summary:

In his series of posts on his website, Bitcoin developer Jeremy Rubin discusses the significance of smart contracts in securing Bitcoin's future. In his day 6 post, Rubin argues that smart contracts should be a critical precursor to securing Bitcoin's future rather than something that should come after making the base layer more robust.Rubin explains the concept of "smart" contracts and proposes a redesign in Bitcoin terms to record a "default branch" that can be automatically executed when the future block height arrives. The N-of-N rule is also discussed, with Rubin cautioning against always using it as it can pose a major obstacle for decentralized coordination-free mining pools. Rubin clarifies in response to a comment that he was discussing the broader community's definition of smart contracts, which is about enhanced functionality rather than true self-enforcement. He emphasizes that improving the four pillars of Bitcoin (scale, privacy, self-custody, and decentralization) should be the focus of smart contract capabilities to solidify Bitcoin's adjudication of transfers of value.Furthermore, Rubin notes that improvements in hardware could give us better ability to adjudicate transfers of value. He also highlights some misleading or incomplete comparisons and links related to Ethereum and the fee market in Bitcoin. The article talks about the differences between Bitcoin and Ethereum that affect the fee market, including block size, block limit, UTXO vs account model, failed transactions that pay fees, and the need for nodes to run smart contracts. Rubin notes that stablecoins, DEX, NFT platforms, CEX, and VCs are all paying fees, which affects the fee market. Other factors include darknet markets using Monero, stablecoins no longer using Omni, and most transactions being related to exchanges.Finally, Rubin notes that projects like DLCs are not yet used in any high-volume projects. He emphasizes the importance of pursuing improvements in the hardware that Bitcoin runs on to improve its ability to adjudicate transfers of value. The article concludes by highlighting that new users are influenced by people like Elon Musk and are interested in quick riches rather than DeFi or other technologies that could improve the Bitcoin ecosystem.
