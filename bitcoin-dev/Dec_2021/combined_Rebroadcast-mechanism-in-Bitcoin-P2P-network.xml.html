Summary:

In a discussion on the bitcoin-dev mailing list, Aymeric Vitte expressed concerns about using the Tor network for Bitcoin traffic due to its centralization, small size, and susceptibility to surveillance. Pieter Wuille clarified that he wasn't specifically referring to Tor's protocol or organization, but rather the ease of creating hidden services which can lead to nodes being eclipsed if they only connect to hidden services. He emphasized that using Tor does not solve all privacy issues in the P2P protocol, and there are trade-offs to consider. Another thread on the same mailing list discussed rebroadcasting mechanisms in Bitcoin full node implementations. Pieter Wuille explained that the current approach, where wallets are responsible for their own rebroadcasting, can reveal a relation between nodes and transactions, potentially leading to privacy leaks. Amiti Uttarwar proposed a new rebroadcast mechanism based on fee rate and mempool age to address this issue. The blog post by Prayank Jain highlights 10 issues and pull requests from various Bitcoin projects focused on privacy, with a section dedicated to a "Hall of Fame" featuring developers who have contributed to improving privacy in Bitcoin projects.
