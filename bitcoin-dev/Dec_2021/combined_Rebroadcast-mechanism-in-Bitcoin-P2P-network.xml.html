Summary:

The email discusses various aspects of privacy in Bitcoin transactions and proposes solutions to improve it. One approach suggested is using privacy-enhancing network communications such as TOR, but concerns are raised about its centralization and susceptibility to being watched and controlled. The writer suggests that a better solution would be to have mempools/nodes themselves responsible for rebroadcasting, which could avoid potential privacy leaks.The email also refers to Prayank's blog dedicated to Hal Finney, which tracks issues and pull requests from different Bitcoin projects that aim to improve privacy. They identify seven developers who have worked on pull requests to improve privacy in Bitcoin projects. Their latest post focuses on the rebroadcast mechanism used in Bitcoin full node implementations and proposes a new rebroadcast mechanism based on fee rate and mempool age.It is emphasized that using Tor protocol alone does not solve all privacy concerns, and there is a conversation about the use of Tor independently from the Tor network and browsers acting as nodes. There are concerns about the use of JavaScript and WebRTC, but some of these concerns were addressed through a link provided. Despite these concerns, the recipient appreciates the proposer's commitment to privacy and suggests presenting the idea with a demo and networking with other developers face-to-face.Overall, while extra measures can make it harder to track the origin of nonlocal transactions, they do not make them anonymous, and everything eventually fails. It is important to carefully consider potential issues and network with other developers to advance privacy efforts.
