Summary:

The Bitcoin community is currently discussing the activation of Taproot, with a debate around height-based activation versus Median Time Past (MTP) activation. While height-based activation offers a guaranteed number of signaling periods that could benefit the short activation period of Speedy Trial, MTP activation can ensure a minimum of seven retargeting periods by carefully selecting signaling start and end dates to line up in the middle of expected retargeting periods that would otherwise be chosen with height-based activation. The community must determine if they can reach a consensus on either approach, taking into consideration the number of retarget periods needed for successful activation, the accuracy of estimation methods, and the need to communicate with users about the deadline for upgrading.On the Bitcoin development mailing list, there was a discussion about the MTP lock-in for activation of soft forks. It was noted that the variable "time_until_next_retargeting_period" could result in a two-week error in estimating activation time if MIN_LOCKIN_TIME ends up close to a retargeting boundary. Russell O'Connor compared MTP LOCKIN with height-based activation and argued that the latter provides more accurate estimates consistently and smoothly as activation approaches. In an email exchange, Dave expressed his preference for a predictable activation date but acknowledged that making the activation state machine more complicated may not be worth it, given the simplicity of the current height-based activation.The Bitcoin-dev meeting scheduled for Tuesday, April 6th will focus on discussing various topics related to activation, including the update to MTP time, selecting between MTP and height, and timeline discussion. If rough consensus is not reached, scheduling realities will be discussed. Anyone who cannot attend the meeting is free to leave a comment on any agenda item or suggest other things to be discussed.Russell O'Connor argued that the simplicity and ease of reasoning about height-based activation make it a better option than MTP LOCKIN, which only guarantees a minimum two-week notice prior to activation. In addition, there is concern about whether signaling will still be possible by the time enough miners have upgraded and are ready to start signaling, and whether nodes have upgraded to enforce new rules when activation occurs.The discussion on bitcoin-dev mailing list revolves around the importance of giving users enough time to upgrade their systems before taproot activates. Therefore, using Towns's updated MTP reduces this problem by providing more accurate predictions. This approach makes it easier to communicate with users about the deadline for upgrading, especially for organizations. Moreover, it makes planning activation parties convenient.Instructions for upgrading a system were also provided by David A. Harding in an email dated April 6th, 2021. He provided variations of the command "date -d "$MIN_LOCKIN_TIME + 11 days" for estimators who can calculate in ten or nine-minute increments.The author presents a table of results obtained from running tests to determine the feasibility of using MTP as a means of signaling a smooth upgrade. The author concludes that the results demonstrate that MTP can be used to ensure a smooth upgrade. The discussion also raised questions around the possibility of signaling by the time enough miners have upgraded and nodes have enforced new rules. The author provides stats on the number of retarget periods one gets depending on the desired signaling period. The repeated trials part does not seem to have any significant effect on mainnet. A link to the code used in the tests is also provided.
