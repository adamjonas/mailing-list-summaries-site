The proposal assumes a PoB model that accurately reflects the investment in and development of ASICs for maintaining miner incentives. The transition would begin with validating nodes requiring PoB in addition to PoW in a soft fork, which would make mining more expensive and lead to a gradual drop in PoW usage. Eventually, PoB would be required at 100% of the "required work" to mine on a predefined schedule.The author proposes a long transitionary period for switching consensus protocols, including switching to a different hash function for proof of work. This period should be long, perhaps years, to give miners time to switch over. There would need to be some manually determined estimate of equivalence hard coded into the software as there would be no trustless mechanism to relate the amount of work done between the two mechanisms. The idea is to have a smooth switch over from blocks created by the old mechanism to blocks created by the new mechanism, eventually reaching 0% for the old and 100% for the new. However, this change could be fraught with perils if the miners using each mechanism don't cooperate to ensure that equivalence is set fairly.A hard fork may be necessary to prevent issues during the transition period, but a soft fork is also possible. It is important to have a long transitionary period and a manually determined estimate of equivalence to ensure a smooth switch over.
