Summary:

The article delves into the engineering challenges involved in creating a "true" full Replace-By-Fee (RBF) wallet for on-chain transactions. The author notes that achieving this goal requires significant effort and explains how some wallets currently handle unconfirmed inputs by deleting them after spending to simplify database design. However, to create a true full RBF wallet, it is necessary to keep track of "pending spends" and correlate them with actual transactions. The author mentions that a race condition can occur when bumping up fees on a transaction while a miner finds a new block with the old transaction. Additionally, it is important to consider that while it is convenient to spend money while it is unconfirmed, the original spender may keep fee-bumping the transaction over time, changing the output. The author admits they have not been considering these factors as well as they should have.In a separate email, Prayank discusses different estimations used in wallets and explorers, questioning whether they are misleading and suggesting that sharing mempool stats and allowing users to decide on the fee rate would be a better approach. They propose using RBF algorithms for automated bidding and seek input on the proposed approach, including whether it could work offline.Jeremy and ZmnSCPxj share their thoughts on fee estimation and the recent CVE related to RBF. They discuss the implementation of RBF in Bitcoin Core compared to btcd and potential use cases for RBF and Child-Pays-for-Parent (CPFP), including the maker-taker model where the receiver pays for the transaction. Thorough testing is necessary due to the complexity of exception handling and many branches in the code.The article proposes a three-step approach for wallets that would allow users to decide the fee rate while providing different algorithms for automated bidding, including RBF algorithms. Such algorithms can be helpful when Bitcoin wallets are open in the background, especially for mobile wallets where users can receive notifications every time a transaction is replaced with a new fee rate automatically.Finally, the article explores the long-term potential of using separate fee-only wallets to arrange bumps in transaction fees without disturbing on-chain dependents. The message emphasizes the importance of careful consideration and engineering effort in creating a true full-RBF wallet.
