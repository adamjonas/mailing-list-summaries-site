Summary:

A recent email exchange on the Bitcoin-dev mailing list revealed that Christopher Gilliard has created three additional Bitcoin Improvement Proposals (BIPs) related to his recent proposals. These BIPs, which can be found on Github, are associated with notification, moderation, and web of trust (WoT). While the documents seem well put together, they do not directly change how Bitcoin operates at its base layer but rather build on top of the platform. Feedback, questions, and comments regarding the BIPs have been encouraged.In a conversation between Luke Dashjr and Billy Tetrud via bitcoin-dev, the topic of Bitcoin Improvement Proposals (BIPs) was discussed. Tetrud expressed his opinion that the BIPs seemed unrelated to changing how Bitcoin operates at its base layer, despite being well put together. However, it was clarified that BIPs are not limited to the base layer and can be used for anything that coordinates between Bitcoin software at any layer. This suggests that BIPs can be used to build protocols on top of the Bitcoin platform.
