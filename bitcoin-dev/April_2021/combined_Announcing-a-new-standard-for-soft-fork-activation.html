Summary:

On April 1, 2021, Michael Folkson proposed a new standard for soft fork activation in an email to the bitcoin-dev mailing list. The proposed standard includes all recent proposals and is a multi-phase, multi-year plan consisting of 14 phases that must be passed through successfully before any soft fork can activate. The phases include Let's See What Happens, Start now, improve later, BIP 9 equivalent, Gently discourage apathy, BIP 91, BIP 148, Speedy Trial on mainnet and signet, Modern Soft Fork Activation, UASF BIP 8, and Second flag day.The Modern Soft Fork Activation phase is the longest and most complex phase of the standard. If a soft fork fails at any point during activation, it must start again from Phase 1. Assuming a soft fork activation has successfully passed through all 14 phases, it should activate. However, it will take a minimum of 13 years. The proposed activation design must be conservative, according to Folkson, to demonstrate Bitcoin's ultimate conservatism in its activation process. He expects that this rigorous standard for soft fork activation will get a BIP number in time.Although all future soft forks will ultimately use this standard, it is copyrighted. Royalties must be paid into a quantum secure Bitcoin vanity address every time it is used. The aim of incorporating all ideas into one standard is to provide a rigorous standard for soft fork activation that demonstrates Bitcoin's ultimate conservatism in its activation process.
