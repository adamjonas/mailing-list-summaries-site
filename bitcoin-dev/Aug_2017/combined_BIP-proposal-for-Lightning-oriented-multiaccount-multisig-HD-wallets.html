Summary:

Simone Bronzini has proposed a Bitcoin Improvement Proposal (BIP) aimed at improving multi-account management in different multisig accounts under the same master key. The proposed structure should facilitate the management of multiple cryptocurrencies or forks of Bitcoin, where users can use the same master key. The proposal aims to address the issue of avoiding key reuse while allowing cosigners to generate new addresses independently. Multiaccount multisig wallets were little used before, but now that LN (Lightning Network) is becoming a reality, it is essential to have a better multiaccount management method to handle multiple payment channels. Two issues have been raised: coin_type level and SegWit addresses.Regarding the coin_type level, some objections have been raised that since this is a Bitcoin Improvement Proposal, it shouldn't care about alt-coins. However, having a coin_type level improves interoperability with multi-currency wallets without any major drawback. Moreover, even a Bitcoin maximalist may hold multiple coins for whatever reason (short term speculation, testing, etc).As for SegWit addresses, mixing SegWit and non-SegWit addresses on the same BIP44 structure could lead to UTXOs not being completely recognized by old wallets. Therefore, two possible solutions have been proposed: create separate purposes for SegWit and non-SegWit addresses or create a new level on this proposed structure to divide SegWit and non-SegWit addresses. The latter solution would give the option of having a multisig wallet with non SegWit-aware cosigners without having to use two different subtrees.The proposal is still a work in progress and will be submitted as a BIP draft after receiving feedback. The developer Simone Bronzini has requested feedback on two issues, which include the coin_type level and SegWit addresses. Feedback is highly appreciated to move on with proposing it as a BIP draft. The BIP draft can be found at https://github.com/chainside/BIP-proposal/blob/master/BIP.mediawiki.
