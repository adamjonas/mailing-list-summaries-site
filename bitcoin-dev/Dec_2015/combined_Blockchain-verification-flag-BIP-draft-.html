Summary:

The Bitcoin community is discussing a proposal for a Blockchain verification flag that miners can use to signal that they have completely validated the content of their block and all blocks preceding it. The idea has been proposed as a solution to the issue of some miners not verifying blocks due to economic reasons, which can harm the system.To solve the issue of cgminer's inability to allow a pool to revert to mining on the previous block, a potential use for a flag suggested in a draft was discussed. This flag would allow for non-monotone mining for non-verified blocks. The deployment of this proposal does not require any special strategy since there is no consensus-enforced behavior.The proposal presented in BIP draft-maxwell-flagverify provides a flag that can be used by block authors to signal that they have completely validated the content of their block and the blocks before it. This mechanism turns otherwise harmful validation skipping by miners into a behavior that benefits the public.Non-verifying clients may use this flag to inform their risk modeling, and once widely accepted by the network, non-full-node wallets are recommended to refrain from counting confirmations on blocks where the bit is not set. However, the accuracy of this field must not be strongly relied upon since any miner who correctly uses this flag could stop doing so at any time, intentionally or not.In addition, a recent BIP suggests including an indicator in a block header to signal whether or not the miner has validated the chain. Gavin Andresen agrees with the proposal to solve the accidental selfish mining problem and recommends using bit 0 instead of bit 30 to avoid confusion. Other members suggest SPV clients should only be informed about fully validated headers, while Rusty Russell points out the need for a delicate balance between penalizing blocks too much and disincentivizing the use of the proposed solution.Meanwhile, the discussion touches upon how SPV clients should be informed about fully validated headers without penalizing the blocks too much. It is important to relay all information to peers as soon as possible to prevent disruption in gossip networks. Although full nodes may receive headers first, it may still be beneficial for SPV wallets to hear about all contending chains to make informed decisions.The email thread also discusses the cgminer tool and its inability to allow a pool to revert to mining on the previous block due to the way chain tracking is implemented.
