Summary:

In December 2015, a discussion took place on the bitcoin-dev mailing list regarding the use of short-circuit evaluation and the possibility of complaints arising from it. One member suggested that humans have the ability to respond to novel situations without predetermined rules and algorithms, while another raised concerns about the need for general consensus rules as a social contract. Jonathan Toomim responded that if they announce the intention to perform a soft fork ahead of time and nobody complains about it destroying their secret stash, they could proceed. However, jl2012 raised the question of whether someone may have a timelocked big transaction with a lost private key. Toomim stated that it is an unlikely scenario and that consensus rules should be established as a social contract to prevent breaking them without exceptional circumstances.The issue of quadratic CHECK(MULTI)SIG execution time was also discussed. Peter Todd proposed a fix by limiting only SignatureHash() to return true if the transaction size is less than or equal to 100KB. This allows for a future soft-fork that properly fixes the hashing issue without limiting all transactions or counting sigops. The fix could be deployed within a matter of days and would be effective in case of issues with major miners exploiting slow-to-propagate blocks to harm their competitors. Todd provided a link to related discussions and can be reached at peter@petertodd.org.
