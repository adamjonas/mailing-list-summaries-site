Summary:

Bryan Bishop, a bitcoin developer, has shared links to transcripts from the Scaling Bitcoin workshops held in Hong Kong and Montreal. The talks cover topics such as block size proposals, scalability, zero-knowledge proofs, and lightning network. Although the transcripts are not exact due to Bishop typing while listening, they offer valuable insights into the discussions at the workshops. These talks discuss various topics related to scalability and security concerns in Bitcoin, including zero-knowledge proofs for scalability, segregated witness, block propagation data, validation costs, relay network, sharding the blockchain, and more. During a discussion on the bitcoin-dev mailing list, Bryan mentioned that he had typed most of the words down while listening to some talks. This was acknowledged as hard work by Johnathan Corgan, who runs Corgan Labs, a company that provides SDR training and development services. The email also included a link to Corgan Labs' website. Overall, the context provides valuable resources for those interested in learning about the latest developments and discussions in the Bitcoin community.
