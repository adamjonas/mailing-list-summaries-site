Summary:

A proposal has been made in the Bitcoin-dev community to address the issue of dormant unspent transaction outputs (UTXOs) in the Bitcoin network. The current consensus is that UTXOs with more than 420,000 confirmations are considered dormant and handled appropriately. One proposal suggests creating Lite UTXO nodes that store only UTXOs created in the past 420,000 blocks. With the proposed softfork, if the UTXO is created within the last 420,000 blocks, the key holder may consult any type of full node, including a lite UTXO node, to create the transaction. However, if the UTXO has been confirmed by more than 420,000 blocks, only an archive node can provide all the information required to spend the coin. This proposal aims to avoid forcing thousands of volunteer full nodes to store UTXO records indefinitely.Another proposal put forth by Peter Todd suggests a consensus-critical maximum UTXO age to be part of the protocol, where UTXOs younger than that age are expected to be cached, and those older can be dropped from the cache but would require proof to spend. This proof would count as blockchain space since they need to be broadcast on the network. It is suggested that wallets should start generating these proofs now, and the max-age should be introduced later as a hard fork. However, there are concerns about “grandfathered” UTXOs generated in 2009 and the reversal of the minimization attribute of HD wallets.Critics argue that the membership proofs required for transactions under this scheme could become too large, trading bandwidth for storage. Additionally, the scheme requires users to contact an "archive node" in order to get the UTXO data before making a transaction, making it harder for people to use Bitcoin. There are also concerns about paper wallets and their ability to include UTXO data. While adding UTXO data to paper wallets may not solve the core problem, it could make it easier for users to obtain the necessary information to spend their coins.Jeff Garzik has suggested that instead of deleting dormant UTXOs permanently, they should re-enter the economy as miner fees, with one dormant UTXO mined per block. This would require a hard fork but could fund cheap transactions indefinitely and incentivize miners to hold copies of dormant UTXOs since they could eventually become theirs.There have also been proposals to classify full nodes into three types: archive, full UTXO, and lite UTXO. The proposal suggests defining lite UTXO nodes as the new consensus rule to mitigate the risk.To implement these proposals, information about the TXO state of all outputs in block Y at block (Y + 420000) and whether there was any spending of any block Y UTXOs after block (Y + 420000) is needed. The membership proof needed cannot be constructed without this information. There are open questions concerning "grandfathered" UTXOs and the reversal of the useful minimization attribute of hierarchical deterministic wallets. Additionally, a change (#6550) has been made to Bitcoin core to remove merkle branches from the wallet, which could make it difficult to spend older coins if pruning gets turned on. Jeff Garzik suggests not removing wallet merkle branches as a solution.
