Summary:

The author of the context is in favor of a "can kick" proposal for Bitcoin. The desirable features for this proposal include a block size limit of 2 to 4 MB, a hard fork with consensus mechanisms similar to BIP101, a 1-2 month delay before activation to allow miners to upgrade their infrastructure, and a validation cost metric such as BIP101's or a UTXO growth metric. The author plans to work on block propagation improvements over the next few months and hopes to increase the block size further in the future if successful.
