The question of when "let's not change the economics" ends was raised and answered in the original email. In the short term, a bump in block size reduces the risk of a Fee Event and lack of solid post-Fee-Event economic plan. However, it also creates the danger of long-term moral hazard, as noted in a referenced document. To mitigate this risk, a bump should be accompanied with at least a weak public commitment to flexcap or a similar long-term proposal. Clear communication of future conditions for change is necessary as well. Feedback should be taken into account for iteration.
