Summary:

Recently, there have been several discussions among Bitcoin developers and stakeholders regarding the future of the cryptocurrency. One major concern is the block size limit, which requires a hard fork to increase. Some developers have suggested an early and conservative increase to avoid a radical "Economic Change Event" during the next halving cycle, while others stress the importance of keeping the Service alive, secure, decentralized, and censorship-resistant for as many Users as possible.Pieter Wuille proposed using Segregated Witness (SegWit) as a first step towards addressing some of the issues. However, Jeff Garzik argues that SegWit does not solve all of the problems and there remains major economic risk due to the core block size freeze, which could cause major market and actor disruption. During a miners' round table discussion, one of the developers stated that he did not want to be seen as the decision maker for Bitcoin, while Chinese miners repeatedly mentioned that they wanted several concrete proposals from the devs which they could choose. The ongoing discussion and decision-making regarding how to improve the scalability and efficiency of Bitcoin transactions includes hard forks, soft forks, and the need for both types of forks in order to achieve constant improvement. In a discussion about the implementation of a hard fork to increase the block size of Bitcoin, it was noted that a hard fork would not only affect miners but all full nodes must accept the new rules or be forked off when the hash rate percentage triggers. The use of a hash power vote to decide whether a hardfork is accepted or not was also questioned, with the opinion that Bitcoin Core maintainers should judge if consensus for a hard fork exists and whether it is technically necessary and safe. According to Pieter Wuille, the role of Bitcoin Core maintainers is to judge whether consensus for a hard fork exists and if it is technically necessary and safe. While a hashpower vote can still be used to ensure that miners also agree, Wuille stressed that full nodes must accept and adopt the hard fork in time. Wuille proposed a soft fork approach that includes Segregated Witness as a first step, which solves several issues at the same time without requiring the degree of certainty needed for a hardfork. Discussions regarding the implementation of a hard fork to increase the block size of Bitcoin were held, with suggestions for a grace period of about 4000 to 8000 blocks (1 to 2 months) instead of a 75% consensus switchover point for a hardfork.The discussion on Bitcoin's economics and the adoption of bip102, which proposes a bump in block size to reduce the risk of a Fee Event and lack of solid post-Fee-Event economic plan, has been ongoing among developers and users. However, concerns have also been raised about the danger of long-term moral hazard. While there is no one group in charge of making decisions regarding changes to the cryptocurrency, miners hold veto power over any blocksize increases. Various proposals have been put forward, including a short-term bump in block size or a soft fork approach that includes Segregated Witness as a first step.The Core development team has no authority to decide the network consensus rules, according to recent email exchanges on the bitcoin-dev mailing list. In light of this debate, it has been suggested that there will be no hard fork in 2016, which would give the ecosystem much-needed clarity and time to prepare for future changes. Decentralization means that miners have veto power over any blocksize increases and it is possible to soft-fork in a blocksize reduction. It was also noted that a hard fork would not only affect miners but all full nodes must accept the new rules or be forked off when the hashrate percentage triggers.Pieter Wuille believes that the consensus system of the Bitcoin network should be hard to change and is proposing that miners serve as an extra layer of safety rather than being the sole party to make judgment calls. Furthermore, Pieter Wuille expressed concern that setting the switchover point for a proposed hardfork at 75% would lead to a temporary forked off chain of 25%. He suggested a grace period of 4000 to 8000 blocks (1 to 2 months) in addition to the 75% threshold. Wuille also mentioned that from his discussions with miners, it seems possible to create a hardfork proposal that would receive around 90% support or even higher.Finally, the role of Bitcoin Core maintainers is to judge whether consensus for a hard fork exists and if it is technically necessary and safe. Pieter Wuille clarified that this is the role of Bitcoin Core maintainers solely because they decide what goes into Bitcoin Core, not because they have authority over other full node implementations such as libbitcoin. Ultimately, no one has such authority over alternative implementations, not even the creator of the system if they were still maintaining Bitcoin Core.
