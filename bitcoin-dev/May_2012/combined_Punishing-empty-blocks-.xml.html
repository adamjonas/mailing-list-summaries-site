In 2012, Bitcoin developers and experts engaged in discussions related to mining and transaction fees. One topic of debate was the freedom of miners to reject transactions based on fees or spam filters, which some argued went against the design of Bitcoin. Another issue was the prevalence of empty blocks being mined by some miners, which raised concerns about optimizing the mining process while maintaining network security. Additionally, there were discussions about a potential DDoS problem in the Bitcoin network and the need for economic solutions to make "cheater" blocks less valuable or to increase the value of transactions.The discussions also touched on the implementation of miners modifying the coinbase and the Service Level Agreement for miners. Peter Vessenes and Luke-Jr suggested using a 20 byte keyhash instead of a full URL in the coinbase to broadcast a full URI out-of-band and recommended the use of http or https, redirecting URLs, and content-type. Additionally, there was a discussion about the possibility of having a flag option to punish blocks that do not include transactions.One proposed solution to the prevalence of empty blocks being mined was to restrict relaying or storing them after a certain amount of time has passed since the last block, or ensuring that the latest block includes a certain percentage of unconfirmed transactions from the mempool. However, concerns were raised about potential issues with both proposals. Developers also discussed optimizing the mining process while maintaining the integrity and security of the network. Other topics included improving transaction fee discovery mechanisms, implementing DDoS solutions, and avoiding rechecking transactions that have already been verified as valid.There were also discussions around the implementation of keyhashes in the Bitcoin protocol, and how they enable secure and private transactions between users. Developers discussed the implementation of Service Level Agreements (SLAs) for miners, with suggestions for using http or https, redirecting URLs, and content-type. There were also concerns raised about non-trivial mining power being devoted to mining empty blocks, despite Satoshi's observation that hashing a fixed 80-byte header is sufficient.Overall, the discussions highlighted ongoing debates within the Bitcoin community over how to balance market tension between miners and users while preserving the integrity and security of the network. The conversations illustrate the ongoing efforts of Bitcoin developers to improve the protocol and address issues such as empty blocks and keyhash implementation.
