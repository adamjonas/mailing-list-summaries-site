The author argues against the practice of changing the "Reply-To" field to a different email address than the one originally used by the sender.The article suggests that this practice can cause confusion and inconvenience for recipients who may not be aware of the change. It also notes that some email clients may not handle munged "Reply-To" fields properly, leading to further complications and potential loss of important information.The author acknowledges that there may be situations where munging the "Reply-To" field is necessary, such as when sending email on behalf of someone else. However, they argue that in most cases, it is better to leave the original "Reply-To" field intact.The article concludes by emphasizing the importance of clear and effective communication in email messages, and encourages readers to consider the impact of their email practices on recipients. Overall, the article provides valuable insights and recommendations for improving email communication.
