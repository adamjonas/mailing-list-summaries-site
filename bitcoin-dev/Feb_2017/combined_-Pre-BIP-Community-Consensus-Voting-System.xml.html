Summary:

A proposed Community Consensus Voting System (CCVS) aims to measure support for Bitcoin Improvement Proposals (BIPs) before implementation. Each competing BIP would be assigned a unique bitcoin address, with anyone who wants to vote casting their ballot by sending 0.0001 BTC to their preferred BIP's address. Mining pools, companies using Bitcoin and Core maintainers/contributors are allowed one confirmed vote each, worth 10,000 times a regular vote. The proposal introduces the Confirmed Vote Multiplier to enable these entities to vote with more weight than other voters.However, there are many unanswered questions surrounding the CCVS proposal. These include deciding eligibility of businesses and websites, how to verify voters, and what stops someone from creating fake companies to sway the voting. Additionally, there is no clear process yet for verifying voters, so this proposal is far from complete.In an email conversation, some expressed disagreement with buying "votes" and portraying open standards as a voting process. Others pointed out that the system depends on address reuse, which is flawed in design. Despite the issues raised, the CCVS aims to allow developers to measure support for potential changes to the Bitcoin protocol.The lack of ability to measure consensus among the Bitcoin community has led to many proposed solutions without clear direction, particularly with controversial changes such as the max block size limit. The proposed CCVS would allow developers to measure support for potential changes to the Bitcoin protocol. However, the author believes in a system of vetos rather than approvals, where some changes don't need support outside the developers.In conclusion, while the proposed CCVS has potential to provide a means of measuring consensus for potential changes to the Bitcoin protocol, there are many unanswered questions surrounding its implementation. The discussion highlights that not all disagreements in tech are purely technical and that political factors can play a significant role in shaping industry standards.
