Summary:

The naming convention of opcode CHECKSEQUENCEVERIFY (OP_CSV) in BIP112 has been a topic of discussion among Bitcoin developers on the bitcoin-dev mailing list. The opcode verifies the time/maturity of transaction inputs relative to their inclusion in a block, and suggestions have been made to rename it to reflect its actual use case. Proposed names include CHECKMATURITYVERIFY, RELATIVELOCKTIMEVERIFY, RCHECKLOCKTIMEVERIFY, and RCLTV.Eric Lombrozo suggests renaming the opcode to RCHECKLOCKTIMEVERIFY to better communicate its semantics. However, others argue that the semantics are not limited to relative lock-time/maturity only. The limited availability of single-byte opcodes left is also highlighted, with only ten single-byte OP_NOPx opcodes defined, and 66 single-byte values currently reserved. In another email thread, Peter Todd suggests changing the name of a proposed change to Bitcoin's protocol from "csv" to "op_maturity" to avoid confusion later on. The originator of the name CHECKSEQUENCEVERIFY defends the purposeful choice of name and argues against the alternatives proposed, which capture only the initial use case of nSequence.Mark Friedenbach expressed his objection to the use of nSequence in Bitcoin's implementation and suggested renaming it to nMaturity. However, he acknowledged that it was now too late to make such changes without disrupting the implementation.Overall, the discussion highlights the importance of careful naming and documentation in software development projects, particularly within the Bitcoin community where opcode naming conventions are crucial for app developers to develop apps. Ultimately, the decision on renaming will be up to the Bitcoin community to decide which name better reflects the intent and use case of the opcode. Suggestions have been made, but no official decision has been made yet.
