Summary:

The concept of compression ratio is discussed in this context, with the clarification that it refers to the ratio of compressed to uncompressed data. A message posted on bitcoin-dev by Peter Tschipper presents data on compression ratios achieved by various methods. Zlib is found to be the most effective at compressing data, followed closely by LZOx-999 at a cost. Compression libraries were tested with and without latency, and all performed better than without compression when latency was present on the network. The discussion also centers around local trade-off decisions being negotiated between peers instead of being a required feature of the network P2P. Participants suggest using compressed message holders for historical data, such as "cblocks" for multiple blocks and "ctxs" for combined and compressed transactions. The thread ends with information about the bitcoin-dev mailing list and how to subscribe to it.
