Summary:

BIP65 is a Bitcoin Improvement Proposal aimed at freezing funds in UTXOs directly on the blockchain. However, there is confusion over its interpretation and potential use, as well as concerns over language suggesting funds can be frozen involuntarily. Jeff Garzik clarifies that users choose to freeze their own funds and it is not an act of force. The proposal suggests using cold storage, hardware wallets, and P2SH multisig outputs to control funds. BIP65 cannot use nLockTime for freezing funds as it cannot prove that a transaction output cannot be spent until some time in the future. Gregory Maxwell requested clarification regarding nlocktime without BIP65 to provide context for the need of BIP65 and recommended changing the wording of the proposal to make this connection clearer. The ongoing development of Bitcoin highlights the importance of clear communication about proposals like BIP65.
