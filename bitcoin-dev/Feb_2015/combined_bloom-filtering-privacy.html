Summary:

The conversation between Adam Back and Mike Hearn revolves around various proposals and solutions to improve the privacy and scalability of Bitcoin transactions. One proposal involves using committed bloom filters to prove absence of withholding in the blockchain system. However, there are concerns about miners needing to upgrade and downloading full blocks being too much data. Another proposal suggests adding opportunistic encryption to the wire protocol instead of relying on Tor for privacy.The use of UTXO commitments and scriptpubkey bitmaps per block is also discussed as a way to improve scalability and privacy. The efficiency of querying a single node and the effectiveness of bloom filtering and Tor against eavesdropper threat models are explored. Overall, the discussions highlight the challenges and trade-offs involved in achieving greater privacy and scalability in Bitcoin transactions.The conversation also touches on the idea of wire encryption as a way to improve privacy, which the speaker sees as a more feasible option than a new PIR protocol. Additionally, the development of Subspace as a potential solution for privacy in Bitcoin transactions is discussed. Another proposal involves the use of per-block bloom filters containing all addresses in the block to allow users to download headers and bloom data for all blocks and then scan it offline and locally by searching for addresses from their wallet in it. However, this method has some drawbacks, including the significant increase in overhead due to the size of the bloom filter per block.There are still concerns about miners having to upgrade to generate the per-block filters and the fact that downloading full blocks is still a lot of data. Additionally, there are discussions on how bloom filtering can be used for performance and how it interacts with the need to download chains without round-tripping to the remote node for each block. The conversation centers around the issue of enhancing privacy in Bitcoin transactions. The use of Tor and Bloom filters to index non-simple transactions containing checksig is proposed, but the proposal is met with technical issues such as latency and efficiency concerns. The speaker suggests that using broadcasting or querying a single node when an address in the wallet matches the block filter relies entirely on Tor for privacy. Lastly, there is a mention of receiving BIP70 payments directly and obtaining merkle path proofs to verify correct hits and false positives. The email concludes with a sponsored message about the BIRT iHub F-Type from Actuate and a Bitcoin-development mailing list.
