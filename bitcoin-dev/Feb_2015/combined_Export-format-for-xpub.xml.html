Summary:

In February and March of 2015, several discussions took place among individuals involved in Bitcoin Improvement Proposals (BIPs). The primary focus was on the need for standardization and clear guidelines for various aspects of Bitcoin transactions and wallets. Levin Keller, CEO of Coyno, proposed making the m/0' key format standard and suggested a bitcoin-pub-export protocol with various parameters. Mike Hearn suggested including the proposal in BIP43, which deals with hierarchies, instead of allocating a new BIP. Andreas Schildbach and Pavol Rusnak discussed the parameterizing of wallets with regards to BIPs and agreed that it would be better to use the BIP number directly instead of trying to predict future BIPs. They also discussed the appropriate gap limit for wallets and recommended using the same gap limit as specified in the BIP44 standard.There were also discussions about using human-readable timestamps and QR codes in Bitcoin transactions. Some argued that they are unnecessary while others suggested that they are useful for transparency and ease-of-use. Pavol Rusnak suggested using the block number as an option for creation date in a bitcoin-pub-export protocol, but it was argued that this may not be the best choice as keys can be used on multiple blockchains according to BIP32.In addition, there were discussions about the appropriate gap limit for h=bip32, and the response was that the wallet should follow the spec. Finally, discussions revolved around improving the standardization and usability of Bitcoin wallets and their associated formats and hierarchies. QR codes were also discussed, with some suggesting more descriptive parameters and Levin Keller noting that data size is a primary concern.
