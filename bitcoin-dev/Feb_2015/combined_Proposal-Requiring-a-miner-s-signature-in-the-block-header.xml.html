Summary:

The issue of centralization in cryptocurrency mining has been raised, with concerns over pool operators holding users hostage and changing rules to suit themselves. To combat this, a proposal has been made to require miners to sign the block header before hashing and only pay out coinbase to the public key counterpart of the private key used to sign the block header. This would prevent pooled hashpower from earning bitcoins illegitimately and carrying out double-spending attacks without sharing their private key with the whole pool. However, others argue that this solution would increase technical trust requirements and could break P2Pool. There are possibilities for decentralised pooled mining by chipping away at getblocktemplate support. The blog post titled "Mining Decentralisation - The Low Hanging Fruit" offers further insights on measures that can be taken to achieve this goal.
