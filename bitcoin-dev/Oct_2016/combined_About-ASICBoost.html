Summary:

Peter Todd has pointed out that the issue with ASICBoost in Bitcoin is not specific to any company, but rather a flaw in Bitcoin's design. He believes that limiting those who can build efficient ASICs through patent rights is far from ideal and proposes guidelines to prevent such issues.Matt Corallo has criticized the lack of transparency surrounding licensing and public discussions of patents related to Bitcoin development. There are concerns about the potential impact of undisclosed patents on Bitcoin development, as they may hinder consensus change proposals.Sergio Demian Lerner's proposal for a hard fork has been criticized by Peter Todd due to its potential to increase centralization pressure. The lack of transparent licensing by the Bitcoin Foundation regarding Lerner's patent also raised concerns about centralization pressure. Lerner had proposed an extra nonce space BIP and had already applied for the ASICBOOST patent without disclosing it in the BIP or the Bitcoin Core pull request. This raised concerns about entanglements of consensus code with patents, which could hinder Bitcoin development. However, Lerner has committed to looking into the Defensive Patent License.Matt Corallo criticized anonymous miner "Satoshi Nakamoto" for not showing concern towards centralization pressure. However, it was revealed that Lerner had privately expressed concerns about centralization pressure and reached out to the Bitcoin Foundation in 2013 to license his patent, but they encouraged him to seek the patent as they didn't think it would be significant.Peter Todd expressed concern about the lack of disclosure of Lerner's patents related to his prior consensus modification proposal when Lerner proposed a new sidechain BIP. The implementation of ASICBoost in Bitcoin, proposed as a way to improve mining efficiency, also raised concerns about centralization pressure. The author of the proposal shared their findings with some core developers prior to the BIP proposal, but it is unclear if they kept it secret or shared it with ASIC manufacturers. They also expressed their desire to give the patent to public domain. The rationale behind the BIP proposal was to eliminate incentives to mess with the merkle root and prevent the mining of empty blocks. However, there are concerns about unnecessary entanglements between consensus code and patents, which could hinder Bitcoin development and increase centralization pressure in the future.
