Summary:

The discussion on the Bitcoin development mailing list has been moved to Bitcoin-discuss, with Andrew questioning the notion that soft forks do not force users to upgrade software. He argues that tightening of rules through past soft forks does not allow miners and users who want to use old rules to do so, which could result in a hard fork. The upcoming Segregated Witness (SegWit) soft fork is also discussed, with concerns raised about the possibility of requiring a hard fork to revert it. Andrew proposes making SegWit optional and removing previously "soft forked" rules for non-SegWit transactions, requiring them only for SegWit transactions. This may relieve tensions for those uncomfortable with SegWit and considering joining a hard fork like the Bitcoin Unlimited project. The idea of creating a new client called Bitcoin Authentic is proposed if his proposal isn't accepted. Douglas Roark suggests that the only way to avoid conflicts when deploying new features is to remove previously "soft forked" rules for non-segwit transactions and require them only for segwit transactions.
