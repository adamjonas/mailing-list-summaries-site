Summary:

The email discusses the tradeoff between trust and capital efficiency in the Lightning Network. It presents a scenario where a single UTXO holds funds for both "B" and millions of casual users ("C_1" through "C_1000000"). If "B" cheats, each casual user would need to drop their channel to the chain or risk losing their funds. This leads to a "thundering herd" problem with a large number of on-chain transactions as everyone tries to recover their funds. The impact of these additional transactions depends on the timeframe over which they occur. To optimize capital usage, the email suggests varying the timeout at different layers of the internal tree or peering with a dedicated node. Covenant-based protocols can enable the creation of Lightning channels for millions of casual users without requiring their signatures, allowing for long-term scaling. The email proposes using variations of the FFO-WF Lightning channel protocol to address the problem of forcing casual users to drain their balances before expiry. Off-chain bitcoin and control UTXOs can be used to rollover funds without requiring the casual user's action. Incorporating mechanisms like CheckTemplateVerify (CTV) or AnyPrevOut (APO) in Bitcoin's consensus rules can facilitate this. Overall, the email suggests using off-chain transactions and covenants to improve scalability in the Lightning Network and make it a widely-used means of payment.
