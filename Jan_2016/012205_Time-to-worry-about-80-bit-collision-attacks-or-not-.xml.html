Time to worry about 80-bit collision attacks or not?

    Summary:

    The context describes a potential vulnerability in contract setups where an attacker could use a collision attack to construct scripts with identical hashes, resulting in the theft of coins. The author argues that collision security is necessary and suggests that 80 bits may not be sufficient for this purpose. However, another individual counters that it is easy for contract wallets to protect against collision attacks by refusing certain scripts or not recognizing them as valid transactions. The original author expresses concern about constructing a 2-of-2 multisig escrow in a contract and suggests that pre-committing pubkeys could help mitigate risks, but also acknowledges that it may not be a practical solution. Finally, the author stresses the importance of using cryptography that is up to par for ensuring security in Bitcoin transactions.
