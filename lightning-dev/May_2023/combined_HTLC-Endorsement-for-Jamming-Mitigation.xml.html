Summary:

A discussion on the Lightning-dev mailing list centered around the usefulness of reputation systems in Lightning, with Christian expressing skepticism due to their susceptibility to sudden behavioral changes and whitewashing attacks. Antoine Riard responded by suggesting that HTLC endorsement schemes are still vulnerable due to local reputations that can be built during periods of low routing fees and then abused during high routing fees. He recommended implementing reputational transitivity between incoming and outgoing traffic channels accessible from its local counterparty granting a high level of reputation. Antoine opened an issue on the repository to converge on a threat model and emphasized the importance of building data gathering infrastructure for Lightning as any jamming mitigation will have to adapt its upfront fees or reputation acquisition cost based on market forces. Carla Kirk-Cohen shared updates on channel jamming and proposed gathering data about the use of HTLC endorsement and local reputation tracking for jamming mitigation. The goal is to gather real-world data for future simulation work and experiment with different algorithms for tracking local reputation. Each node locally tracks the reputation of its direct neighbors and allocates a number of slots reserved for endorsed HTLCs from high-reputation peers based on their risk tolerance. Endorsement and reputation are proposed as the first step in mitigating channel jamming, with unconditional fees for quick jams being the second step.
