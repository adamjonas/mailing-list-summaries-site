Summary:

In a Lightning-dev mailing list, Dr. Praveen Baratam asked why SegWit is required for the Lightning Network (LN). Johan Torås Halseth replied that before broadcasting the funding transaction, two parties involved in a channel must sign a commitment transaction to spend the output from the funding transaction. Without SegWit, the funding transaction can be malleated, rendering the commitment transaction invalid, and funds may get locked up if one of the parties stops cooperating. ZmnSCPxj provided some background information on LN, including its requirements on the blockchain, limitations/known-gaps in the protocol, and the most mature Lightning implementation available, lnd, which has many features. ZmnSCPxj also mentioned a few issues with LN, such as the same hash used for the entire route, one-sided funding of channels, missing Burchert-Decker-Wattenhofer channel factories, and the need to watch the blockchain for theft attempts if any receives are made. He also shared an article he wrote on Generalized Bitcoin-Compatible Off-Chain Protocols and a link to the Lightning BOLT specs, which contain many sequence diagrams.
