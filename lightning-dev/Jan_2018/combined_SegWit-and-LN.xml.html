Summary:

A member of the Lightning-dev mailing list, Praveen Baratam, questioned the necessity of SegWit for the Lightning Network, suggesting that waiting for the funding transaction to be confirmed would allow for the safe creation and updating of unconfirmed commitment transactions. However, Johan Torås Halseth emphasized the importance of SegWit to prevent transaction malleability. The email conversation also discussed the requirements for implementing full SegWit in funding transactions and recommended lnd as the most mature Lightning implementation. It also acknowledged the limitations and known-gaps in the Lightning network protocol and the importance of all BIPs, including SegWit, for the successful implementation of LN.SegWit is important for LN because it helps to increase the block size limit without a hard fork, reduces transaction fees, and prevents transaction malleability attacks. It is required for the funding transaction to be confirmed, after which unconfirmed commitment transactions can safely be created and updated. Therefore, SegWit is an essential component for the proper functioning of LN. The activation of all BIPs, including SegWit, is crucial for the successful implementation of LN.
