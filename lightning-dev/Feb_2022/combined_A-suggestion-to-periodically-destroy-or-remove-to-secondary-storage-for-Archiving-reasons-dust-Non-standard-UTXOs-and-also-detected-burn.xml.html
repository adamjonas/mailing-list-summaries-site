Summary:

The email proposes a solution to the issue of data deletion in computer systems using blockchain technology, specifically Bitcoin. The author suggests setting nodes with parameters `prune=1` or `prune={>550}` for manual or automated pruning, respectively. While acknowledging the usefulness of pruned nodes, the author argues that nothing should be deleted from the blockchain forever as it is necessary for clients to validate the blockchain in its entirety. There are concerning statistics related to Bitcoin addresses with small balances, including those with less than 1000 Satoshis, which make up approximately 7% of UTXOs. BitInfoCharts provides a link for dust analysis that shows over 8 million addresses with balances of less than $1 and over 13.5 million addresses with balances of less than 0.0001 BTC. Shymaa Arafat suggests incentivizing people to collect these small UTXOs into one of at least $5 or $1. However, the number of addresses holding less than 1000 Satoshis has recently increased by around 2,000 each day, while the number of addresses holding less than $4.1 increased by 4,000 to 8,000 per day.It is essential to note that the number of addresses does not necessarily reflect the number of UTXOs, and there are approximately 0.6 million non-standard and burned UTXOs, which can be misleading on the status of the value they hold. Pieter Wuille argues against deleting or archiving these UTXOs, suggesting instead using the amount as a heuristic for what to keep and how long, and investigating the use of two levels of storage (disk and in-RAM cache). He believes that deleting or archiving these UTXOs would be considered theft and potentially destroy useful funds people own. Furthermore, the email includes contact information for Willtech, go-overt.com, and DUIGCO API, along with a disclaimer that the email does not constitute general advice. While it may be feasible to implement archiving as a standardness rule, it deviates from economically rational behavior and would be controversial as a consensus rule.
