Summary:

The email exchange in question took place on a mailing list for Lightning developers hosted by the Linux Foundation. In it, Thomas Voegtlin proposed an extension to BOLT-11 that would allow for two bundled payments in a single invoice. This feature would address issues that arise from submarine swaps and JIT channels, where service providers are required to ask prepayment of mining fees because they cannot guarantee that the user will claim the payment.The proposed extension would level the field of competition between lightning service providers. The bundled payments feature allows for a prepayment and main payment to be made in one invoice, with the receiver waiting until HTLCs of both payments have arrived before fulfilling the HTLCs of the pre-payment. However, the proposal requires senders to understand the new fields.Bastien Teinturier suggested that extending Bolt 12 may make more sense as it requires new sender code anyway, while Thomas believes that it could be implemented in BOLT-11 without adding new messages.
