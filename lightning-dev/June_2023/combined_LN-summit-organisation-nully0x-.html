Summary:

Antoine Riard, a member of the lightning dev community, has proposed organizing the LN Summit in Africa in June 2024. This proposal stems from the belief that holding the summit in Africa would have numerous benefits. These include showcasing the continent's rich cultural and historical background, addressing fragmentation in financial systems through a compatible payment protocol, and fostering growth in local Lightning economies. Typically, the organization of CoreDev or LN Summit events is not announced on open communication channels to maintain serenity in engineering conversations and avoid trolling or tabloid-style journalism. However, given the potential complexity of operational challenges such as visa travels and tropical weather, Antoine suggests announcing the event well in advance. The operational and financial resources are expected to be sufficient, but a multi-stakeholder approach is encouraged to ensure fairness and transparency throughout the process.To begin planning, Antoine proposes surveying countries with reasonable operational stability that could potentially host the event, such as Algeria, Ghana, Senegal, Nigeria, and others. He plans to share the feedback privately with Lightning attendees based on neutral and technical proof of works heuristics at the end of the summer. Antoine has set up a dedicated communication endpoint at lnsummit2024@ariard.me for anyone interested in being part of the organization. This presents an opportunity for knowledge transfer between generations of contributors. Connecting with various communities across Africa, such as Qala Africa and BitDevs, who already organize local meetups focused on technical sessions about Bitcoin and the lightning network, is considered a good step in the planning process.Overall, Antoine seeks feedback and objections to his contribution to the organization of the LN Summit 2024 and emphasizes his commitment to transparency and accountability. He previously co-organized the CoreDev event in Zurich in 2021, which provides him with operational templates for this endeavor.
