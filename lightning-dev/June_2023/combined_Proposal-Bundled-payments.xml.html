Summary:

Thomas Voegtlin has proposed an extension to BOLT-11 that allows invoices to contain two bundled payments with distinct preimages and amounts. The proposal is aimed at services requiring the prepayment of a mining fee for non-custodial exchanges such as Submarine swaps and JIT channels. The proposal aims to level the playing field in terms of competition between Lightning service providers.The semantics of bundled payments include the invoice containing two preimages and amounts, and the receiver waiting until all HTLCs arrive before fulfilling the HTLCs of the pre-payment. This will ensure that competitors who do not have an established user base running a dedicated client are exposed to a mining fee attack. Thomas believes that ACINQ would benefit from this because it would make it possible for them to make their pay-to-open service fully non-custodial.However, Matt Corallo responded to Thomas's proposal stating that adding another incompatible version of BOLT-11 doesn't seem likely. He suggested working on a standard way of doing swaps built on OMs to ensure privacy. He also explained how submarine swaps add receiving capacity to a channel.Bastien Teinturier highlights that the proposal requires senders to be aware that the payment will lead to a channel creation or a splice on the receiver end, and it requires all existing software used by senders to be updated. He thinks extending Bolt 12 makes more sense than updating Bolt 11.There are currently three strategies deployed on the network to provide JIT liquidity, and Thomas proposes a third option that involves pre-paying fees then forwarding the whole HTLC amount on that channel. The decision to make this feature required or optional remains with the service provider.Overall, while Thomas Voegtlin's proposal may help level the playing field for Lightning service providers, Matt Corallo suggests focusing on a more standardized way of doing swaps built on OMs to ensure compatibility and privacy.
