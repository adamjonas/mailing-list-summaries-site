In an email thread on the Lightning-dev mailing list, there was a discussion about reputation systems in the Lightning Network. However, it was suggested that choosing a counterparty with a proven track record may be better than choosing a newcomer. The placement of reputation systems in the software stack was also discussed, with the suggestion that they should be a separate API or plugin rather than part of the protocol or P2P gossiping. Privacy concerns were mentioned as a reason why some users might choose not to participate in reputation services.Regarding reputation schemes for mitigating channel jamming, the proposal was for nodes to track the reputation of their direct neighbors and allocate slots and liquidity based on their risk tolerance. High reputation peers would be given priority in forwarding HTLCs, while unknown HTLCs would only be forwarded if there are available resources. It was emphasized that reputation and endorsement form the first step in a two-part scheme for mitigating channel jamming.Data gathering was considered valuable for future simulation work and experimenting with different algorithms for tracking local reputation.
