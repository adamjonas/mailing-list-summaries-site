Summary:

The Lightning Network community is actively working towards addressing the issue of channel jamming attacks. One proposed solution is a reputation scheme that tracks reputation locally for each node's peers based on their history with forwarded HTLCs. Reputation is slow to build and fast to degrade, ensuring that sudden changes in behavior are short-lived. Nodes will allocate slots and liquidity reserved for endorsed HTLCs from high reputation peers. Carla and Clara have proposed a two-part scheme involving HTLC endorsement and reputation to mitigate channel jamming. There are concerns about the vulnerabilities of the HTLC endorsement scheme and the lack of transitivity of reputation acquisition cost between hops in a payment path. The team aims to gather real-world data and experiment with different algorithms for tracking local reputation.The conversation also discusses the compensation mechanism in the HTLC endorsement model. Questions are raised about whether the fees paid fully compensate for the damage inflicted during an attack. The dynamic ratio between fees and reputation is also discussed, with suggestions for proportional compensation for receiving nodes. However, the criteria for determining this ratio are not specified.There is a discussion about the implementation of extensions and experimental data gathering mechanisms. Users express interest in helping with implementation details and suggest using lnmetrics as a platform for data collection.The email thread also highlights the importance of differentiating between fees charged by a node and reputation revenue in the HTLC endorsement model. An example is given where a node has to decide whether to forward an HTLC or suffer damage if the sender turns out to be a jamming attacker. The compensation mechanism in the chain of nodes with high reputation is unclear, and the HTLC endorsement mitigation suffers from classic issues of reputation systems.Overall, the Lightning Network community is actively exploring various schemes and reputation algorithms to address channel jamming. Further discussions and data gathering efforts are planned to evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed solutions.
