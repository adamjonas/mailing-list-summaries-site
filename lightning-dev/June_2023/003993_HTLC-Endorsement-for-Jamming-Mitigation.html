The conversation revolves around the topic of jamming attacks in the context of the Lightning Network protocol. The main concern is whether the fees paid to routing nodes can fully compensate for the damage caused by such attacks. It is mentioned that the only observable damage in the protocol is routing fees, and it should be possible to choose a different threshold based on the preference of a node. The difficulty to gain reputation is said to be proportional to the amount of damage that can be inflicted.There is a discussion about the goals of the proposed htlc endorsement system and whether outlawing the cost of the attack from the system contradicts those goals. The focus is on compensating the victim rather than imposing a significant cost on persistent attackers. The precise problem to be solved by any jamming mitigation is questioned, as well as the absence of formalized problem definitions in existing papers.The significance of liquidity griefing issues affecting Lightning and second-layers is emphasized, and the need for evaluating any jamming solution with conceptual rigor is stressed. Without such analysis, measurable bounds cannot be established, and the operational costs of routing hops may not be adequately covered. The soft criteria, such as user experience and ease of implementation, make it difficult to prove theorems, but simulations are being conducted to evaluate quantifiable criteria.The mention of DDoS attacks being difficult to mitigate highlights the challenges in addressing jamming attacks within the Lightning Network.
