Summary:

The Lightning Network has introduced dual funded transactions, which have raised challenges regarding liquidity griefing attacks from malicious peers. One proposed solution is to not lock UTXOs used in dual funded transactions, as they will automatically be reused in another instance of the protocol if a remote node goes silent. However, this approach falls short when using 0-conf, as accidentally double-spending a 0-conf channel can result in loss of funds. Nodes offering 0-conf services must ensure that the UTXOs they use for 0-conf are isolated from those used for non 0-conf to avoid race scenarios and accidental double-spending. This can be addressed by using a "soft lock" when selecting UTXOs for non 0-conf funding attempts.In the case of Eclair, an open-source Lightning Network implementation, they currently employ "opportunistic" 0-conf by immediately sending 'channel_ready' if certain conditions are met. However, with mixed 0-conf and non 0-conf funding attempts, the presence of soft-locked UTXOs prevents them from upgrading to 0-conf.The discussion on the mailing list also touches on the safety of dual-funded 0-conf transactions. It is suggested that swap-in-potentiam addresses can be used to make such transactions safe. If the initiator stalls, the acceptor can refuse to sign the swap-in-potentiam UTXOs, effectively locking their funds until the timeout. This prevents liquidity griefing from being cost-free. The use-case for dual-funded 0-conf transactions is when users expect on-chain operations to be slow and take multiple confirmations. Once a swap-in-potentiam address has deep confirmation, it can be transferred immediately to a 0-conf Lightning channel.Overall, the mailing list thread seeks ideas for improving the situation and offering better protections against liquidity griefing for nodes offering 0-conf services.
