Summary:

The Lightning Network has introduced dual funded transactions, which have raised concerns about liquidity griefing attacks. One proposal suggests not locking UTXOs used in these transactions to prevent such attacks. However, this approach is not suitable for 0-conf channels, as accidentally double-spending a 0-conf channel can result in the loss of funds. To address this issue, a "soft lock" mechanism was proposed, where UTXOs are locked only for non 0-conf funding attempts. This allows for reusing soft locked UTXOs for non 0-conf transactions.There is also a need to isolate UTXOs used for 0-conf and non 0-conf channels to prevent accidental double-spending. It was suggested that a "soft lock" should be implemented when selecting UTXOs for non 0-conf funding attempts, while 0-conf funding attempts should ignore soft locked UTXOs.In addition to these challenges, there were discussions about offering 0-conf channels. While 0-conf is popular among users, it is a complex implementation issue. Ideas were sought to improve protection against liquidity griefing for nodes offering 0-conf services.Another thread discussed the safety of dual-funded 0-conf transactions. The use of swap-in-potentiam addresses with the acceptor was suggested as a means to make these transactions safe. If the initiator stalls, the acceptor can retaliate by refusing to sign the swap-in-potentiam UTXOs, effectively locking their funds until the timeout. This prevents liquidity griefing from being cost-free. Dual-funded 0-conf transactions are intended for immediate transfers despite slow on-chain operations.Overall, these discussions aim to address the challenges and risks associated with liquidity griefing attacks and the implementation of 0-conf channels in the Lightning Network. Various solutions and ideas have been proposed to improve the safety and efficiency of the network while meeting user needs.
