Summary:

Thomas Voegtlin has proposed an extension to BOLT-11, the Lightning Network protocol, that would allow invoices to contain two bundled payments with distinct preimages and amounts. This proposal is aimed at services such as Submarine swaps and JIT channels that require a prepayment of a mining fee for a non-custodian exchange to take place. The goal of the proposal is to level the playing field in terms of competition between lightning service providers.Currently, using Loop, a dedicated client is required to use certain lightning services. Competitors without an established user base running a dedicated client are exposed to the mining fee attack. By implementing Thomas's proposal, ACINQ, a lightning service provider, could make their pay-to-open service fully non-custodian. Additionally, the proposal aims to address potential issues related to the European MICA regulation.The semantics of bundled payments would involve the BOLT-11 invoice containing two preimages and two amounts: a prepayment and a main payment. The receiver would need to wait until all the HTLCs (Hashed Time-Locked Contracts) of both payments have arrived before fulfilling the HTLCs of the pre-payment. If the main payment does not arrive, the pre-payment would be failed with a MPP (Multi-Path Payment) timeout. Once the HTLCs of both payments have arrived, the receiver fulfills the HTLCs of the pre-payment and broadcasts their on-chain transaction.Thomas suggests implementing this change in BOLT-11 rather than using BOLT-12 or onion messages, as it does not require the exchange of new messages and can be done in a non-interactive way. However, there have been suggestions that this use case may be better suited for BOLT-12 or OM (Onion Messages).Matt Corallo, in response to Thomas's proposal, expresses skepticism about getting any new interoperable change into BOLT-11 and suggests working towards specifying a "standard" way of doing swaps, possibly built on OMs for increased privacy. He also mentions that submarine swaps make it possible to add receiving capacity to a channel.In conclusion, Thomas Voegtlin's proposal for bundled payments in BOLT-11 aims to address issues related to lightning service providers and provide a more level playing field for competition. The proposal involves extending the BOLT-11 invoice to include two bundled payments with distinct preimages and amounts. However, there are differing opinions on whether this change should be implemented in BOLT-11 or using other protocols such as BOLT-12 or onion messages.
