Summary:

Thomas Voegtlin has proposed an extension to BOLT-11, the Lightning Network specification, that would allow invoices to contain two bundled payments with distinct preimages and amounts. This proposal is targeted at services that require a prepayment of a mining fee for non-custodian exchanges, such as submarine swaps and JIT channels.The objective of this proposal is to level the playing field in terms of competition between lightning service providers. Currently, using services like Loop requires a dedicated client, which puts competitors without an established user base at a disadvantage due to potential mining fee attacks. By implementing this change, it would enable ACINQ, for example, to make their pay-to-open service fully non-custodian.The semantics of bundled payments would involve the BOLT-11 invoice containing two preimages and two amounts: prepayment and main payment. The receiver would need to wait until all the Hashed Time-Locked Contracts (HTLCs) of both payments have arrived before fulfilling the HTLCs of the pre-payment. If the main payment does not arrive, the pre-payment would fail with a MPP timeout. Once the HTLCs of both payments have arrived, the receiver can fulfill the HTLCs of the prepayment and proceed to broadcast their on-chain transaction. It is important to note that the main payment can still fail if the sender never reveals the preimage of the main payment.Thomas acknowledges that this proposal does not prevent service providers from potentially stealing the pre-payment, but he argues that this is already a risk with the current system. He believes that implementing this change in BOLT-11 would be preferable to using BOLT-12 or onion messages, as it does not require the exchange of new messages and can be done in a non-interactive way.Matt Corallo responded to Thomas's proposal expressing skepticism about getting any new interoperable change into BOLT-11. He suggests working towards specifying a "standard" way of doing swaps, built on onion messages (OMs), to ensure more privacy. He also clarifies the distinction between splice-out and submarine swaps, with submarine swaps allowing for adding receiving capacity to a channel.In conclusion, Thomas Voegtlin's proposal aims to enhance BOLT-11 by introducing bundled payments in invoices, benefiting lightning service providers, enabling non-custodian exchanges, and leveling the competition field. The proposal suggests implementing this change in BOLT-11 rather than using BOLT-12 or onion messages. However, there are concerns about the feasibility of such changes in BOLT-11 and suggestions to focus on specifying a standard method for swaps using OMs.
