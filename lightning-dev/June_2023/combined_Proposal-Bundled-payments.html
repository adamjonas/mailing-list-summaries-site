Summary:

Thomas Voegtlin has proposed an extension to BOLT-11, the Lightning Network protocol, that would allow for bundled payments in invoices. This change is aimed at lightning service providers that require a prepayment of a mining fee for non-custodian exchanges like submarine swaps and JIT channels. The goal is to level the competition between providers by allowing reverse submarine swap payments from any wallet.Currently, Loop requires a dedicated client, which puts competitors without an established user base at a disadvantage. By implementing this proposal, it would eliminate the exposure to mining fee attacks and create a fairer competition among service providers. However, it would require updating all existing software used by senders.The proposed extension involves adding two fields to the BOLT-11 invoice: prepayment_amount and prepayment_hash. The sender would then send the main payment amount minus the prepayment amount with the payment hash, and also send the prepayment amount with the prepayment hash. The decision to make this feature required or optional would be up to the service provider.Matt Corallo responded to the proposal, expressing doubt about introducing new interoperable changes into BOLT-11. He suggests focusing on specifying a "standard" way of doing swaps using onion messages (OMs) for increased privacy. He also addresses the distinction between splice-out and submarine swaps, noting that submarine swaps allow for adding receiving capacity to a channel.There is discussion about whether to include this feature in BOLT-11 or wait for BOLT-12. Thomas argues that implementing it in BOLT-11 could lead to faster deployment, while Matt believes it may be challenging to introduce new changes to BOLT-11 at this point.Overall, Thomas's proposal aims to enhance the functionality and fairness of lightning service providers by introducing bundled payments into the invoice structure. It addresses vulnerabilities and competition imbalances in the lightning network. The specifics of the proposal, including the semantics of bundled payments, are still under discussion within the community.
