Summary:

Thomas Voegtlin has proposed an extension to BOLT-11, the protocol for Lightning Network invoices. The proposal suggests including two bundled payments with distinct preimages and amounts in a single invoice. This would be useful for services that require a prepayment of a mining fee for non-custodian exchanges like submarine swaps and JIT channels.The motivation behind the proposal is to level the playing field in terms of competition between lightning service providers. Currently, using Loop, a dedicated client is required, which puts competitors without an established user base at a disadvantage due to the mining fee attack. ACINQ, in particular, could benefit from this change as it would allow them to make their pay-to-open service fully non-custodian.The semantics of bundled payments are as follows: 1. The BOLT-11 invoice contains two preimages and two amounts: prepayment and main payment.2. The receiver must wait until all the HTLCs (Hashed Time-Locked Contracts) of both payments have arrived before fulfilling the HTLCs of the pre-payment. If the main payment does not arrive, the pre-payment fails with a MPP (Multi-Path Payments) timeout.3. Once the HTLCs of both payments have arrived, the receiver fulfills the HTLCs of the prepayment and broadcasts their on-chain transaction. However, the main payment can still fail if the sender never reveals the preimage of the main payment.Thomas acknowledges that this proposal does not prevent service providers from stealing the pre-payment, but highlights that this is already a risk in the current system. He believes that implementing this change in BOLT-11, rather than using BOLT-12 or onion messages, is the best approach. He argues against adding new messages when the same functionality can be achieved in a non-interactive way.Matt Corallo responded to the proposal, expressing doubt about getting any kind of new interoperable change into BOLT-11. He suggests working towards specifying a "standard" way of doing swaps, built on onion messages for privacy.In summary, Thomas Voegtlin's proposal aims to improve competition between lightning service providers by allowing two bundled payments in a single BOLT-11 invoice. The proposal has potential benefits for ACINQ and non-custodian exchanges. However, there are differing opinions on whether this change should be implemented in BOLT-11 or through other means such as BOLT-12 or onion messages.
