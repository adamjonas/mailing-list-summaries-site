Summary:

Thomas Voegtlin has proposed an extension to BOLT-11, the Lightning Network protocol, that would allow invoices to contain two bundled payments with distinct preimages and amounts. This proposal is aimed at services such as submarine swaps and JIT channels, which require a prepayment of a mining fee for a non-custodian exchange to take place. Currently, these services face competition challenges as a dedicated client is required to use Loop, leaving competitors without an established user base vulnerable to mining fee attacks.The proposed change would level the playing field between lightning service providers by allowing them to include bundled payments in their invoices. This would eliminate the need for a dedicated client and make it possible for competitors to offer similar services without exposing themselves to mining fee attacks. The proposal also requires senders to update their existing software to support this feature. It is suggested that Bolt 12 would be a more suitable option for implementing this change rather than updating Bolt 11.There are currently three strategies deployed on the network to provide Just-in-Time (JIT) liquidity. However, Thomas proposes a third option that involves pre-paying fees and forwarding the entire HTLC amount on the channel. This option would be more suitable if trust and regulation are the primary concerns. It is noted that until Bolt 12 is deployed, the network will likely continue to use 0-conf transactions, making it more reasonable to include this new feature in Bolt 12 rather than Bolt 11.Matt Corallo, in response to Thomas's proposal, believes that it may be challenging to introduce new interoperable changes into BOLT-11 at this point. Instead, he suggests focusing on specifying a "standard" way of performing swaps, built on Onion Messages (OMs) to ensure greater privacy. He also addresses Thomas's question about the distinction between splice-out and submarine swaps, stating that submarine swaps allow for the addition of receiving capacity to a channel. However, he notes that proper checks and fund sweeping are necessary for a submarine swap client to function correctly.Thomas acknowledges that his proposal does not prevent service providers from stealing the pre-payment. However, he believes that implementing this change in BOLT-11 would level the competition between lightning service providers. Additionally, ACINQ would benefit from this change as it would make their pay-to-open service fully non-custodian. There are concerns about introducing new changes to BOLT-11, but Thomas suggests that including this feature in Bolt 12 would be more reasonable since all implementations are actively working on it.
