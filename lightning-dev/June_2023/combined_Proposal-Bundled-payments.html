Summary:

Thomas Voegtlin has proposed an extension to BOLT-11, the Lightning Network protocol. The extension aims to include bundled payments within invoices for services that require prepayment of mining fees. This proposal is intended to level the competition among lightning service providers and enhance non-custodian exchanges like ACINQ. However, there are discussions about the feasibility of integrating these changes into BOLT-11 and the potential use of Onion Messages (OMs) for swaps.The proposed extension would allow an invoice to contain two bundled payments with distinct preimages and amounts. This feature is targeted at services that require prepayment of a mining fee for non-custodian exchanges, such as submarine swaps and JIT channels. Currently, only dedicated clients can use Loop, leaving competitors without an established user base susceptible to mining fee attacks.The semantics of bundled payments in this proposal involve the BOLT-11 invoice containing two preimages and two amounts: prepayment and main payment. The receiver should wait until all the HTLCs (Hashed Time-Locked Contracts) of both payments have arrived before fulfilling the HTLCs of the pre-payment. If the main payment does not arrive, the pre-payment should be failed with a Multi-Path Payments (MPP) timeout. Once the HTLCs of both payments have arrived, the receiver fulfills the HTLCs of the pre-payment and broadcasts their on-chain transaction.Matt Corallo responded to Thomas's proposal, expressing skepticism about introducing new interoperable changes into BOLT-11. Instead, he suggests focusing on specifying a "standard" way of conducting swaps built on OMs for increased privacy. He also addresses Thomas's inquiry about the distinction between splice-out and submarine swaps, explaining that submarine swaps enable the addition of receiving capacity to a channel.Thomas emphasizes the need for a submarine swap client to validate all swap parameters and unilaterally sweep or timeout an on-chain HTLC. While some websites may distribute a separate submarine swap client that is independent of the user's lightning wallet, dedicated logic is still required for proper verification and sweep actions. This separation between wallet and submarine swap providers allows for increased competition.Thomas concludes by highlighting ongoing efforts for Lightning Service Provider (LSP) protocol standardization. He mentions that Boltz is currently working on adding submarine swaps to this standardization initiative.Overall, Thomas Voegtlin's proposal aims to address the need for bundled payments in lightning services that require prepayment of mining fees. The extension to BOLT-11 would level the competition between lightning service providers and provide more options for non-custodian exchanges. However, there are discussions about whether this change should be implemented in BOLT-11 or if it should be built on OMs for more privacy.
