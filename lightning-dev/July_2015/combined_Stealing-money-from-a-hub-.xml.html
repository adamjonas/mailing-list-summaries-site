Summary:

The conversation revolves around the implementation of the Lightning Network, and concerns are raised about identifying parties involved in a payment route involving Alice, Bob, and Carol. Additionally, there are concerns about out-of-band key exchange. Rusty Russell suggests that miners could act as third-party entities to check for cheaters in LN by offering them fees. However, this would require a new sighash op mode or normalized txids, making it difficult to deploy such a system currently.There is a need for a formal BIP, but it is considered premature at this point. The participants also discuss the timestop feature for CLTV, which raises concerns about potential DoS attacks on hubs if implemented incorrectly. Delaying timeouts is suggested as a soft fork to prevent HTLCs from expiring.Furthermore, outsourcing blockchain monitoring is discussed, with miners being suggested as a possible third party to check for cheaters in the Lightning Network. However, this idea faces challenges, including the lack of standardized data formats and consistency issues across different blockchain platforms. Overall, there is a need for further research and development before implementing these solutions.
