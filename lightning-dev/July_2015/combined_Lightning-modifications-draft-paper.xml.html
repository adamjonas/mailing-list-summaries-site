Summary:

Rusty Russell has proposed a construction with OP_CHECKLOCKTIMEVERIFY and OP_CHECKSEQUENCEVERIFY to expedite the deployment of lightning on Bitcoin. Joseph Poon praised the idea, stating it makes lightning more usable. However, BIP62 is still being developed as it takes time to implement the necessary changes for safety. BIP66 was rushed to close serious vulnerabilities, while rules 2-7 are non-standard but not applied to blocks. The deployment priority of BIP65's CHECKLOCKTIMEVERIFY is higher than that of BIP62, which is currently low in the soft-fork logjam. In response to Nick ODell's post, Mark Friedenbach stated that there is no deployment timeline for BIP62 due to its low priority. Peter Todd added that only one soft-fork upgrade signaling can be pushed out at a time, though they have the capability to push out more if needed. He also mentioned that miners cannot implement BIP65 without supporting BIP66. Rusty Russell clarified that he had not proofread or checked the script examples in his proposal but provided the link for people to review.
