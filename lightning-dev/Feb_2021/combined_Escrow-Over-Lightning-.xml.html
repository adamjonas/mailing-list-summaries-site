The first payment is from the seller to the buyer, claimable by the buyer and escrow key, while the second payment is from the buyer to the seller, claimable by the seller key. The seller hands over the item once both payments are made, and if the buyer is satisfied, they fail the seller-to-buyer payment after the seller claims the buyer-to-seller payment, and the seller is paid with no more obligations. If the buyer is dissatisfied, they can ask the escrow to judge, and the escrow will either reveal the escrow key to the buyer, who can then clawback the payment to the seller if the escrow judges in their favor, or delete the escrow private key, and the Seller-to-Buyer payment eventually times out, ending the obligation of the seller.The article discusses how Boolean logic can be converted into two standard forms, sum-of-products and product-of-sums, and proposes the use of reversal payments and "option not to release scalar" to express any Boolean logic.
