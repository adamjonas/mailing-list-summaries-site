Summary:

During an email conversation, Mark Friedenbach explained the use of sequence numbers in the Lightning Network. These numbers are utilized to enforce a minimum age for each input, resulting in relative lock-time through BIP 68. The value of the sequence number field can be checked in the script, with the value set to MAX_INT - 4320, allowing for a confirmation delay of 30 days for the parent.In bidirectional payment channels, the sequence number is increased each time payment direction changes. This results in the reduction of the confirmation delay. However, Jeremy Rubin raised concerns over race conditions and information leakage due to the use of sequence numbers. He suggested incrementing by a random amount to prevent external parties from measuring the number of LN transactions taking place.Mark Friedenbach clarified that the sequence number field was not being used as an increment-on-each-transaction sequence number but rather to achieve relative lock-time. He further elaborated on how this works and the ratcheting process in bidirectional payment channels.Jorge Timón also weighed in on the conversation, suggesting renaming nSequence to nMaturity to simplify documentation.
