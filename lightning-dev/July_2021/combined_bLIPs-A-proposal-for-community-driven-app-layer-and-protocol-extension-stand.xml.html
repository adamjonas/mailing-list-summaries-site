Summary:

The Lightning Network's author has suggested an idea to use Bitcoin Improvement Proposals (BIPs) for supporting new network additions, but raised concerns about how these new BIPs would be implemented without the basis of the Lightning technology. There has been a discussion on whether to merge layer 2 and layer 3 protocols such as BOLT, RGB, Volts, etc., into BIPs system. In response, Luke Dashjr suggests adding editors and revising the BIP process as needed and set aside the BIP 2xx range for Lightning years ago. Michael Folkson proposed adding a third BIP editor to cover Lightning related BIPs or subsuming BOLTs into BIPs. Nathanael compares BIPs to RFCs of the internet and questions whether lightning improvements are ultimately bitcoin improvements too. It makes sense to address all wishlist improvements at once.
