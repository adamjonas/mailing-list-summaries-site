Summary:

The Lightning Network community is actively discussing and exploring various approaches to mitigate channel jamming attacks. One of the proposed solutions is a reputation scheme, which involves tracking local reputation for each node's direct peers based on their history with all HTLCs forwarded to them. Reputation is slow to build and fast to degrade, making sudden changes in behavior short-lived. The goal is to establish reputational transitivity between incoming and outgoing traffic.To mitigate channel jamming, nodes will allocate slots and liquidity based on reputation and give priority to endorsed HTLCs. The community plans to gather data on the use of HTLC endorsement and local reputation tracking for further development and improvement of the Lightning Network. Different algorithms for tracking local reputation will be experimented with, and the data gathered will help inform future simulation work.However, there are concerns raised about the vulnerability of the endorsement scheme and the lack of transitivity in reputation acquisition cost between hops in a payment path. This can lead to potential vulnerabilities and whitewashing attacks. It is suggested that reputation revenue should be considered along with fees when determining the reliability of other nodes. The compensation mechanism in the chain of nodes with high reputation is unclear, and the HTLC endorsement mitigation suffers from the classic issues of reputation systems.There is also a discussion on the impact of dynamic reputation windows on payment reliability and the potential coupling effects with historical liquidity buckets in routing scoring algorithms. Sudden changes in link throughput based on HTLC resolution may affect routing algorithms. It is important to differentiate between routing fees and reputation revenue in the HTLC endorsement model and carefully evaluate the reliability of other nodes when accepting or refusing HTLC forwarding requests.The Lightning Network community is actively working towards mitigating channel jamming and experimenting with different schemes and reputation algorithms. Data gathering, simulation work, and experimentation with different algorithms for tracking local reputation are considered valuable for further development and improvement of the Lightning Network. The community invites further discussion and offers assistance with implementation details to address the challenges and vulnerabilities of the HTLC endorsement model.
