Summary:

The email thread revolves around the HTLC endorsement scheme in the Lightning Network and the challenges associated with it. One concern is the compensation mechanism, as it is unclear whether the fees paid fully compensate for the damage inflicted on the victim or if it is proportional compensation. The evaluation criteria for determining this ratio are also unspecified. Another issue is the lack of a formal definition of the problem to be solved and the absence of clarity in the proposal's goals. These factors raise concerns about the effectiveness of any jamming solution without rigorous analysis and measurable bounds.The conversation also discusses the impact of link-level liquidity fluctuation due to the HTLC endorsement system. While it acknowledges that endorsement can increase liquidity fluctuation, the effect on HTLC routing traffic downgrade is uncertain. The trade-offs involved in other proposed jamming solutions are also difficult to determine.There is a discussion about reputational transitivity and the need for a reputation scheme to prevent abuse during high-fee periods. The proposal is for each node to track the reputation of its direct neighbors and allocate slots and liquidity based on their risk tolerance. Reputation and endorsement form the first step in a two-part scheme for mitigating channel jamming.The conversation further explores the distinction between routing fees and reputation revenue in the HTLC endorsement model. It discusses a scenario involving two nodes, Caroll and Bob, sharing the same parameters, and the decision Bob has to make when receiving an HTLC forwarding request from Alice. The compensation mechanism in the chain of nodes with high reputation is unclear, and there are concerns about the lack of transitivity in reputation acquisition cost.Overall, the discussion emphasizes the importance of rigorous analysis, measurable bounds, and evaluation of trade-offs in addressing liquidity griefing issues in the Lightning Network. It also highlights the need for data gathering, experimentation, and collaboration within the Lightning Network community to develop effective mitigation strategies for reputation and channel jamming vulnerabilities.
