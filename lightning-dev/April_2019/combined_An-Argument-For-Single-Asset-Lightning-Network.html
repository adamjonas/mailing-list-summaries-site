Summary:

The Lightning Network is a payment system that uses hashlocked timelocked contracts (HTLCs) to transfer value between different cryptocurrency systems. The discussions on the Lightning-dev mailing list revolve around American Call Options, cross-asset brokers, option premiums, and trust issues. The use of HTLCs allows for routing payments and cross-system trades, which could potentially allow for seamless currency conversions. However, this use of HTLCs also allows for the creation of American Call Options without any premium, which are exploited by users seeking risk-free earnings.This creates an unintended consequence of significant liquidity being tied up in stalled forwarding payments with a risk of monetary loss during times of volatility. Proposed solutions such as adding an extra hashlock to the HTLC or modifying the collateral-based construction have been suggested, but they come with their own limitations and risks. Trustless systems create risks, but if the alternative trustless system is very impractical, and the risk is small enough, the benefits might be worth the risks.In another discussion, Rusty Russell argued that multi-asset Lightning Network (LN) may fail due to the problem of long-lived spam payments. Despite some vulnerabilities, ZmnSCPxj proposed a de-coupling solution that has the benefit of allowing trading parties to remain anonymous to RM while they hide from RM what second asset they are trading and to what exchange rate.ZmnSCPxj also discussed the potential solution of using collateralized HTLCs for cross-currency swaps on the Lightning Network. A protocol was also suggested for creating an American Call Option with a premium in the context of a BTC-WJT exchange. However, communication problems and trust issues were noted as potential challenges.Various proposals have been made to mitigate the issue of American Call Options, including the use of atomic information swaps and the addition of an extra hashlock to the HTLC. However, each proposed solution comes with its own limitations and risks. The persuasiveness of words should be independent of who speaks them, and if the alternative system is impractical and the risk is small enough, the benefits might outweigh those risks on an individual, voluntary basis.In conclusion, while the Lightning Network has potential for seamless cross-currency on-Lightning exchange, there are technical barriers that prevent its implementation. Solutions such as adding an extra hashlock to the HTLC or modifying the collateral-based construction have been proposed, but they come with their own limitations and risks. Trustless systems create risks, but if the alternative trustless system is very impractical, and the risk is small enough, the benefits might be worth the risks. However, note that this is a subjective trade-off, so it is only acceptable on an individual, voluntary basis.
