Summary:

The Lightning Network community is proposing a reputation-based mitigation strategy to address channel jamming attacks. Nodes will track local reputation for their direct peers based on their history with all HTLCs (Hashed Time-Locked Contracts) forwarded to them. Reputation will be slow and expensive to build, fast to degrade, and examined relative to recent routing activity.Nodes that forward fewer HTLCs will have difficulty building a good reputation with active routing nodes. However, this should not significantly impact their activity as they don't require much from their peers. Small and low-activity nodes may face competition for liquidity during an attack but can still interact with similar nodes to build a good reputation.To gather data on the use of HTLC endorsement and local reputation tracking, the Lightning Network community plans to observe the effect of endorsement in steady state, collect real-world data for simulation work, and experiment with different algorithms for tracking local reputation. Nodes will allocate slots and liquidity for endorsed HTLCs based on reputation and risk tolerance.The discussion on the Lightning-dev mailing list also explores the limitations of reputation systems in providing security guarantees. While reputation data can be valuable, sudden behavioral changes and whitewashing attacks make it unreliable for guarantees. Nonetheless, choosing a channel counterparty with a demonstrated track record of competence and reliability is preferable.The Lightning-dev mailing list, hosted by the Linux Foundation, serves as a platform for developers and enthusiasts to discuss and develop the Lightning Network.
