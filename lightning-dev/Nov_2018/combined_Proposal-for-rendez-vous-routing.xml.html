Summary:

The email exchange between CJP and ZmnSCPxj discusses the potential scenario where a powerful participant in the Lightning network starts enforcing KYC requirements on nodes. To combat this, ZmnSCPxj suggests that the rest of the network should punish this participant by closing any channel to it and doing retaliatory preemptive closing of any channel to any participant publicly connecting, directly or indirectly, to that participant. The discussion also touches on the desire for privacy and anonymity on the Lightning Network, with Rusty Russell proposing a "merchant forward" service where Alice can remain anonymous from the network by using Bob's forwarding service. However, CJP raises concerns about the proposal since Bob could risk being excluded from the side of the network with more harsh regulatory conditions if it is widely known that he provides this forwarding service. Rusty acknowledges these concerns and suggests rendezvous routing as a potential solution. Rendezvous routing may offer a better solution where payee chooses one or more routes from certain third-party nodes in the network to himself and passes sphinx-encrypted blobs for those routes to the payer. The discussion also highlights the need for private channels as a countermeasure against KYC enforcement and the risks associated with the existence of private channels being known to payers who need to cross the gap between compliant and non-compliant parts of the network, which risks penalties for the node owners on the compliant side. Overall, the conversation highlights the importance of maintaining a decentralized and inclusive network free from central authorities imposing arbitrary rules.
