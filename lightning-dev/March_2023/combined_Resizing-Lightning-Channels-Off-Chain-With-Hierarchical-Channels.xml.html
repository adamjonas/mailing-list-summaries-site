Summary:

The Lightning Network is a payment system that uses channels instead of nodes to route payments. The Lightning-dev mailing list discusses the complexities of routing to a "channel" rather than a node and proposes creating clear definitions of terms like "user", "node", "channel", and "party" to better understand how they work. Routing fees in the Lightning Network are negotiated by all users who own a given logical channel. Gossiping channel capacity is quite reasonable as long as you use channel_update_v2 messages and understand how logical channels work.Hierarchical channels are proposed, which allow a single utxo to contain multiple channels between different users that can be reorganized without going on-chain. This method can improve scalability, efficiency, and usability of the LN without changes to the underlying Bitcoin protocol. However, this involves complexity, and it may be harder in practice than in theory.Off-chain resizes can be achieved using hierarchical channels, which allow for the flexible resizing of channels without limitations on exchange within a limited pool of users. Moreover, liquidity multiplexing allows multiple parties to own a portion of a UTXO, ensuring that Alice and Bob can spend back and forth the entirety of their portions of the UTXO even if other participants are unavailable.
