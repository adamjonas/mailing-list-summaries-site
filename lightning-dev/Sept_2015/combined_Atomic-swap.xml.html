Summary:

The idea presented in the BitcoinTalk forum is to allow secure movement of funds in and out of an existing channel by using HTLC contracts. The proposal aims to fund balanced channels and provide a way for merchants to cash out their funds seamlessly. The swap in and swap out flows are detailed with rusty-style protobuf messages. In the regular swap in flow, Bob sends Alice the swap_in_offer and Alice accepts it. Bob publishes the swap tx and Alice ensures that the scriptpubkey pays her providing r. Alice then sends update_add_htlc with the same amount and r_hash to Bob, who accepts it. Bob reveals the r with update_fulfill_htlc, and Alice spends the swap tx. In the regular swap out flow, Bob sends Alice the swap_out_offer and Alice accepts it. Alice publishes the swap tx and Bob ensures that the scriptpubkey pays him providing r. Bob then sends update_add_htlc with the same amount and r_hash to Alice, who accepts it. Alice reveals the r with update_fulfill_htlc, and Bob spends the swap tx. The proposal requires adding another anchor to a channel since the atomic swap cannot increase channel capacity. The use of normal HTLCs is proposed, where the next hop is not a lightning node but a bitcoin address.
