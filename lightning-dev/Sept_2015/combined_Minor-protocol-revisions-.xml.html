In an email exchange, Pierre expressed difficulty understanding the rationale behind all existing closing flows and states related to SVG. Rusty chimed in, stating that despite the three cases (current commit, revoked commit, or mutual close) spending the same anchor, it is necessary to keep watching until any one of them is buried deep enough to prevent cheating of funds. The discussion included various updates and concerns regarding the Lightning Network protocol, including the addition of a second ACK stage for Close, HTLC rejection for bad routes or insufficient fees, and an upper bound of 0.04 BTC on each HTLC. The low ceiling raised concerns about targeting micropayments over scalability and speed. Rusty suggested setting expectations at "beer money, not rent money" to avoid innocent people getting caught up in hype.A discussion between Anthony Towns and Pierre focused on the updated version of SVG. Rusty discussed state coverage testing with Anthony Towns, acknowledging that testing isn't exhaustive and only serves to ensure that the state machine doesn't crash. Additionally, Rusty proposed increasing the precision of HTLC and a ceiling drop, with a ceiling of 0.04 satoshi (currently about $10 USD) on each HTLC due to 32-bit limitations. In a recent email conversation, Rusty shared updates on the Close and HTLC rejection scenarios in Lightning Network, including placing an upper bound of 0.04 BTC on each HTLC and refusing to create a channel that would overflow this limit.
