Summary:

The Lightning-dev mailing list is an online platform hosted by the Linux Foundation for discussions related to the Lightning Network. Recently, concerns were raised on the list about the lack of space available to store the entire blockchain on a laptop. Federico Tenga suggested pruning as a solution to this issue. Pruning involves validating the entire chain without storing it all on disk. However, Segue had a different idea. They proposed archiving old chapters of the blockchain in an evenly distributed manner across 150 computers on seven continents. The aim was to avoid adding to the increasingly long chain.Mahesh Govind suggested using a similar technique as Hyperledger to prune the chain based on consensus. Christian Decker recommended moving the discussion to either the bitcoin-dev or bitcoin-core-dev mailing lists. ZmnSCPxj urged Segue to create an implementation of their idea for bitcoind and share it on bitcoin-dev. They also suggested making a pull request on github.com/bitcoin/bitcoin. However, ZmnSCPxj noted that C-lightning does not currently work with a pruning bitcoind.The Lightning-dev mailing list plays an important role in enabling developers and enthusiasts to collaborate and drive innovation in the area of cryptocurrency technology.
