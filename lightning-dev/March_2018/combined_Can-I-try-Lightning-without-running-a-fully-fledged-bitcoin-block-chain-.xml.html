Summary:

In the Lightning-dev mailing list, Yubin Ruan expressed concern over not having enough space on their laptop to store the entire blockchain. Aleksej suggested running a pruning node instead which still requires validation of the entire chain but doesn't need to store all of it on the disk. The idea of archiving old chapters of the chain on a minimum of 150 computers across 7 continents was also proposed as a solution to storing the chain on individual devices. ZmnSCPxj proposed creating an implementation of an idea for bitcoind and sharing it on bitcoin-dev. Segue questioned why old chapters of the blockchain couldn't be periodically peeled off and stored in an evenly distributed manner across the planet. Yubin Ruan asked if they could try Lightning without running a fully-fledged Bitcoin block chain due to limited space on their laptop. Federico Tenga suggested pruning as an option. Mahesh Govind brought up the possibility of using a similar technique used in Hyperledger to prune the blockchain based on consensus. Christian pointed out that it is probably off-topic for the Lightning mailing list. Meanwhile, Segue suggested archiving chunks of blockchain on a minimum of 150 computers across 7 continents. The Lightning-dev mailing list is hosted by the Linux Foundation and is dedicated to discussing development related to the Lightning Network, a layer-two scaling solution for Bitcoin.
