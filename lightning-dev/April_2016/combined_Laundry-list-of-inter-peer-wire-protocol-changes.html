The email conversation between CJP and Rusty revolves around the development of the Lightning Network protocol by Rusty Russell. The discussion covers various topics such as cryptographic algorithms for securing the network, efficient ways to generate revocation secrets, renegotiation of anchor transactions, and the use of multi-signature transactions to enable escrow-style services.During the conversation, proposed solutions are debated among contributors, with Rusty Russell providing detailed explanations and implementations of some of these solutions. The lack of overlap between C-Lightning's core protocol and that of Amiko Pay is also mentioned, which implements a different subset of the functionality of the Lightning Network.They discuss the use of packet numbers for message confirmation and retransmission, as well as the implementation of two-way communication between payer and payee. They also talk about latency and the tradeoff between reduced latency and privacy concerns.Furthermore, the discussion revolves around various proposals and changes in the Lightning Network protocol, including allowing an arbitrarily deep tree with a seed, using multiple hashes for multi-sig transactions, enabling escrow-style services, and supporting 2-of-3 hashes. They also discuss the use of JSON as a wire format, with one member preferring other formats like protobufs or Capnproto. Other topics include length prefixes for packets, HTLC pipelining, and abort stages for HTLC negotiations.Lastly, they suggest using version bits to exchange compulsory and optional features during the initial handshake after key setup. In summary, the ongoing design and alternative concepts of certain subjects in the protocol are summarized, including micro-transaction channel design, commit conditions, and routing.The conversation starts with the discussion of channel implementation details and the lack of information on routing in C-Lightning protocol. Rusty Russell proposes changes and suggestions to nail down a "1.0" version of the inter-node protocol. The email also mentions the Amiko Pay protocol, which utilizes bi-directional routing and multiple channels per link, and allows for arbitrary-content receipts to be transmitted between payer and payee.
