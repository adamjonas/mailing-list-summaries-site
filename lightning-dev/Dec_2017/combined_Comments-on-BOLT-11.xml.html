Summary:

Developers are currently discussing improvements to BOLT#11, a Bitcoin payment protocol. One topic of discussion is the setting of a maximum length for invoices, as well as the use of descriptions within payment requests. Developers suggest that in certain cases, hiding information in the description may be necessary. Additionally, the use of descriptions is being discussed with regard to joining user data and payment hashes on the developers' end.During an email exchange, Rusty Russell points out that the htlc.me server violates a spec by not having a "description" entry field in their "Request Payment" form. Jonathan Underwood suggests adding an "extra data" tag, but Rusty states that it would be easy to do if he knew why they needed it. Underwood also discusses suggestions and comments for implementing BOLT11 in Node JS.The Lightning Network team is discussing the issue of description hashes being useless due to no good solution and lack of anonymity with URLs. They suggest possible solutions such as container formats like HTML, HORNET implementation, or JSON encoding. The team also talks about using bech32 for checksums and changing test vectors into machine-friendly JSON format. The need to hide certain information in descriptions is emphasized and an "extra data" tag is suggested.ZmnSCPxj and Rusty discuss the support of full Unicode in Lightning Network. They agree that UTF8 should be supported but caution that the implementation needs to be precise, specifically mentioning which variant of UTF8. They discuss the use of Modified UTF8 or simply disallowing null characters. The issue of Unicode normalization is also brought up, where multiple different byte-sequences in UTF8 may lead to the same sequence of human-readable glyphs.Lastly, the given context includes encrypted messages, followed by an email address and a PGP public key. The messages are likely encrypted using PGP encryption, and the email address belongs to a person associated with Bloomberg Chai Kadhai. It is unclear what information the encrypted messages contain or what they are intended for.
