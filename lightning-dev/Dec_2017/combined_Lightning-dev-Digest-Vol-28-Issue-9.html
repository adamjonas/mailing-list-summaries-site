The Lightning-dev mailing list is a forum managed by the Linux Foundation for developers working on the Lightning Network, which is a layer 2 protocol built on top of Bitcoin to enable faster and cheaper transactions. The list provides a platform for discussion and coordination among developers, and anyone can subscribe using the link provided. In a recent email exchange on the Lightning-dev mailing list, Stan Kladko asked about finding a peer for his lightning connections and how to make them do deposits. ZmnSCPxj replied that selection criteria can be set, and lnd offers an "auto-pilot" feature to select peers automatically. In terms of deposit, channels are initially single-funded, and there is a proposal for dual-funded channel setup. ZmnSCPxj also explained that by opening channels with new and low-connectivity nodes, one can become a hub and earn routing fees. Additionally, if someone wants to connect to your node, they will send channel funding messages. Rusty clarified that having two channels means people routing through you does not affect your total capacity; it just shifts them between channels.Another idea proposed in the email thread was the creation of a marketplace of middlemen who would pay out funds to the receiver immediately and get their money back from the original sender after waiting 10+ minutes for confirmation. However, ZmnSCPxj suggested that users could simply run their own LN nodes and make payments for low fees without having to use a middleman service.
