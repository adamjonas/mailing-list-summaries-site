Summary:

On March 8, 2016, Martijn Meijering proposed a new opcode for signature verification in Bitcoin's scripting system. The proposed opcode would verify appropriately length-restricted messages and could simplify the Lightning Network protocol. Users could sign a message that revokes a previous balance with a hash and sequence number instead of revealing hash preimages and maintaining mirror images of transactions. This proposal was discussed on the Lightning-dev mailing list. Meijering asked if he was overlooking anything and suggested that the new opcode could substantially simplify the LN protocol.
