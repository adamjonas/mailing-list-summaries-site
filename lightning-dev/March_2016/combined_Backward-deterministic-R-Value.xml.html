Summary:

The email conversation between Mats Jerratsch and Nicolas Dorier discusses the use of R values in bipayment channels without HTLCs (Hashed Time-Locked Contracts) in Lightning. They clarify that commitments can't be made before accepting previous ones, and propose a new method for deterministic RValue generation to save off-chain one signature per commitment. Rusty Russell and Nicolas Dorier discuss the idea of one-way deterministic RValue generation and shachain, which avoids generating several million hashes in advance. They also mention Adam Back's contribution to the discussion on the Lightning-dev mailing list in May 2015. The concern is raised about the security implications of disclosing R values when the payment isn't in the current commitment. CJP asks how deriving R values from a tree structure works for larger Lightning networks, and Nicolas Dorier responds with the concept of shachain.
