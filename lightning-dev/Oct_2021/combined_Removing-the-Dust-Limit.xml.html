Summary:

Antoine Riard raised concerns about the trim-to-dust evaluation not accurately matching the lifetime of the HTLC in a recent email exchange on the bitcoin-dev mailing list. Rusty Russell responded that the evaluation should be determined each time the channel is updated and that dust limits only provide a fixed size lower limit. He also addressed the issue of uneconomic outputs, explaining that it becomes irrational before it becomes uneconomic. Pieter Wuille joined the conversation and agreed with David Harding's statement that UTXO set size is an externality that Bitcoin's consensus rules fail to account for.Wuille believes that having a relay policy that avoids economically irrational behavior makes perfect sense, but it's not obvious how consensus rules could deal with this without hardcoded prices/feerates. A proposal to remove the dust limit from Bitcoin has been made, but Matt Corallo argues that it's important to consider increasing the dust limit instead. The current dust limit is based on a feerate of only 3 sat/vByte, which is incredibly low. As feerates have gone up over time, and are expected to continue to rise, drastically increasing the 3 sat/vByte basis to something more like 20 sat/vB should be considered. The removal of the dust limit was suggested because it's not Bitcoin's business to dictate what outputs people want to create, but Corallo argues that the costs are born by miners and full node operators, so it is precisely Bitcoin's business. Dust outputs can be used in various authentication/delegation smart contracts, but Corallo suggests that low-value-but-enough-to-be-worth-spending-when-you're-done-with-them outputs could be used instead.Thinly divisible colored coin protocols might make use of sats as value markers for transactions, but Corallo believes that these schemes can and should use values which make them economical to spend. Lastly, the significant costs to every miner and full node operator are a reason to keep the dust limit in place. Russell also notes that if someone is trying to save every satoshi, dust limits aren't their problem, and if they're not trying to save every satoshi, they can simply add 305 sats to their output to avoid the dust limit.
