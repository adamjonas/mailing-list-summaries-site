Summary:

In a mailing list discussion, Christian Decker and Fabrice Drouin discuss the importance of routing data. They talk about how flapping can provide useful information to nodes like mobile clients who do not require all the bandwidth to stay fully in sync. Flapping also indicates an unreliable channel that is not suitable for routing. However, Christian is unconvinced that we should optimize for minimum bandwidth use. Fabrice highlights an example where not replacing updates results in nodes not knowing that a channel has been re-enabled. He asks how one would inform their peer if they discard the update and keep only the first one. Christian agrees with this point and the discussion moves on to optimizing the network structure. They suggest heuristics to reduce gossip traffic and introduce local policies at senders of the update to minimize flapping updates.To improve the network's resiliency and redundancy, they recommend implementing "eager-disable" and "lazy-enable" or vice versa, depending on the network structure. They hope that these efforts will lead to better routing performance over time.
