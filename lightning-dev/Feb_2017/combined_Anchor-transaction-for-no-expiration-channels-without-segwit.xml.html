Summary:

The email conversation between Christopher Jämthagen, Nicolas Dorier, and Tier Nolan revolves around creating anchor transactions for indefinite channel lifetimes without Segwit. The proposed solution involves using nTimelock after the bounty's expiration, which would keep the transaction smaller. The locktime was initially created to give Bob time to reclaim the bounty before Alice can broadcast TX2. A symmetrically funded channel requires four 1BTC outputs, making it essentially two single-funded channels.Dorier suggests having a way to cooperatively reclaim the deposit as a good idea. The proposed time-locked transaction system aims to provide security and efficiency. If Alice notices Bob not signing it, she can quickly reclaim the bounty. However, if Bob signs it, Alice cannot immediately broadcast it. Nicolas Dorier suggests that a second timeout is unnecessary. Bob would only sign TX2 when he has the second output of TX1. But Alice must have a fully-signed TX2 version before the timeout ends. Without it, Bob can reclaim TX1/1 once the timeout expires and refuse to sign TX2. Alice needs a signed TX2 to broadcast it and close the channel, claiming TX1/0. Once accepted, Alice can claim TX1/1. The discussion also mentions Alice and Bob opening a channel with 1BTC each.
