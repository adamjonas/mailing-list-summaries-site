Summary:

The Lightning Network (LN) and its feasibility without the activation of SegWit has been a topic of discussion on various platforms. The Lightning paper outlines three levels, with level 1 using CLTV only, while level 2 uses CLTV and CSV, and level 3 adding segwit, making malleability irrelevant. Outsourcing monitoring of the blockchain to a server is not currently feasible due to the inability to hand over penalty transaction signatures without knowing what the bad transaction will look like. Solutions involving Spillman-style payment channels or OP_CLTV/OP_CSV-style channels have been proposed but are not yet implemented in the spec. It has been suggested that variations of level 2 may exist. Rusty Russell states that if SegWit does not activate, something is seriously wrong with Bitcoin. Despite concerns from miners about losing their fee-gathering monopoly for moving money to layer2-actors, adoption will take time to roll out across products anyway. Stefano Pepe, CEO of UniquID Inc., has expressed interest in helping with the evolution of LN and Segwit.
