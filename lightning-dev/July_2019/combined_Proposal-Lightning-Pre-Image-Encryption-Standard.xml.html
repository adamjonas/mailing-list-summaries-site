Summary:

Alexander and ZmnSCPxj discuss the benefits of placing a MAC inside the encryption to ensure data replacement over an insecure channel can be detected. The use of MAC is said to be useful in preventing client DoS attacks on servers. The conversation then shifts to a proposal made by Nadav for a monetized REST API scheme that aims to simplify the client-server interaction, reduce round trips, and ensure payment to the merchant. However, there are concerns about the lack of integrity guarantees for the encrypted data and the potential risk of a client DoS attack if encryption occurs before payment is received.To address these concerns, Alex proposes two different lightning payment approaches to minimize risks for DoS attacks: purchasing an auth token upfront or paying per request. ZmnSCPxj highlights the possibility of a DoS attack when larger data payloads are involved under Nadav's proposed scheme. He also suggests encrypting data after proof-of-payment is shown to prevent clients from requesting and receiving data immediately without paying. The group discusses various strategies to mitigate potential risks associated with using lightning payments for REST APIs. A higher-level abstraction could possibly be standardized to handle more mainstream use-cases.
