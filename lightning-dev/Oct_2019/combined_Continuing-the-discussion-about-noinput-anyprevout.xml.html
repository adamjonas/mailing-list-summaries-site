Summary:

On the bitcoin-dev mailing list, there was a proposal to create a new opcode called `OP_CHECKSIG_WITHOUT_INPUT` instead of using `SIGHASH_NOINPUT`. The proposed new opcode would ignore any `SIGHASH` flags present on a signature. A renewed interest in eltoo and clean abstractions for off-chain protocols has led Christian Decker to revisit the "sighash_noinput" proposal (BIP-118) and AJ's "bip-anyprevout" proposal. There are open questions that remain to be addressed, such as general agreement on the usefulness of noinput/ anyprevoutanyscript/ anyprevout, strong support or opposition to chaperone signatures, and the same for output tagging/ explicit opt-in. It is suggested to have a public testnet with fake exchanges/merchants and scheduled reorgs to demo every weird noinput/anyprevout case anyone can think of to work out if any extra code/tagging/whatever is needed to keep those fake exchanges/merchants from losing money. A proposal has been made on the Lightning-dev mailing list to remove SIGHASH from signatures and put it on public keys. This proposal would solve issues with SIGHASH_NONE and SIGHASH_SINGLE. Problems with NONE and SINGLE are no worse than using SIGHASH_ALL to pay to "1*G". NOINPUT/ANYPREVOUT is a greater concern and committing to enabling NOINPUT for an address may make sense. Additionally, graftroot allows updating of conditions for address "A" after the fact.The conversation between Christian Decker and AJ discusses the use of opt-in via pubkey prefix as a means to avoid losing funds while handling additional coins sent to the same address. The scheme is extendable to opt into all sighash flags, which would allow the pubkey prefix to act as a mask for the sighash flags, and fail verification if they do not match. The conversation also highlights the need for an effective demonstration of the theoretical attacks people have dreamt up in a laboratory setting, to ensure that every possible corner case is handled.
