The author of the post is curious about what it means to "fail a channel" in BOLT #2. The author asks whether failing the channel means closing the channel unilaterally or mutually, or ending the TCP connection, or something else entirely.In response to Benjamin Weintraub's question, a Lightning Developer explains the concept of failing a channel in BOLT 5. Failing a channel means closing it, and peers should first attempt to mutually close the channel before resorting to a unilateral force close, which could result in higher fees and pending HTLCs. Discussions are ongoing around the semantics of failing a channel and what actions should be taken, with a recent pull request resulting from discussions around warning messages and issues arising during `channel_reestablish.` There is also talk of introducing warning messages to relax situations where errors are sent, potentially preventing channels from being closed as quickly. The section regarding failing a channel can be found on GitHub and outlines what should happen when a peer fails a channel, typically due to the sending of an error message.
