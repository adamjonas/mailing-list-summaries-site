Summary:

The email exchanges among Lightning Network developers focus on the dynamic nature of channel parameters and potential improvements to the network's routing scheme. Bastien proposes raising `htlc_minimum_msat` on big channels without closing them, but this could cause issues with MPP-split. The use of big payments as an aggregation method is suggested as a solution. The onions-per-channel model is questioned, and the idea of using HTLC packets that openly show the next destination node is suggested. Adding tlv records in `commitment_signed` to inform channel peers about changing values for certain fields is proposed to make channel parameter updates more transparent.Antoine suggests using a web-of-trust/reputation system based on counterparty reputation or an upfront payment system to mitigate channel jamming rather than relying solely on straighter channel parameters. Hybrid models are also suggested where good behaving peers can be rewarded with discounts on upfront payment policies. However, these proposals present complications such as off-chain liquidity and on-chain fees trade-offs and risks centralization around long-lived nodes.Despite some disagreements, both parties agree that negotiating policies before opening channels can help avoid overstaking liquidity more than what can be gained from it. They also suggest gradually increasing a peer's access to resources based on successful payments and decreasing it if there are long-lived HTLCs or excessive failures. This mechanism would give forwarding nodes more control over how their allocated bandwidth is used by all connected peers and mitigate several classes of attacks.
