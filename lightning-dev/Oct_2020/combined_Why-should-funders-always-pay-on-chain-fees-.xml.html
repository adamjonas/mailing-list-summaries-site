Summary:

A suggestion has been made by Bastien Teinturier in a Lightning-dev mailing list regarding the rule of "funder pays all the commit tx fees". He believes that this rule exists solely for simplicity, which may lead to a web-of-trust network where channels would only exist between peers that trust each other. Bastien proposes two solutions to distribute the worst-case cost of needing to go to the chain with a "fully loaded" commitment transaction more fairly. His first proposal involves deducting the HTLC cost from the offerer's main output. The second proposal ties the total commit-tx fee to the channel usage and uses the on-chain fee as collateral for channel usage. This forces the fundee to care about on-chain feerates, creating a feedback loop between on-chain feerates and routing fees, which is beneficial in the long run. By implementing these proposals, it will be easier to establish channels between peers that do not necessarily trust each other. The current system may lead to a web-of-trust network that can limit the potential growth of the Lightning Network. Therefore, Bastien's proposals aim to make the distribution of on-chain fees more fair and provide a better long-term solution for the network.
