Summary:

The Lightning Network has been discussing solutions to prevent opportunistic attackers from delaying payments. One proposed solution is a reputation system for nodes, which would keep all links high-quality and quickly disconnect from delaying nodes if the incentives are right. However, concerns were raised about the potential risks associated with this solution, such as the potential for de-anonymization and less incentive for nodes to forward payments if their reputation is at risk.Other proposals include randomizing the reputation-loss-rate, implementing CLTV-delay randomization for payments, and aggregating losses along the route. The discussion also delves into proving channel closure and attacks on lightning routing nodes, including the Loop Attack, which locks up funds in every channel on the route. Possible solutions to this attack include protocol support for decrypting the onion route, requiring fees even if the payment fails, and implementing a reputation system for nodes.The trade-off between network efficiency and privacy needs to be carefully evaluated. There are concerns about the leakage of information due to the aggregation of losses along the route, but it is argued that this actually serves to prevent large nodes from ruining links elsewhere. In relation to reputation and `reputation_loss_rate` in HTLC, ZmnSCPxj explained that the reputation score is private per-node while `reputation_loss_rate` is explicit in the HTLC.Possible risks associated with the proposed reputation system for Lightning Network were discussed. Concerns were raised regarding deanonymizing the network and creating new vectors of attack. The need for a real design and careful evaluation was emphasized. Mallory can destroy N reputation points in 18 nodes for cost N in this scheme by sending a payment to Mallory2, which takes a day to resolve, after paying a crapload of fees to Node18 to pay Mallory3, getting great reputation. This is why ramped reputation_loss_rate is necessary. Exponential reputation_loss_rate can fix the issue, but Node1 may never get the 262144x reputation needed to make the payment in the first place.In conclusion, the context provides an in-depth analysis of the proposed reputation system for Lightning Network, highlighting its potential benefits and drawbacks. The discussion also delves into issues such as information leakage and misaligned incentives, emphasizing the need for careful evaluation and consideration of all trade-offs involved in implementing such a system.
