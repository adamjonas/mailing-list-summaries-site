    Summary:

    In an email exchange, Gary Rowe suggests changing the reserved general delimiter ":" in a URL scheme to one of four unreserved characters that do not need to be URL encoded, as recommended by RFC 3986. The options are underscore, hyphen, tilde, and period, with Rowe preferring hyphen. Andreas Schildbach questions whether the colon sign is allowed in URL parameter names and suggests using an unrestricted character instead, such as an underscore. Matt Corallo acknowledges the usefulness of sources other than the RFC itself, and agrees to change it to a hyphen.
